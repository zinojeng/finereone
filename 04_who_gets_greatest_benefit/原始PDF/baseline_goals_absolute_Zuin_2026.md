## Abstract

### Aims
Finerenone, a selective nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist, has been
shown to reduce cardiovascular (CV) events in patients with Type 2 diabetes mellitus and
chronic kidney disease. We aimed to quantify the robustness of its CV benefits in
randomized controlled trials using fragility metrics coupled with conventional clinical
effect measures.

### Methods
We systematically searched MEDLINE and Scopus (from inception to June 2025) for Phase 3/4
placebo-controlled randomized trials of finerenone in Type 2 diabetes mellitus and chronic
kidney disease reporting dichotomous CV outcomes. Three studies were included: FIDELIO-DKD,
FIGARO-DKD, and the pooled FIDELITY analysis. We extracted hazard ratios (HRs), absolute
risk reduction, relative risk reduction, number needed to treat (NNT), fragility index (FI),
reverse FI, fragility quotient (FQ), and reverse FQ for the primary composite CV outcome
(CV death, non-fatal myocardial infarction, non-fatal stroke, heart failure hospitalization)
and individual components.

### Results
The primary composite outcome was significantly reduced in FIDELIO-DKD (HR 0.86, NNT 56;
FI 4, FQ 0.0007) and FIDELITY (HR 0.86, NNT 59; FI 38, FQ 0.002), but not FIGARO-DKD
(HR 0.87; reverse FI 7, reverse FQ 0.0009). Among individual outcomes, heart failure
hospitalization showed the most consistent and robust benefit (FIDELITY: HR 0.78, NNT 91;
FI 23, FQ 0.001). Effects on CV death, myocardial infarction, and stroke were numerically
favourable but statistically non-significant, with low reverse fragility indices.

### Conclusions
Finerenone significantly reduces heart failure hospitalization and modestly improves CV
outcomes in Type 2 diabetes mellitus with chronic kidney disease, but effects on CV death,
myocardial infarction, and stroke are fragile. Combining fragility metrics with standard
efficacy measures offers a clearer view of the reliability of trial results.
