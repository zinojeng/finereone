# Outcomes With Finerenone by Baseline Treatment Goals in Type 2 Diabetes and Chronic Kidney Disease: A FIDELITY Analysis

**Journal:** Diabetes, Obesity and Metabolism, 2026; 28(7):6022-6033
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/dom.70750 | **PMID:** 42027097
**Article type:** Original Article (Open Access, CC BY-NC)
**Received:** 23 Dec 2025 | **Revised:** 30 Mar 2026 | **Accepted:** 31 Mar 2026

## Abstract

**Aims:** The American Diabetes Association (ADA) recommends achieving treatment goals (glycated haemoglobin ≤53 mmol/mol [7.0%], blood pressure <130/80 mmHg, LDL cholesterol <1.81 mmol/L, and use of SGLT2 inhibitors or GLP-1 receptor agonists) to reduce cardiovascular (CV) and kidney complications in people with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes (T2D). This exploratory study assessed whether treatment effects of finerenone versus placebo were modified by the number of treatment goals met at baseline.

**Materials and Methods:** FIDELITY, a prespecified pooled analysis, combined participant-level data from the randomized, double-blind, multicentre phase III FIDELIO-DKD (NCT02540993) and FIGARO-DKD (NCT02545049) trials. Participants with T2D and CKD were randomized 1:1 to finerenone or placebo. Composite CV and kidney outcomes, hospitalization for heart failure (HHF) or CV death, HHF, and treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) were assessed by subgroups based on the number of treatment goals achieved.

**Results:** At baseline, 29%, 40%, 24% and 7% of participants had met 0, 1, 2 and ≥3 treatment goals, respectively. Those with more treatment goals met had fewer composite CV outcomes (events per 100 patient-years in the placebo group: 6.0, 5.1, 4.3 and 3.5 for 0, 1, 2 and ≥3 treatment goals, respectively). Similar patterns were observed for other outcomes. There was no heterogeneity in the effect of finerenone versus placebo on outcomes between subgroups. The safety profile of finerenone (vs. placebo) was similar across subgroups.

**Conclusions:** Finerenone demonstrated treatment benefits in people with CKD and T2D, irrespective of treatment goals achieved at baseline.

**Keywords:** cardiovascular complications | chronic kidney disease | finerenone | type 2 diabetes

## 1 | Introduction

- CKD develops in 30%-40% of people with T2D. People with both T2D and CKD are at high risk of progressing to end-stage kidney disease and experiencing major adverse CV events and CV mortality.
- Finerenone, a selective nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA), reduces the risk of both CV and kidney complications in individuals with T2D and CKD.
- In FIDELITY, finerenone reduced the risk of the composite kidney outcome by 23% and the composite CV outcome by 14% compared with placebo.
- The ADA Standards of Care recommends a multifactorial approach with four main pillars: (1) glycaemic management; (2) blood pressure management; (3) lipid management; and (4) use of therapeutic agents with CV and kidney benefits (SGLT2is or GLP-1RAs).
- This subanalysis evaluated finerenone versus placebo according to attainment of ADA-recommended treatment goals at baseline.

## 2 | Materials and Methods

### 2.1 | Study Design and Participants
- Prespecified FIDELITY analysis pooled individual participant-level data from FIDELIO-DKD (NCT02540993) and FIGARO-DKD (NCT02545049) phase III trials.
- Adults aged ≥18 years with T2D and CKD (UACR 30 to <300 mg/g and eGFR 25 to ≤90 mL/min/1.73 m², or UACR 300 to ≤5000 mg/g and eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m²), serum potassium ≤4.8 mmol/L at screening and run-in, no symptomatic chronic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction.
- Randomized 1:1 to oral finerenone (10 or 20 mg once daily) or placebo, on top of maximum tolerated labelled dose of renin-angiotensin system (RAS) blockade.

### 2.2 | Definition of Treatment Goals
Treatment goals defined per ADA Standards of Care:
1. Glycated haemoglobin ≤53 mmol/mol (7.0%)
2. Blood pressure <130/80 mmHg
3. LDL cholesterol <1.81 mmol/L
4. Use of SGLT2is or GLP-1RAs

Participants divided into subgroups meeting 0, 1, 2 and ≥3 treatment goals.

### 2.4 | Outcomes
- **Composite CV outcome:** time to CV death, nonfatal myocardial infarction, nonfatal stroke or HHF.
- **CV death or HHF**; **HHF**.
- **Composite kidney outcome:** time to kidney failure (kidney dialysis for ≥90 days, kidney transplantation, or sustained decrease in eGFR to <15 mL/min/1.73 m²), sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease from baseline over ≥4 weeks, or kidney-related death.
- Additional: all-cause mortality, changes in UACR and eGFR to month 48.
- Sensitivity analysis excluding use of SGLT2is or GLP-1RAs as a goal.
- Safety: number of investigator-reported TEAEs.

### 2.5 | Statistical Analysis
- Stratified multivariable Cox proportional hazards models with treatment-by-subgroup interaction term. Adjusted for age, sex, and systolic blood pressure at baseline.
- UACR over time via mixed-effect model for repeated measures; eGFR slopes via 2-slope linear spline mixed model.
- 2-sided p<0.05 significant for main effects; interaction tests <0.05 (less statistical power). SAS version 9.4.

## 3 | Results

### 3.1 | Participants and Baseline Characteristics
- Total **12 990 participants**. At baseline: **29% (n=3732), 40% (n=5208), 24% (n=3091) and 7% (n=959)** met 0, 1, 2 and ≥3 goals, respectively.
- Mean age and proportion of male participants increased with number of goals met.
- Larger proportions met higher number of goals in Asia and Western Europe vs other regions; smaller proportions in Eastern Europe and Latin America.
- History of CV disease more common in those who met more goals; median UACR decreased as number of goals met increased.
- Statins, SGLT2is and GLP-1RAs receipt increased with number of goals met; insulin and analogues decreased.
- In participants achieving one goal, most frequent goal achieved was HbA1c ≤53 mmol/mol (7.0%), followed by LDL <1.81 mmol/L, SBP <130 mmHg and DBP <80 mmHg; lowest proportion had goal use of SGLT2i or GLP-1RA.

### 3.2 | Effect of Treatment Goal Subgroup on CV and Kidney Outcome Incidence Rates (Placebo Arm)
In the placebo arm, participants who met a higher number of treatment goals at baseline had lower risk of outcome events (events per 100 patient-years [PY] for 0 / 1 / 2 / ≥3 goals):

| Outcome (placebo arm) | 0 goals | 1 goal | 2 goals | ≥3 goals |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Composite CV outcome | 6.0 | 5.1 | 4.3 | 3.5 |
| CV death or HHF | 3.9 | 3.3 | 2.9 | 2.2 |
| HHF | 1.9 | 1.6 | 1.7 | 1.4 |
| Composite kidney outcome | 3.1 | 2.7 | 2.0 | 1.4 |

A similar pattern was observed for participants randomized to finerenone (Figure 2).

### 3.3 | Effect of Finerenone on CV Outcomes by Treatment Goal Subgroups
- **Composite CV outcome overall:** HR 0.86 (95% CI 0.78-0.95); finerenone 823/6498 (4.34/100 PY) vs placebo 938/6492 (5.02/100 PY). Overall relative risk reduction 14%.
- By number of goals met (composite CV outcome HR [95% CI]):
  - 0 goals: **0.90 (0.76-1.05)** — finerenone 295/1905 (5.52) vs placebo 307/1827 (6.01)
  - 1 goal: **0.79 (0.68-0.92)** — finerenone 299/2534 (4.04) vs placebo 388/2674 (5.05)
  - 2 goals: **0.88 (0.72-1.08)** — finerenone 182/1568 (3.85) vs placebo 194/1523 (4.30)
  - ≥3 goals: **0.89 (0.59-1.37)** — finerenone 47/491 (3.19) vs placebo 49/468 (3.51)
  - **p_interaction = 0.7481** (no significant heterogeneity)
- **CV death or HHF overall:** HR 0.83 (0.74-0.93); finerenone 537/6498 (2.76) vs placebo 640/6492 (3.32).
  - 0 goals: 0.84 (0.69-1.03); 1 goal: 0.77 (0.64-0.92); 2 goals: 0.88 (0.69-1.13); ≥3 goals: 0.75 (0.44-1.29). **p_interaction = 0.8891**
- **HHF overall:** HR 0.78 (0.66-0.92); finerenone 255/6498 (1.31) vs placebo 325/6492 (1.69).
  - 0 goals: 0.73 (0.54-0.99); 1 goal: 0.84 (0.65-1.09); 2 goals: 0.74 (0.53-1.04); ≥3 goals: 0.49 (0.23-1.03). **p_interaction = 0.7288**
- Sensitivity analysis excluding SGLT2i/GLP-1RA as a goal: overall risk reduction 14%, p_interaction = 0.85.

### 3.4 | Effect of Finerenone on Kidney Outcomes by Treatment Goal Subgroups
- **Composite kidney outcome overall:** HR 0.76 (0.66-0.88); finerenone 356/6498 (1.95) vs placebo 465/6492 (2.56). Overall relative risk reduction 24%.
- By goals met:
  - 0 goals: **0.77 (0.60-0.97)** — finerenone 128/1905 (2.47) vs placebo 155/1827 (3.11)
  - 1 goal: **0.71 (0.57-0.88)** — finerenone 144/2534 (2.03) vs placebo 202/2674 (2.72)
  - 2 goals: **0.70 (0.50-0.97)** — finerenone 63/1568 (1.38) vs placebo 89/1523 (2.03)
  - ≥3 goals: **1.11 (0.56-2.18)** — finerenone 21/491 (1.47) vs placebo 19/468 (1.41)
  - **p_interaction = 0.6095**
- Note: relative risk increase of 11% (1.11; 0.56-2.18) in ≥3 goals subgroup, but with wide CIs due to fewer events. No significant heterogeneity when absolute rather than relative risk reductions considered (p_interaction = 0.13).
- Sensitivity analysis excluding SGLT2i/GLP-1RA as a goal: overall risk reduction 24%, p_interaction = 0.40.

### 3.5 | Effect of Finerenone on All-Cause Mortality
- All-cause mortality low: 2.5 to 3.5 events/100 PY with placebo; 2.3 to 3.1 with finerenone. Highest number of events in those with no goals achieved.
- Overall finerenone reduced relative risk of all-cause mortality: **HR 0.89 (95% CI 0.79-1.00)**; no significant heterogeneity between groups (**p_interaction = 0.0659**).

### 3.6 | Effect on UACR and eGFR Changes Over Time by Subgroups
- Finerenone significantly reduced UACR from baseline to month 4 across all subgroups (LS mean treatment ratios): 0.70 (0.66-0.74) for 0 goals; 0.68 (0.65-0.71) for 1 goal; 0.68 (0.64-0.71) for 2 goals; 0.62 (0.56-0.68) for ≥3 goals. UACR reduction sustained throughout trial regardless of subgroup.
- Finerenone attenuated eGFR decline vs placebo across subgroups. Change in total eGFR slope baseline to month 48:
  - 0 goals: -3.80 finerenone vs -4.35 placebo (LS mean difference 0.56 [0.23-0.88])
  - 1 goal: -3.14 vs -3.81 (LS mean difference 0.67 [0.42-0.92])
  - 2 goals: -2.86 vs -3.15 (LS mean difference 0.29 [0.01-0.57])
  - ≥3 goals: -2.43 vs -2.71 (LS mean difference 0.28 [-0.21 to 0.78])

### 3.7 | Change in Attainment of Treatment Goals Over Time
- Finerenone group: proportion achieving ≥3 treatment goals rose from 7.6% at baseline to 10.0% at 3 years. Placebo group: 7.2% to 8.7%. Other changes minor.

### 3.8 | Safety Outcomes (Table 2)
- Number of TEAEs generally similar between finerenone and placebo groups, and across the four goal-attainment subgroups.
- Acute kidney injury occurred in <5% of participants: 3.0%, 3.4%, 4.1% and 2.9% in the 0, 1, 2 and ≥3 goal subgroups (finerenone); 3.3%, 3.3%, 4.3% and 4.5% (placebo).
- Investigator-reported hyperkalaemia more common with finerenone: **12.3%-15.2% with finerenone vs 6.5%-9.0% with placebo** across the four subgroups.
- Serious hyperkalaemia leading to hospitalization or permanent discontinuation rare (≤1.2% finerenone; ≤0.2% placebo across all four subgroups). No hyperkalaemia-related deaths reported.

## 4 | Discussion — Key Points
- Subgroups who met more ADA-recommended treatment goals at baseline had lower risk of adverse CV outcomes, HHF, and adverse kidney outcomes, irrespective of finerenone or placebo treatment. eGFR decline was also slower in those meeting more goals.
- Results consistent with STENO-2 (multifactorial intensive intervention over 7.8 years associated with 51% reduced risk of adverse CV events, 33%-48% reduced microvascular complications, 45% reduced all-cause mortality over 21 years follow-up).
- Consistent with CKD-REIN cohort: 30% lower risk of CV and kidney events over 5 years in those reaching two treatment targets vs fewer than two.
- Hyperkalaemia rates lower when finerenone and SGLT2is used together (CONFIDENCE trial: 9.3% for finerenone-empagliflozin combination vs 11.4% finerenone alone).
- **Conclusion:** This exploratory analysis suggests participants who met more treatment goals at baseline generally had lower risk of CV and kidney events (likely because disease was well controlled). Finerenone reduced CV and kidney outcomes vs placebo, and this effect was observed in all participants regardless of number of treatment goals met at baseline. Finerenone should be used regardless of number of treatment goals met — treatment should not be delayed until risk factors controlled; even those achieving all classic treatment goals benefit.

### Limitations
- FIDELITY was itself a prespecified pooled analysis of two trials; current analysis not prespecified and must be interpreted as exploratory.
- Randomization not stratified by number of treatment goals met at baseline.
- Only 7.4% of participants met ≥3 treatment goals at baseline (n=959), with low number receiving SGLT2is/GLP-1RAs likely contributing; fewer events in this subgroup led to wider CIs.
- Treatment goal attainment based on baseline measurements only; did not account for changes during trial.
