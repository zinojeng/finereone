# Effect of Finerenone on Albuminuria in Patients With Diabetic Nephropathy: A Randomized Clinical Trial (ARTS-DN)

**Bakris GL, Agarwal R, Chan JC, Cooper ME, Gansevoort RT, Haller H, Remuzzi G, Rossing P, Schmieder RE, Nowack C, Kolkhof P, Joseph A, Pieper A, Kimmeskamp-Kirschbaum N, Ruilope LM; ARTS-DN Study Group.**

ClinicalTrials.gov: NCT01874431.

## Importance
Steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists, when added to a renin-angiotensin system blocker, further reduce proteinuria in patients with chronic kidney disease but may be underused because of a high risk of adverse events.

## Objective
To evaluate the safety and efficacy of oral doses of the nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist finerenone in patients with diabetic nephropathy (high albuminuria) receiving an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor or an angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB), over a 90-day treatment period.

## Design, Setting, and Participants
Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group study conducted at 148 sites across 23 countries between June 2013 and August 2014.
- Screened: 1501
- Randomized: 823
- Received study drug: 821
- Mean age: 64.2 years; 78% male
- Baseline: 36.7% had very high albuminuria (UACR ≥300 mg/g); 40.0% had an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) ≤60 mL/min/1.73 m²

## Interventions
Placebo or one of seven finerenone once-daily dose groups (1.25, 2.5, 5, 7.5, 10, 15, or 20 mg/d; an additional 25-mg exploratory dose was included), for 90 days, on top of ACE inhibitor or ARB therapy.

## Main Outcomes and Measures
Primary outcome: the placebo-corrected mean ratio of the urinary albumin-creatinine ratio (UACR) at day 90 relative to baseline.
Prespecified secondary safety outcome: hyperkalemia leading to discontinuation; and eGFR decline.

## Results
Finerenone demonstrated a dose-dependent reduction in UACR. Placebo-corrected mean ratio of UACR at day 90 relative to baseline:
- 7.5 mg/d: 0.79 (90% CI, 0.68–0.91; P = .004)
- 10 mg/d: 0.76 (90% CI, 0.65–0.88; P = .001)
- 15 mg/d: 0.67 (90% CI, 0.58–0.77; P < .001)
- 20 mg/d: 0.62 (90% CI, 0.54–0.72; P < .001)

Hyperkalemia leading to discontinuation was not observed in the placebo and finerenone 10-mg/d groups; it occurred at rates including 7.5 mg/d (2.1%), 15 mg/d (3.2%), and 20 mg/d (1.7%).

No significant differences between finerenone and placebo were observed for the outcome of eGFR decrease of 30% or more, or for overall adverse events.

## Conclusions and Relevance
Among patients with diabetic nephropathy, most receiving an ACE inhibitor or an ARB, the addition of finerenone compared with placebo resulted in improvement in the urinary albumin-creatinine ratio. Further trials are needed to compare finerenone with other active medications.
