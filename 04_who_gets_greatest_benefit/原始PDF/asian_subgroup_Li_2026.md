## Abstract

**Introduction:** Finerenone, a nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist, reduced the risk of heart and kidney outcomes in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes (T2D) in FIDELITY, a prespecified pooled analysis of the FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD trials. This subanalysis explored the efficacy and safety of finerenone vs. placebo in Chinese patients.

**Methods:** Patients with CKD (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio [UACR] 30–5,000 mg/g, estimated glomerular filtration rate [eGFR] ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m²) and T2D, on optimized renin-angiotensin system inhibitors, were randomized 1:1 to finerenone or placebo. Key outcomes included a kidney composite (kidney failure, sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease from baseline over ≥4 weeks, or kidney-related death) and a cardiovascular (CV) composite (CV death, nonfatal myocardial infarction, nonfatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure). An additional kidney composite outcome was kidney failure, sustained ≥40% eGFR decrease from baseline over ≥4 weeks, or kidney-related death. Treatment-emergent adverse events were also assessed.

**Results:** In this Chinese patient subanalysis (n = 697), finerenone reduced the risk of the ≥57% and ≥40% kidney composite outcomes (hazard ratio [HR]: 0.57; 95% confidence interval [CI]: 0.38–0.86; p = 0.0066 and HR: 0.54; 95% CI: 0.40–0.74; p < 0.0001, respectively) and CV composite outcome risk vs. placebo (HR: 0.82; 95% CI: 0.52–1.29; p = 0.3866). Safety outcomes were similar between treatment arms. Hyperkalemia leading to treatment discontinuation was low for finerenone (2.6%) and placebo (0.9%).

**Conclusion:** Finerenone demonstrated kidney benefits, favorable trends on CV outcome, and a manageable safety profile in the FIDELITY Chinese subpopulation.

## Plain Language Summary
Finerenone is a medicine used to treat the heart and kidneys in people with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes (T2D). Finerenone was tested in 12,990 participants in a clinical project called FIDELITY. Here, the researchers looked at how well finerenone worked in Chinese participants. They saw that finerenone slowed down kidney damage and lowered the chances of heart disease-related problems (heart attack, stroke, hospital admission for heart failure, and kidney-related deaths). Among the Chinese participants, side effects like a rise in blood potassium were effectively managed by doctors.

## Introduction (key facts)
- In 2017, 7.6% of all deaths globally due to CV disease were attributable to impaired kidney function.
- A leading global cause of CKD is T2D; up to 40% of patients with T2D have CKD.
- In China, CKD prevalence was estimated at 82 million; incidence of treated ESKD in China reported at 127 per million population in 2021.
- Three drug classes with strong evidence-based recommendations: renin-angiotensin system inhibitors (RASis), SGLT-2 inhibitors, and nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (MRAs).
- Finerenone: selective nonsteroidal MRA. Chinese NMPA granted initial marketing authorization in 2022 (based on FIDELIO-DKD, eGFR 25–75 mL/min/1.73 m² with albuminuria) to reduce risk of sustained eGFR decline and ESKD; label extended after FIGARO-DKD to lower risk of HHF and CV death.
- FIDELITY = prespecified pooled analysis of individual patient-level data from FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD.

## Methods (key facts)
- FIDELITY consisted of 13,171 randomized patients from FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD (phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter). 181 excluded for GCP violations → 12,990 patients in statistical analyses.
- Chinese patients: aged ≥18 years with CKD; either (i) persistent, moderately increased UACR 30 to <300 mg/g and eGFR 25 to ≤90 mL/min/1.73 m², or (ii) persistent, severely increased UACR 300 to ≤5,000 mg/g and eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m²; and T2D; serum potassium ≤4.8 mmol/L at screening. Patients with symptomatic HF with reduced ejection fraction excluded.
- Randomized 1:1 to once-daily finerenone (10 mg or 20 mg) or placebo, on optimized RASi.
- Key kidney composite: time to first onset of kidney failure, sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease from baseline ≥4 weeks, or kidney-related death.
- Kidney failure = ESKD (chronic dialysis ≥90 days or kidney transplantation) or sustained eGFR decrease to <15 mL/min/1.73 m².
- CV composite: time to CV death, nonfatal MI, nonfatal stroke, or HHF.
- Additional kidney composite: kidney failure, sustained ≥40% eGFR decrease from baseline ≥4 weeks, or kidney-related death.
- Statistics: full analysis set. HRs with 95% CI from stratified Cox proportional hazards model; p values for interaction (p_interaction) comparing Chinese vs. non-Chinese. Linear mixed-effects models for UACR/eGFR changes; two-slope linear spline mixed model for chronic eGFR slope (change from 4 months to end of study).

## Results

### Population
- Among 12,990 FIDELITY patients, **697 patients (5.4%) identified as Chinese** (finerenone n = 350; placebo n = 347).
- ≥24 months on study drug: 72.3% (finerenone) vs. 71.8% (placebo); median treatment durations 30.8 vs. 30.2 months.
- Mean daily dose of finerenone: 16.7 ± 4.3 mg vs. placebo 17.5 ± 3.7 mg.

Notable: Chinese subpopulation had higher median UACR at baseline vs. non-Chinese (985.0 vs. 497.8 mg/g) despite similar baseline eGFR (60.0 vs. 57.4 mL/min/1.73 m²). Smaller proportion of Chinese had baseline serum potassium >4.5 mmol/L (31.5% vs. 21.4%) and history of CVD (46.2% vs. 36.4%).

### Kidney composite outcome
- Overall FIDELITY: finerenone associated with 24% relative risk reduction in ≥57% kidney composite (HR 0.76; 95% CI 0.66–0.88; p = 0.0001).
- **Chinese subpopulation, ≥57% kidney composite:** occurred in 39 patients (11.1%) finerenone vs. 63 patients (18.2%) placebo → **43% relative risk reduction (HR 0.57; 95% CI 0.38–0.86; p = 0.0066)**. At year 3, absolute risk reduction 8.3% (NNT = 12).
- Sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease: HR 0.60; 95% CI 0.38–0.92; p = 0.0187.
- Kidney failure: HR 0.53; 95% CI 0.33–0.86; p = 0.0094.
- ESKD: HR 0.63; 95% CI 0.36–1.10 (trend).
- No significant subgroup interaction between Chinese and non-Chinese (≥57% kidney composite p_interaction = 0.15). Non-Chinese: 21% reduction (HR 0.79; 95% CI 0.68–0.91).

### CV composite outcome
- Overall FIDELITY: HR 0.86; 95% CI 0.78–0.95; p = 0.0018.
- **Chinese subpopulation:** 36 patients (10.3%) finerenone vs. 41 (11.8%) placebo → **HR 0.82; 95% CI 0.52–1.29; p = 0.3866** (numerical reduction, favors finerenone).
- HHF numerical reduction: HR 0.55; 95% CI 0.24–1.25; p = 0.1455.
- Non-Chinese: 14% reduction (HR 0.86; 95% CI 0.78–0.95). CV composite p_interaction = 0.89.

### Other prespecified outcomes
- Overall FIDELITY ≥40% kidney composite: reduced 16% (HR 0.84; 95% CI 0.77–0.92; p = 0.0003).
- **Chinese ≥40% kidney composite:** 65 patients (18.6%) finerenone vs. 107 (30.8%) placebo → **46% relative risk reduction (HR 0.54; 95% CI 0.40–0.74; p < 0.0001)**.
- Chronic eGFR slope (Chinese): finerenone −4.57 vs. placebo −6.80 mL/min/1.73 m²/year; LS mean difference 2.24 (1.26 to 3.22); p < 0.0001.
- UACR: placebo-corrected reduction of 33% at month 4 (LS mean ratio 0.67; 95% CI 0.60–0.76; p < 0.0001), maintained to month 36.

## Discussion (key points)
- Largest Chinese subpopulation analysis to date assessing finerenone vs. placebo in CKD + T2D.
- Effect on ≥57% kidney composite not statistically modified between Chinese and non-Chinese subpopulations, but reduction appeared more pronounced in Chinese, key driver being 47% risk reduction in kidney failure.
- Higher median UACR at baseline in Chinese subpopulation vs. overall FIDELITY, possibly due to high sodium intake/sensitivity. Genetic Epidemiology Network of Salt-Sensitivity (GenSalt): ~32.4% of Chinese adults in rural northern China sodium sensitive.
- A recent mediation analysis showed 84% of the ≥57% kidney composite outcome in FIDELITY was mediated by reduction in UACR.
- CV composite: numerical reduction in Chinese but not statistically significant — likely due to small Chinese sample size and low CV event occurrence (11% vs. 14% in non-Chinese). Meaningful conclusion on CV cannot be fully drawn.
- Limitations: post hoc subgroup analysis focused on China only; limited sample size (n = 697); low number of CV events; hypothesis-generating.
- Conclusion: Finerenone reduced risk of CKD progression and displayed a trend toward more favorable CV outcomes vs. placebo in mainland Chinese patients with CKD and T2D; well-balanced safety with manageable hyperkalemia risk. Supports use of finerenone for all stages of CKD in Chinese patients.
