# Cardiovascular and kidney outcomes with finerenone in patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease: the FIDELITY pooled analysis

**European Heart Journal, 2022;43(6):474–484**
Rajiv Agarwal, Gerasimos Filippatos, Bertram Pitt, Stefan D. Anker, Peter Rossing, Amer Joseph, Peter Kolkhof, Christina Nowack, Martin Gebel, Luis M. Ruilope, George L. Bakris

## Study design and population

- Prespecified pooled analysis combining the phase 3 trials **FIDELIO-DKD (NCT02540993)** and **FIGARO-DKD (NCT02545049)**.
- **13,026** patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease (145 patients with GCP violations excluded from the pooled population).
- Randomized 1:1 to finerenone vs. placebo, on top of maximally tolerated renin–angiotensin system (RAS) inhibition.
- Median follow-up: **3.0 years** (IQR 2.3–3.8).

### Baseline characteristics
- Mean age: 64.8 years
- Male: 69.8%
- Mean eGFR: 57.6 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Median UACR: 515 mg/g
- History of cardiovascular disease: 45.6%
- On RAS inhibition: 99.8%

## Primary and key outcomes

### Composite cardiovascular outcome
(cardiovascular death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure)
- Finerenone: 825 patients (12.7%)
- Placebo: 939 patients (14.4%)
- **HR 0.86 (95% CI 0.78–0.95; P = 0.0018)**
- Number needed to treat (NNT): 46 (95% CI 29–109)

### Composite kidney outcome
(kidney failure, sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease from baseline, or renal death)
- Finerenone: 360 patients (5.5%)
- Placebo: 465 patients (7.1%)
- **HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.67–0.88; P = 0.0002)**
- NNT: 60 (95% CI 38–142)

### Individual / secondary endpoints
- **Hospitalization for heart failure:** HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.66–0.92; P = 0.0030) — 22% relative risk reduction
- **Sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease:** HR 0.70 (95% CI 0.60–0.83; P < 0.0001) — 30% risk reduction
- **End-stage kidney disease:** HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.64–0.99; P = 0.040) — 20% risk reduction
- **Secondary kidney outcome (sustained ≥40% eGFR decrease):** Finerenone 854 (13.1%) vs. placebo 995 (15.3%); HR 0.85 (95% CI 0.77–0.93; P = 0.0004)
- **UACR:** ~32% greater reduction with finerenone vs. placebo at month 4
- **All-cause mortality:** not statistically significant (P = 0.051)

## Safety

### Hyperkalaemia
- Any hyperkalaemia: Finerenone 14.0% vs. placebo 6.9%
- Permanent treatment discontinuation due to hyperkalaemia: 1.7% vs. 0.6%
- Serious hyperkalaemia: 1.1% vs. 0.2%
- Hyperkalaemia-related hospitalization: 0.9% vs. 0.2%
- Fatal hyperkalaemia: 0 in both groups

### Hypokalaemia
- Finerenone 1.1% vs. placebo 2.3%

### Serum potassium change (month 4)
- Finerenone: +0.21 mmol/L (SD 0.47)
- Placebo: +0.02 mmol/L (SD 0.43)

### Overall
- Serious adverse events: 31.6% (finerenone) vs. 33.7% (placebo)
- Acute kidney injury / renal-related AEs: similar between groups (~3.4–3.6%)

## Conclusions

Finerenone reduced the risk of clinically important cardiovascular and kidney outcomes vs. placebo across the spectrum of CKD in patients with type 2 diabetes. Hyperkalaemia was more frequent with finerenone but was manageable with monitoring and dose management; serious/fatal hyperkalaemia events were uncommon.
