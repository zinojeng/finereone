# Finerenone Reduces Risk of Incident Heart Failure in Patients With Chronic Kidney Disease and Type 2 Diabetes: Analyses From the FIGARO-DKD Trial

**Filippatos G, Anker SD, Agarwal R, Ruilope LM, Rossing P, Bakris GL, Tasto C, Joseph A, Kolkhof P, Lage A, Pitt B; on behalf of the FIGARO-DKD Investigators**

*Circulation.* 2022;145:437-447. DOI: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.121.057983. February 8, 2022. NCT02545049.

## Abstract

**BACKGROUND:** Chronic kidney disease and type 2 diabetes are independently associated with heart failure (HF), a leading cause of morbidity and mortality. In the FIDELIO-DKD (Finerenone in Reducing Kidney Failure and Disease Progression in Diabetic Kidney Disease) and FIGARO-DKD (Finerenone in Reducing Cardiovascular Mortality and Morbidity in Diabetic Kidney Disease) trials, finerenone (a selective, nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist) improved cardiovascular outcomes in patients with albuminuric chronic kidney disease and type 2 diabetes. These prespecified analyses from FIGARO-DKD assessed the effect of finerenone on clinically important HF outcomes.

**METHODS:** Patients with type 2 diabetes and albuminuric chronic kidney disease (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio ≥30 to <300 mg/g and estimated glomerular filtration rate ≥25 to ≤90 mL per min per 1.73 m², or urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio ≥300 to ≤5000 mg/g and estimated glomerular filtration rate ≥60 mL per min per 1.73 m²), without symptomatic HF with reduced ejection fraction, were randomized to finerenone or placebo. Time-to-first-event outcomes included new-onset HF (first hospitalization for HF [HHF] in patients without a history of HF at baseline); cardiovascular death or first HHF; HF-related death or first HHF; first HHF; cardiovascular death or total (first or recurrent) HHF; HF-related death or total HHF; and total HHF. Outcomes were evaluated in the overall population and in prespecified subgroups categorized by baseline HF history (as reported by the investigators).

**RESULTS:** Overall, 7352 patients were included in these analyses; 571 (7.8%) had a history of HF at baseline. New-onset HF was significantly reduced with finerenone versus placebo (1.9% versus 2.8%; hazard ratio [HR], 0.68 [95% CI, 0.50-0.93]; P=0.0162). In the overall population, the incidences of all HF outcomes analyzed were significantly lower with finerenone than placebo, including an 18% lower risk of cardiovascular death or first HHF (HR, 0.82 [95% CI, 0.70-0.95]; P=0.011), a 29% lower risk of first HHF (HR, 0.71 [95% CI, 0.56-0.90]; P=0.0043) and a 30% lower rate of total HHF (rate ratio, 0.70 [95% CI, 0.52-0.94]). The effects of finerenone on improving HF outcomes were not modified by a history of HF. The incidence of treatment-emergent adverse events was balanced between treatment groups.

**CONCLUSIONS:** The results from these FIGARO-DKD analyses demonstrate that finerenone reduces new-onset HF and improves other HF outcomes in patients with chronic kidney disease and type 2 diabetes, irrespective of a history of HF.

**Key Words:** aldosterone; chronic kidney disease; type 2 diabetes; finerenone; heart failure; mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist

## Clinical Perspective

**What Is New?**
- These prespecified analyses of the FIGARO-DKD trial describe novel heart failure (HF)-related outcomes, not previously published in finerenone studies, including new-onset HF (hospitalization for HF in patients without a history of HF), and different outcomes containing first or total (first and recurrent) hospitalization for HF events in the overall population.
- The results indicate that, in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes (T2D) on a maximum tolerated labeled dose of renin-angiotensin system inhibitor therapy, finerenone reduces new-onset HF and improves HF-related outcomes, irrespective of history of HF.

**What Are the Clinical Implications?**
- This is the first indication that a nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist may provide benefit in a population with CKD and T2D in which patients with HF with reduced ejection fraction and New York Heart Association class II to IV were excluded, indicating that patients with CKD in T2D at risk of HF or early-stage HF may benefit from finerenone treatment.
- FIGARO-DKD included patients with less advanced CKD than studied in other trials in patients with CKD and T2D.
- The results suggest screening for albuminuria and early treatment of patients with CKD and T2D are important to reduce HF burden in this patient population.

## Methods (key details)

- FIGARO-DKD: international, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter, phase III trial. Registered EudraCT 2015-000950-39 and ClinicalTrials.gov NCT02545049.
- Eligibility: age ≥18 years, clinically diagnosed T2D and albuminuric CKD (either [1] persistent, moderately increased UACR ≥30 to <300 mg/g and eGFR ≥25 to <90 mL/min/1.73 m², or [2] persistent severely increased UACR ≥300 to ≤5000 mg/g and eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²), treated with a maximum tolerated dose of ACE inhibitor or ARB, serum potassium ≤4.8 mEq/L at screening.
- Excluded: chronic symptomatic HFrEF (NYHA class II-IV) with class IA recommendation for an MRA at run-in visit.
- Randomization 1:1 to once-daily finerenone (10 or 20 mg) or matching placebo. Starting dose 10 mg (eGFR 25 to <60) or 20 mg (eGFR ≥60).
- Median follow-up 3.4 years.

**Prespecified HF outcomes:** (1) time to new-onset HF (first HHF in patients without a history of HF at baseline); (2) time to cardiovascular death or first HHF; (3) time to HF-related death or first HHF; (4) time to first HHF; (5) time to cardiovascular death or total (first or recurrent) HHF; (6) time to HF-related death or total HHF; (7) time to total HHF.

## Patients

- FIGARO-DKD randomized 7437 patients; after prospective exclusion of 85 patients (critical GCP violations), 7352 patients assessed in the full analysis set.
- 571 (7.8%) had a history of HF at baseline (290 finerenone, 281 placebo).
- Patients with a history of HF vs without: more likely female, White (less likely Asian), higher BMI and waist circumference, higher hs-CRP, lower heart rate, lower mean eGFR and median UACR, longer duration of diabetes, more likely atherosclerotic CVD, more likely receiving β-blockers, diuretics, potassium supplements, and insulin; fewer treated with metformin and DPP-4 inhibitors.

## Effect of Finerenone on Time to New-Onset HF (First HHF in Patients Without History of HF)

- New-onset HF (first adjudicated HHF in patients without history of HF): finerenone 65/3396 (1.9%); 0.57 per 100 patient-years [95% CI, 0.44-0.72] vs placebo 95/3385 (2.8%); 0.84 per 100 patient-years [95% CI, 0.68-1.02]; **HR 0.68 [95% CI, 0.50-0.93]; P=0.016** (Figure 1).
- Absolute risk reduction at month 48 = 1.1%; number needed to treat = 91 (95% CI, 49-605).

## Effect of Finerenone on HF-Associated Outcomes (overall population)

- CV death or first HHF: finerenone 290/3686 (7.9%); placebo 351/3666 (9.6%); **HR 0.82 [95% CI, 0.70-0.95]; P=0.011**. ARR at month 48 = 1.8%; NNT = 55 (95% CI, 29-393).
- HF-related death or first HHF: finerenone 120/3686 (3.3%); placebo 173/3666 (4.7%); **HR 0.68 [95% CI, 0.54-0.86]; P=0.0013**. ARR month 48 = 1.7%; NNT = 60 (95% CI, 35-177).
- First HHF: finerenone 117/3686 (3.2%); placebo 163/3666 (4.4%); **HR 0.71 [95% CI, 0.56-0.90]; P=0.0043**. ARR month 48 = 1.4%; NNT = 70 (95% CI, 39-292).
- CV death or total HHF: **rate ratio 0.79 [95% CI, 0.66-0.95]; P=0.013**.
- HF-related death or total HHF: **rate ratio 0.70 [95% CI, 0.53-0.93]; P=0.014**.
- Total HHF: **rate ratio 0.70 [95% CI, 0.52-0.94]**. Absolute reduction in mean cumulative events at month 48 for CV death and total HHF = 3.0%.

**Effect not modified by history of HF** (P for interaction all nonsignificant):
- CV death or first HHF: with history of HF 54/290 (18.6%); without 236/3396 (6.7%); P for interaction = 0.81.
- HF-related death or first HHF: with history 30/290 (10.3%); without 90/3396 (2.7%); P for interaction = 0.82.
- First HHF: with history 29/290 (10.0%); without 88/3396 (2.6%); P for interaction = 0.77.

Because event rates were higher in patients with a history of HF, absolute risk reductions with finerenone were larger than for those without a history of HF. ARR at month 48 for CV death or first HHF: −4.8% (95% CI, −13.7 to 4.1) with history vs −1.6% (95% CI, −3.1 to 0.0) without.

## Subgroup analysis (Figure 3, time to CV death or first HHF)

Results not modified across key prespecified/post hoc subgroups (sex, age, duration of diabetes, BMI, history of CVD, baseline diuretic use, baseline insulin use, baseline GLP-1RA use), except for a nominally significant interaction for the SGLT-2i subgroup:
- Baseline SGLT-2i use: Yes HR 0.35 [95% CI, 0.16-0.75]; No HR 0.85 [95% CI, 0.72-1.00]; **P for interaction = 0.046** (small subgroup, n=314 finerenone / 304 placebo; interpret with caution).

## Safety Outcomes

- Incidence of overall treatment-emergent AEs balanced between treatment groups, irrespective of HF history.
- Treatment-emergent hyperkalemia greater with finerenone vs placebo: 8.3% vs 4.6% in patients with a history of HF; 11.0% vs 5.3% in patients without a history of HF.
- Few cases of hyperkalemia led to permanent discontinuation of study drug: finerenone 0.3% and placebo 0.7% in patients with a history of HF; finerenone 1.3% and placebo 0.3% in patients without a history of HF.
- Finerenone produced modest reduction in systolic blood pressure; no effect on body weight.

## Discussion / Conclusions

- In patients with T2D and CKD (stage 2-4 with moderately elevated albuminuria or stage 1-2 with severely elevated albuminuria) without a history of symptomatic HFrEF, finerenone led to significant reduction in clinically important HF outcomes, including 29% reduction in first HHF and 18% reduction in CV death or first HHF vs placebo. Effect independent of history of HF.
- Risk of new-onset HF was 32% lower with finerenone. This is the first study to show that an MRA, specifically the selective nonsteroidal MRA finerenone, may prevent development of HF in patients with CKD and T2D.
- The entire FIGARO-DKD population had CKD and T2D and can be considered as having at least stage A HF (at risk of developing HF); results suggest finerenone slows progression from at-risk (stage A) or pre-HF (stage B) to symptomatic HF (stage C).
- 62% of patients had albuminuric CKD with eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²; initiating treatment at earlier disease stages may be more beneficial.
- Limitations: definition of medical history of HF determined on the basis of investigator reports without specification of LVEF or natriuretic peptide levels; relatively small subgroup sample size of patients with a history of HF.
