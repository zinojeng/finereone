# First-in-human study evaluating safety, pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics of lorundrostat, a novel and highly selective aldosterone synthase inhibitor

**Citation:** Clin Transl Sci. 2024;17:e70000. https://doi.org/10.1111/cts.70000
Shimizu H, Tortorici MA (co-first authors, equal contribution), et al.
Affiliations: Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma Corporation (Japan/UK); Mineralys Therapeutics, Radnor, Pennsylvania, USA; ICON, Groningen, The Netherlands.

## Abstract

Dysregulation of the mineralocorticoid hormone aldosterone is an increasingly prevalent cause of hypertension. Aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2) shares 93% homology to 11β-hydroxylase (CYP11B1), which produces cortisol. Lorundrostat, a highly selective inhibitor of CYP11B2, is a potential safe and effective treatment for aldosterone-dependent, uncontrolled hypertension, including treatment-resistant hypertension. Lorundrostat showed highly selective inhibition of CYP11B2 in vitro, with **374-fold selectivity for CYP11B2 vs. CYP11B1**. A first-in-human study of single ascending doses ranging from **5 to 800 mg** and multiple ascending doses ranging from **40 to 360 mg once daily** was conducted in healthy participants. After single- and multiple-dose administration, lorundrostat plasma levels peaked **1–3 h** after administration with a **t1/2 of 10–12 h**. Plasma aldosterone decreased up to **40% with single 100-mg to 200-mg doses** and up to **70% with single 400 to 800-mg doses**. Plasma aldosterone returned to baseline within 16 h after single 100-mg doses and multiple once-daily 120-mg doses. Lorundrostat demonstrated a favorable safety profile in healthy participants. Dose- and exposure-dependent inhibition of renal tubular sodium reabsorption was observed across a clinically relevant dose range with no suppression of basal or cosyntropin-stimulated cortisol production and only a modest increase in mean serum potassium.

## Study Highlights

- **Current knowledge:** Applying 2017 hypertension guidelines (BP <130/80 mmHg) to NHANES 2017–2020 data, 77.5% of US adults with hypertension had uncontrolled blood pressure. Development of selective CYP11B2 inhibitors suitable for human use has been challenging.
- **Question addressed:** Safety, tolerability, PK, and PD of single and multiple oral doses of lorundrostat in healthy adults.
- **What it adds:** A highly selective CYP11B2 inhibitor with favorable safety, optimized PK, and evidence of inhibition of renal tubular aldosterone signaling across a broad dose range with no suppression of basal or stimulated cortisol production. Unlike MRAs, lorundrostat is designed to reduce (rather than stimulate) circulating aldosterone, potentially reducing adverse non-MR-mediated aldosterone signaling via GPER (GPR30), avoiding estrogenic MRA side effects and preventing "aldosterone breakthrough" during RAASi treatment.

## Introduction (key points)

- Lorundrostat is a novel molecular entity targeting the RAAS via inhibition of aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2), producing targeted suppression of aldosterone production.
- Aldosterone synthase (CYP11B2) is a mitochondrial cytochrome P450 enzyme converting 11-deoxycorticosterone (11-DOC) to aldosterone in three steps. CYP11B1 (glucocorticoid biosynthesis) has >93% homology to CYP11B2; high selectivity for CYP11B2 over CYP11B1 is essential.
- Osilodrostat, the first aldosterone synthase inhibitor, lacked high selectivity, resulted in blunted ACTH-stimulated cortisol release in some patients, and instead became a treatment for Cushing's disease.
- Global prevalence of treatment-resistant hypertension estimated at 10.3% (higher in CKD 22.9%, renal transplant recipients 56.0%, older adults 12.3%).
- A study in left ventricular dysfunction (RESOLVD) showed RAAS suppression with ACEi + ARB did not suppress long-term aldosterone secretion ("aldosterone breakthrough").

## Methods

**In vitro:** Inhibitory effects on hCYP11B1 and hCYP11B2 evaluated using V79 cells stably expressing each enzyme; osilodrostat used as positive control.

**Phase I study design:** Four-part, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, first-in-human study (single-center, Netherlands; November 16, 2016 – May 01, 2017; EudraCT Number 2016-003500-32).
- **Part 1 (SAD):** Single ascending doses, fasted; cohorts 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 400, 800 mg. Six lorundrostat + two placebo per cohort (with sentinel dosing).
- **Part 2 (MAD):** Once-daily dosing Days 1–7, fed; final dose levels 40, 120, 360 mg. Nine lorundrostat + three placebo per cohort. ACTH challenge test on Day -2 and Day 6 to evaluate selectivity for aldosterone synthesis.
- **Part 3 (sex effect):** Single 100-mg dose, fasted, female participants (6 active + 2 placebo).
- **Part 4 (age effect):** Single 100-mg dose, fasted, male participants aged ≥65 years (6 active + 2 placebo).
- PK by non-compartmental analysis (WinNonlin v6.3); dose proportionality by power model.

## Results

### In vitro selectivity
Lorundrostat inhibited hCYP11B2 and hCYP11B1 with inhibition constants of **1.27 nmol/L** and **475 nmol/L**, respectively → **374-fold selectivity for CYP11B2 over CYP11B1**.

### Participant disposition
- 245 screened, **116 randomized**. All in parts 1, 3, 4 completed per protocol; one participant in part 2 did not complete.
- Part 1 (SAD): 64 males aged 18–55.
- Part 2 (MAD): 36 males aged 19–54.
- Part 3 (sex): 8 females aged 20–35.
- Part 4 (age): 8 males aged 68–80.
- All participants were white; none Hispanic/Latino.

### Safety and tolerability
- Well tolerated; **no serious TEAEs**. One participant (cohort 3, part 2) withdrawn Day 2 due to sinus tachycardia (117 bpm) before dosing on Day 2 (received only one 360-mg dose Day 1); telemetry suggested stimulus–response reaction to study procedures rather than drug relationship.
- Overall TEAE incidence comparable: lorundrostat 41/87 (47%) vs. placebo 18/29 (62%).
- Dizziness (mild) in 9 (10.3%) lorundrostat vs. 1 (3.4%) placebo.
- No clinically significant findings in clinical laboratory, vital signs, ECGs, or physical examination.

### Pharmacokinetics
- **SAD (5–800 mg):** Rapid absorption, median Tmax 1.0–1.5 h; mean t1/2 7.9–10.5 h; apparent clearance 18.7–27.6 L/h. Dose-proportional AUC0–∞ (95% CI of slopes included 1). Cmax slightly greater than dose proportional (slope 1.104, 95% CI 1.043–1.164). Interindividual variability of Cmax and AUC0–∞ low to moderate (<20%).
- **MAD (40–360 mg once daily × 7 days):** Rapid absorption, median Tmax 1.5–2.5 h. AUC0–24 and Cmax slightly more than dose proportional (Day 1 slopes 1.143–1.146, 95% CI 1.011–1.281; Day 7 AUC0–24 slope 1.147, 95% CI 1.060–1.234). Steady state reached ~Day 5; slight accumulation (mean accumulation ratio for AUC0–24 1.15–1.19). Mean t1/2 Day 7: 9.1–11.9 h.
- Fraction excreted in urine as unchanged drug was low (Day 1: 1.2–1.8%; Day 7: 2.3–2.6%); renal clearance low (0.3–0.6 L/h).
- **Sex (part 3):** No statistically significant difference in exposure between females and males; median Tmax female 1.3 h vs male 1.5 h; mean t1/2 female 12.4 h vs male 10.0 h.
- **Age (part 4):** No statistically significant difference between older and younger adults; median Tmax older 1.0 h vs younger 1.5 h; mean t1/2 older 11.4 h vs younger 10.0 h.
- **Food:** PK profile in fed state (part 2 first dose) did not appear affected vs fasted (part 1).

### Pharmacodynamics
- **SAD:** All doses reduced plasma aldosterone concentration (PAC) vs baseline at 4 h and 8 h post-dose. Lower doses (5–50 mg) PAC returned near baseline within 24 h; higher doses (100–800 mg) reduction sustained to 24 h. Maximum PAC decrease ~80% vs placebo at 4 h and 8 h. Single doses 10–800 mg reduced PAC AUC0–24 dose-dependently, statistically significant decreases **36% to 77% vs placebo**, without affecting cortisol.
- **MAD:** PAC decreased dose-dependently 2–12 h post-dose on Day 7. From 24 h after last dose (Day 7), a clear rebound in PAC (73–163% vs placebo) observed at all dose levels, sustained at least until Day 10 (3 days after last dose).
- **ACTH challenge:** At all MAD levels, lorundrostat completely blunted the ACTH-stimulated aldosterone response on Day 6 vs placebo, with no effect on the cortisol response. No relevant changes in serum cortisol or plasma 11-deoxycortisol under non-ACTH conditions.
- Once-daily lorundrostat 40–360 mg increased plasma renin activity and serum 11-DOC vs placebo. Elevations in serum potassium (from reduced PAC) were mild across all doses; declined during washout.
- Urine sodium and urinary log10(10 × Na+/K+) ratio increased at all SAD doses (consistent with reduced aldosterone signaling). In MAD, increased on Day 1, returned to baseline by Day 7; Day 7 to Day 9 clear reductions in ratio (rebound effect).

## Discussion

- Oral lorundrostat, single dose up to 800 mg (fasted) or once-daily up to 360 mg × 7 days (fed), was well tolerated with a favorable safety profile in healthy males; single 100-mg dose also well tolerated by healthy females and older adult males (≥65 years).
- No relevant effects of sex, age, or fed/fasting state on PK. A separate formal food effect study (tablet formulation, data on file) confirmed lack of food effect.
- Plasma peaks 1–3 h post-dose, t1/2 10–12 h; exposure generally dose proportional.
- PAC AUC0–24 reduced up to 40% with single 100–200 mg doses and up to 70% with single 400–800 mg doses. No meaningful effect on serum cortisol (basal or ACTH-stimulated) — consistent with 374-fold CYP11B2 vs CYP11B1 selectivity vs non-selective osilodrostat.
- In MAD, doses ≥40 mg increased urinary log10(10 × Na+/K+) ratio confirming functional effect during initial aldosterone suppression.
- Within 2 days of MAD initiation, serum potassium rose modestly in all three dose cohorts by group mean **0.28–0.38 mmol/L**, remained elevated through 7 days, and rapidly fell toward baseline on cessation (consistent with 10–12 h half-life).
- Because PD effects on renal electrolyte regulation were near maximum at 120 mg daily and excessive 11-DOC accumulation was observed at 360 mg once daily, dose selection for hypertension should avoid the higher dose and **focus on doses up to 120 mg daily**.
- Results supported progression to a phase II dose-range finding and proof-of-concept study in uncontrolled/treatment-resistant hypertension. The capsule formulation used here is equivalent to ~50% of the exposure seen in the tablet formulation used in phase II (phase II doses: 12.5 mg once/twice daily, 25 mg twice daily, 50 mg once daily, 100 mg once daily). ~77% of US adults with hypertension fail to achieve targeted BP control.
