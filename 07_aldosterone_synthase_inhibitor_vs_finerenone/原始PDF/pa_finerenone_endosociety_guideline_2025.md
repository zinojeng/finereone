# Primary Aldosteronism: An Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline (2025)

## Relevance to project (ASI vs finerenone / MRA titration)
- Spironolactone recommended **first-line MRA** (over other MRAs) due to low cost and wide availability.
- MRA preferred over ENaC inhibitors (amiloride, triamterene).
- **Renin-guided titration**: when BP uncontrolled AND renin suppressed → increase MRA dose to raise (normalize) renin. This is the core physiological monitoring principle that ASIs (e.g., baxdrostat, lorundrostat) and finerenone trials are compared against.

## Structure of guideline
- Guideline Development Panel (GDP), multidisciplinary; used GRADE approach.
- Defined **10 questions** on diagnosis and treatment of PA; systematic reviews for each; GRADE Evidence-to-Decision (EtD) framework.
- Released July 15, 2025 at Endocrine Society Annual Meeting; co-endorsed by 6 international organizations.

## Key diagnostic recommendations (abstract, verbatim gist)
- Screen ALL individuals with hypertension for PA: measure aldosterone and renin, determine aldosterone-to-renin ratio (ARR); subsequent care guided by results.
- All PA patients should receive PA-specific therapy (medical or surgical).
- Medical therapy for those not desiring/not candidate for surgery, or when probability of lateralizing PA is low.
- Aldosterone suppression testing when intermediate probability of lateralizing PA and patient desires surgery eligibility.
- Positive suppression test / high probability of lateralizing PA → CT + adrenal venous sampling (AVS) before deciding medical vs surgical.
- All PA + adrenal adenoma → 1-mg overnight dexamethasone suppression test.

## Treatment recommendations (full-text extract from publisher page)

**Recommendation 7 (renin-guided titration)** — "In individuals with primary aldosteronism receiving PA-specific medical therapy whose hypertension is not controlled and renin is suppressed, we suggest increasing PA-specific medical therapy to raise renin" (2 | ⊕OOO — conditional/weak, very low certainty).

**Recommendation 9 (spironolactone first-line)** — Suggests spironolactone over other MRAs "due to its low cost and widespread availability" (2 | ⊕OOO). Panel notes: "all MRAs, when titrated to equivalent potencies, are anticipated to have similar efficacy in treating PA." MRAs with greater MR specificity and fewer androgen/progesterone receptor side effects may be preferred in some situations.

**Recommendation 10 (MRA over ENaC inhibitor)** — Use mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists rather than epithelial sodium-channel (ENaC) inhibitors (amiloride, triamterene) for PA (2 | ⊕OOO).

### MRA dosing strategy (full-text extract)
- Initial dosing: **spironolactone 12.5–25 mg/d** or **eplerenone 25 mg daily**.
- More severe cases: **spironolactone 50 mg/d** or **eplerenone 50 mg twice daily**.
- Dose adjustments at **8- to 12-week intervals**.
- Most individuals achieve renin normalization at **spironolactone 50–100 mg/day**.

### Potassium management (full-text extract)
- Serum potassium normalization typically in the **first 3 to 5 days**.
- Potassium supplementation can be reduced at **day 2 to 4** of MRA initiation.

### Blood pressure targets
- No explicit BP numeric target specified; "the primary goal of therapy is control of BP."

## Notes
- The guideline is a qualitative recommendation document; it does not report trial-level BP mmHg deltas, % biochemical response, HR/OR (those live in the companion systematic review, PMID 40658500, DOI 10.1210/clinem/dgaf290, which included 95 studies / 7 RCTs + 88 observational).
