## Data-integrity assessment for §8 of the review — NO impact

**The erratum corrects a plotting error only, not any efficacy or safety statistic.**

- **Nature of the error**: in a per-patient longitudinal figure, the solid→dashed line transition (the visual marker of treatment withdrawal) was drawn at the wrong time point for **two** patients — shown at week 12, should have been at **~week 36** and **~week 60**.
- **Direction of the correction**: the two patients stayed on treatment **longer** than the original figure implied (withdrawal later, not earlier).
- **No numeric value is retracted, revised, or restated.** No SBP/DBP, aldosterone, renin, potassium, DOC, cortisol, or event-count figure is touched.
- NEJM notes the online version is already fixed: *"The letter is correct at NEJM.org."*

### Cross-check against what §8 actually asserts
Grep of the topic-07 main file (lines 431 and 690) shows §8's SPARK claims are: N=15 (14 into the W72 extension), baseline SBP 151.5 mmHg, PRA<1 in 80%, hypokalaemia 53%, ΔSBP −29.5/−24.4/−23.9 mmHg at W12 by 2/4/8 mg, BP<140/90 in 11/15 (73%), BP<130/80 40%, 24h urinary aldosterone 30.3→3.2 µg/day, PRA≥1.0 in 7/15 (47%), K⁺ 3.9→4.7 mEq/L, hyperkalaemia 1/15 (7%) Part 1 → 5/14 (36%) Part 2 (one serious), no deaths.

**None of these is a withdrawal-timing claim, and none is sourced from Figure 1 of the reply letter.** §8's numbers are transcribed from the ENDO 2025 abstract (PMC12545722) of the same SPARK trial, an entirely separate document that this erratum does not touch. **§8 requires no re-checking and no revision as a result of this erratum.**

⚠ Standing caution for future edits: if any future draft asserts *when* SPARK patients withdrew from baxdrostat, the corrected timings (**~week 36 and ~week 60**, not week 12) are the ones to use, and the claim must be sourced from the corrected online reply letter — which is paywalled and **not** in this corpus.
