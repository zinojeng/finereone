# Post-Hoc Analysis on the Effects of Vicadrostat/Empagliflozin by CKD Severity/Hypertension Status

**Correa-Rotter R, Rossing P, Canziani ME, Caramori ML, Cherney D, Cronin L, Hugo C, Ji B, Meyerhoff J, Nangaku M, Silva A, Urbach D, Shah SV, de Zeeuw D, Tuttle KR.** Kidney International Reports (2026) 11, 106545. DOI: 10.1016/j.ekir.2026.106545. Received 25 March 2026; accepted 7 April 2026; published online 16 April 2026. Open access CC BY 4.0.

Correspondence: Ricardo Correa-Rotter, National Medical Science and Nutrition Institute Salvador Zubirán, Mexico City, Mexico. Email: correarotter@gmail.com

## Abstract

**Introduction:** This phase 2 trial (NCT05182840) assessed the efficacy and safety of the aldosterone synthase inhibitor, vicadrostat, alone or with empagliflozin, in people with chronic kidney disease (CKD). These post hoc analyses assessed the effects of vicadrostat, with or without background empagliflozin, on albuminuria across a range of CKD severity and hypertension (HTN) status.

**Methods:** The analysis set included 503 participants who had urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) measures available at baseline and week 14. Subgroup analyses of the effects of vicadrostat, given alone or with empagliflozin, were performed by the presence or absence of uncontrolled HTN (defined as blood pressure [BP] ≥ 140/90 mm Hg and use of ≥2 classes of antihypertensive medication), kidney failure risk equation (KFRE) 4-factor 5-year risk < 5% versus ≥ 5%, Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes (KDIGO) moderate-to-high risk versus very high risk, and diuretic use at baseline.

**Results:** UACR was reduced with vicadrostat, with or without empagliflozin background, across all subgroup analyses (adjusted mean reduction with 10 mg and 20 mg vicadrostat dose groups: 25%–55% vs. 24%–51% in the overall study). UACR reductions were consistent in participants irrespective of KDIGO risk group, KFRE subgroup, presence or absence of uncontrolled HTN at baseline or diuretic use.

**Conclusion:** The effects of vicadrostat on albuminuria reduction, with or without empagliflozin, were generally consistent irrespective of CKD stage and kidney risk as scored using KDIGO or the KFRE, HTN status, or use of diuretics.

**Keywords:** albuminuria; aldosterone synthase inhibitor; chronic kidney disease; kidney function; risk factor

## Introduction / Background

- CKD remains a major clinical challenge despite treatments (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, SGLT2 inhibitors). People with CKD remain at high risk of continuous declining kidney function, cardiovascular (CV) events, and death.
- Aldosterone plays a central role in cardio-kidney-metabolic (CKM) conditions. It promotes sodium retention and potassium excretion, contributing to HTN. Beyond hemodynamic effects, excess aldosterone may contribute to kidney damage through activation of the mineralocorticoid receptor, triggering inflammation and fibrosis.
- Elevated aldosterone levels are independently associated with increased risk of CKD progression; individuals with high aldosterone levels face 45% greater risk of worsening kidney function than those with low aldosterone levels.
- Unlike nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (which block aldosterone signaling at the receptor level), aldosterone synthase inhibitors suppress aldosterone production directly by inhibition of the CYP11B2 enzyme, which catalyzes the final steps in the synthesis of aldosterone.
- **Vicadrostat** is a potent and highly selective aldosterone synthase inhibitor being investigated in phase 3 trials with empagliflozin for the treatment of CKD (NCT06531824), heart failure (NCT06424288 and NCT06935370), and for reduction of CV risk in patients with HTN, T2D, and established CV disease (NCT07064473).
- Primary phase 2 findings demonstrated UACR reductions of ≤40% with 10 mg or 20 mg vicadrostat, and ≤61% when given with empagliflozin. Participants receiving vicadrostat and empagliflozin together (VicaEmpa) showed UACR response rates (defined as UACR reduction ≥ 30%) ≤ 70%.

## Methods

- Phase 2 dose-finding trial. Adults with CKD were eligible if they had eGFR of 30 to < 90 ml/min per 1.73 m² and UACR of 200 to < 5000 mg/g while receiving a maximally tolerated, stable dose of a renin-angiotensin system inhibitor.
- **Design:** Participants randomized (R1) 1:1 to receive background empagliflozin 10 mg or matching placebo (PBO_EMPA) during an 8-week run-in period. Participants were then re-randomized (R2) 1:1:1:1 to receive vicadrostat (3 mg, 10 mg, or 20 mg) or matching placebo (PBO_VICA) for 14 weeks, in addition to assigned empagliflozin or PBO_EMPA.
- **Primary endpoint:** change from R2 baseline in UACR at week 14. UACR response (≥ 30% reduction from R2 baseline in UACR at week 14) was a secondary outcome. Additional outcomes: changes in systolic BP (SBP) and eGFR from R2 baseline to week 14.
- **Subgroup analyses (4 categories):** (i) KDIGO moderate-to-high risk versus very high risk; (ii) KFRE 4-factor 5-year risk (< 5% vs. ≥ 5%) based on UACR, eGFR, age, and sex (female vs. male); (iii) presence or absence of uncontrolled HTN (SBP ≥ 140 mm Hg or diastolic BP ≥ 90 mm Hg and use of ≥ 2 classes of antihypertensive medication); (iv) diuretic use at baseline.
- **Statistical methods:** Primary efficacy analysis based on full analysis set. Subgroup analyses used mixed models for repeated measures (placebo-adjusted changes from R2 baseline in log-transformed UACR at week 14). Interaction terms between treatment and subgroup tested heterogeneity. P_INTERACTION calculated for each of the 4 subgroup analyses. P-values adjusted using Bonferroni correction; adjusted significance level = 0.05/n. All P-values presented are nominal. Adjusted multiple imputation for missing UACR at week 14 for responder analysis.

## Results

### Study Population
- Of 714 participants initially randomized (R1), 586 continued to re-randomization (R2) forming the treated set (n = 583). UACR data incomplete for 83 participants (full analysis set, n = 503).
- Overall mean age 63.8 years (SD 11.3); 33% (196/586) female.
- Of 583 participants in analyses: 229 (40%) categorized as moderate-to-high KDIGO risk; 345 very high risk. (9 patients meeting eGFR G3a/UACR A1 criteria not included in KDIGO subgroup analysis but accounted for in overall cohort.)
- KDIGO very high risk group had higher serum aldosterone (median: 130.7 vs. 119.6 pmol/l), lower eGFR (mean 40.6 vs. 67.8 ml/min per 1.73 m²), higher NT-proBNP (362.3 vs. 231.8 ng/l), and higher UACR (mean: 586.5 vs. 272.7 mg/g) versus moderate-to-high risk group.
- 246 participants (42%) had KFRE risk ≥ 5% at baseline (higher UACR 693.1 vs. 330.3 mg/g; lower eGFR 36.5 vs. 63.2 ml/min per 1.73 m²; NT-proBNP 372.3 vs. 263.5 ng/l).
- Uncontrolled HTN present in 197 (33.6%) at baseline (higher SBP mean 142.7 vs. 129.2 mm Hg; more prior T2D 81.7% vs. 39.3%; higher NT-proBNP 367.1 vs. 280.4 ng/l; higher UACR 558.7 vs. 380.5 mg/g). 63 (11%) had treatment-resistant HTN (BP ≥ 140/90 mm Hg with ≥3 antihypertensive medications).
- 209 participants (35.7%) receiving diuretics at baseline (more prior T2D 83.3% vs. 63.7%).

### Change in UACR to Week 14
- Vicadrostat, with or without empagliflozin, reduced UACR across all subgroup analyses (adjusted mean reduction with 10 mg and 20 mg vicadrostat dose groups: 24%–55% vs. 37%–46% in the overall study).
- Consistent reductions in placebo-adjusted percentage UACR change across KDIGO risk groups (P_INTERACTION = 0.053 and 0.783, with and without empagliflozin, respectively).
- Consistent reductions across KFRE subgroups (P_INTERACTION = 0.238 and 0.910).
- Reductions in UACR with vicadrostat versus PBO_VICA observed in participants with and without uncontrolled HTN at baseline (P_INTERACTION = 0.030 and 0.617; P_INTERACTION with empagliflozin was not significant after correcting for multiple comparisons). Note: 0.030 marked as not significant after Bonferroni correction.
- Vicadrostat consistently reduced UACR irrespective of diuretic use at baseline (P_INTERACTION = 0.305 and 0.416, with and without empagliflozin).

### UACR Responder Analysis (≥ 30% UACR reduction from baseline to week 14)
- UACR response rate across all kidney risk subgroups showed improvement consistent with overall population, with greater proportion of participants meeting ≥30% UACR reduction in the 10 mg and 20 mg groups.
- UACR response (≥ 30% reduction) achieved in 43% to 82% of participants in vicadrostat 10 mg and 20 mg dose groups across all subgroups, with numerically greater number of responders with VicaEmpa versus vicadrostat alone.
- Response rates with VicaEmpa were higher in treatment groups taking diuretics at baseline (82% in the 10 mg and 67% in the 20 mg vicadrostat dose group) than in those not taking diuretics (65% and 55%). In contrast, UACR response rates with vicadrostat 10 mg and 20 mg doses without background empagliflozin tended to be higher in groups not on diuretics at baseline (56% and 54% in 10 mg and 20 mg groups without empagliflozin vs. 45% and 44% with diuretics).

### SBP
- Vicadrostat associated with consistent SBP reductions comparable across all subgroups, including participants with uncontrolled HTN at baseline. SBP reductions generally greater with background empagliflozin across all subgroups.
- SBP reductions observed in both KDIGO risk groups (P_INTERACTION = 0.300 and 0.287, with and without empagliflozin) and both KFRE risk groups (P_INTERACTION = 0.429 and 0.490).
- SBP change consistent across HTN status at baseline (P_INTERACTION = 0.517 and 0.092).
- SBP reductions observed irrespective of diuretic use (P_INTERACTION = 0.368 and 0.049; P_INTERACTION without empagliflozin not significant after correcting for multiple comparisons — 0.049 marked with asterisk).
- Overall SBP change (Figure 3, mm Hg, 95% CI): VICA 20mg/PBO_EMPA -4.94 (-9.44, -0.43); VICA 20mg/EMPA -5.78 (-9.37, -2.19); VICA 10mg/EMPA -5.34 (-10.04, -0.64).
- Investigator-reported hypotension occurred in only 1.4% of trial participants (n = 8).

### eGFR
- Overall, modest changes in eGFR from baseline to week 14 ranging from -1.64 to -4.75 ml/min per 1.73 m² in vicadrostat treatment groups, ranging from -0.17 to -8.85 across subgroups tested.
- Changes in eGFR consistent in both KDIGO risk groups (P_INTERACTION = 0.566 and 0.508) and both KFRE risk groups (P_INTERACTION = 0.401 and 0.782).
- Participants in very high KDIGO risk group and KFRE ≥5% group did not tend to have substantial eGFR decline (possibly because of lower eGFR at baseline) compared with moderate-to-high risk / KFRE <5%.
- In HTN subgroups, eGFR changes appeared dependent on vicadrostat dose with empagliflozin background; magnitude comparable in controlled vs. uncontrolled HTN (P_INTERACTION = 0.823 and 0.182).
- Changes in eGFR consistent irrespective of diuretic use (P_INTERACTION = 0.414 and 0.345).
- Overall eGFR change (Figure 4, ml/min per 1.73 m², 95% CI): VICA 10mg/PBO_EMPA -4.75 (-7.35, -2.16); VICA 20mg/PBO_EMPA -4.13 (-6.32, -1.94); VICA 20mg/EMPA -4.40 (-7.56, -1.24).

### Safety
- Overall safety for vicadrostat with and without empagliflozin previously reported. AE frequency (all types) similar between KDIGO risk class groups, KFRE risk groups, and irrespective of background diuretic use, with no clear patterns by vicadrostat dose or background empagliflozin. Any AE frequency comparable in participants with controlled versus uncontrolled HTN.

## Discussion / Key Points
- This post hoc analysis of a phase 2, double-blind, placebo-controlled study demonstrates vicadrostat was associated with clinically meaningful reductions in UACR in adults with CKD across a broad range of CKD stages, in presence or absence of uncontrolled HTN or concomitant diuretics at baseline. Responder rates (≥ 30% UACR decrease) numerically greater for vicadrostat 10 mg or 20 mg combined with empagliflozin than vicadrostat alone across all subgroups.
- Vicadrostat decreased SBP in people with CKD, comparable across subgroups. These reductions are for the vicadrostat treatment period (R2 as baseline) and are in addition to reductions during the run-in period where -2.2 mm Hg was observed for empagliflozin vs. matched placebo.
- Observed eGFR changes generally modest and consistent with expected glomerular hemodynamic effects of vicadrostat and empagliflozin. An initial eGFR dip (~3 to 5 ml/min per 1.73 m²) has been reported across SGLT2 inhibitor trials and similar initial reversible eGFR decline with RAAS inhibitors.
- Results support inclusion of a broad range of patients in a wider VicaEmpa phase 3 clinical trial program: phase 3 EASi-KIDNEY trial (NCT06531824) comparing VicaEmpa vs. empagliflozin, includes patients with and without albuminuria, with and without T2D, across broad range of kidney function (down to eGFR of 20 ml/min per 1.73 m²). This contrasts with studies restricted to CKD+HTN (BaxDuo-ARCTIC) or CKD+T2D (FIDELIO-DKD, FIGARO, CONFIDENCE, FLOW).
- Additional phase 3 trials: EASi-HF Preserved (NCT06424288), EASi-HF Reduced (NCT06935370), EASi-PROTKT (NCT07064473).
- The CONFIDENCE trial demonstrated that inhibition of both mineralocorticoid receptor (finerenone) and SGLT2 (empagliflozin) yields additive reductions in albuminuria compared with either agent alone.

## Limitations
- Phase 2 trial not powered for these post hoc subgroup analyses.
- Several subgroups closely associated, particularly KDIGO risk and KFRE (all 229 in moderate-to-high KDIGO risk group had KFRE risk < 5%; very high KDIGO risk group included all 244 of KFRE ≥5% group and 101 participants from KFRE < 5% group).
- Phase 2 trial not designed to assess BP; mean baseline SBP only modestly increased. Observed SBP changes smaller than dedicated aldosterone synthase inhibitor HTN trials (BaxHTN, Launch-HTN) and should not be directly compared.
- None of subgroup interaction terms remained significant after adjustment for multiple comparisons; raising possibility of type II error.
- Sample size precluded definitive assessment of link between aldosterone levels and outcomes.

## Conclusion
The effects of vicadrostat on albuminuria reduction, whether given alone or with empagliflozin, were generally consistent irrespective of CKD stage and kidney risk as scored by KDIGO or KFRE, uncontrolled HTN status, or use of diuretics at baseline. Vicadrostat meaningfully decreased SBP in people with CKD, with reductions comparable across all subgroups including uncontrolled HTN, whereas eGFR changes were generally modest and consistent with expected hydrodynamic effects.
