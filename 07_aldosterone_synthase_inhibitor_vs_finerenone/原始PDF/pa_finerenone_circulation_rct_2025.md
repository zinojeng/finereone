# Efficacy and Safety of Finerenone in Patients With Primary Aldosteronism: A Pilot Randomized Controlled Trial

*Circulation.* 2025;151(2):196-198. Research Letter. DOI: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.124.071452. PMID: 39804908.

## Design and population

- Design: pilot, randomized, controlled trial (open-label; head-to-head finerenone vs spironolactone).
- Eligibility: patients 18вҖ“70 years, diagnosed with primary aldosteronism (PA), not taking or on a stable regimen of antihypertensive agents other than a mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist for >4 weeks before screening, with seated office SBP вүҘ140 mm Hg or DBP вүҘ90 mm Hg.
- Randomization: finerenone or spironolactone **20 mg once daily**, titrated up to **40 mg/d** to achieve targeted BP.
- 24-hour ambulatory BP monitoring at baseline and final visit.
- Primary outcome: mean change in **daytime SBP** from baseline to **day 56** (8 weeks).
- Secondary outcomes: changes in daytime DBP, 24-hour BP, office BP, serum potassium; safety.

## Enrollment / flow

- 125 patients screened вҶ’ **60 randomized (30 per group)**.
- 1 patient assigned to spironolactone did not receive it вҶ’ analysis included **30 finerenone** and **29 spironolactone**.
- PA subtypes (unilateral / bilateral / indeterminate): finerenone 4 / 21 / 5; spironolactone 4 / 15 / 10.
- Baseline characteristics (age, sex, BP, antihypertensive drugs, serum potassium, plasma renin and aldosterone) comparable between groups.
- Average dose: **finerenone 21.8 mg/d**, **spironolactone 23.4 mg/d**.

## Key narrative findings

- Primary outcome: daytime SBP reduction not significantly different between groups (mean difference вҲ’2.1 mm Hg [95% CI вҲ’8.2 to 4.0]).
- Daytime DBP, 24-h SBP/DBP, office SBP/DBP all comparable between the 2 groups (all CIs cross 0).
- Serum potassium: finerenone group had a **smaller** increase than spironolactone (mean difference вҲ’0.3 mmol/L [95% CI вҲ’0.5 to вҲ’0.1]).

## Safety

- Adverse events in **6 (20.7%)** spironolactone patients: 4 impotence, 1 mastodynia, 1 hyperkalemia.
- **None** in the finerenone group.
- No patient discontinued treatment because of adverse events.
- Hyperkalemia: none with finerenone; 1 patient with spironolactone.

## Conclusion (verbatim)

"In conclusion, in patients with PA, finerenone was well tolerated and achieved BP reduction over 8 weeks of treatment. Finerenone is a potential [alternative...]" (larger RCTs needed).

## Note on biochemical response

This BP-focused pilot did NOT report PA-specific "complete biochemical response" (renin unsuppression + normokalemia) or "complete clinical response" per the PASO/PAMO consensus criteria. Biochemical data reported are within-group changes in upright PRC and PAC (see table) and the office BP control rate (66.7% finerenone vs 48.3% spironolactone). Both groups showed a rise in PRC (renin unsuppression) with no significant between-group difference.
