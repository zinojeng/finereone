## Core thesis
PA carries substantially higher CV risk than essential hypertension (EH), not explained by BP alone. **Residual CV risk** persists despite MR antagonism due to (1) incomplete aldosterone suppression and (2) non-genomic signaling (GPER/GPCR). Inflammation (macrophage- and T-cell-dependent pathways, NLRP3 inflammasome, IL-6 trans-signaling) is the central mediator of aldosterone-induced myocardial fibrosis and vascular dysfunction. Proposes **combination strategies**: aldosterone synthase inhibitors (ASIs) targeting aldosterone production upstream + MR antagonism ± anti-inflammatory / anti-non-genomic interventions.

## Key numbers (verbatim from review)

### Epidemiology / CV risk
- PA vs EH: **hazard ratio ≈ 1.9** for cardiovascular events; **excess ≈ 14% in CV events over 10 years**.
- CV-outcome meta-analysis population: **3,838 patients with PA vs 9,284 with essential hypertension**.
- PA prevalence in resistant hypertension: **≈ 20% or higher**.
- PA prevalence in hypertension with atrial fibrillation: **as high as 30%–42%**.

### Nonsteroidal MRA — Esaxerenone (most robust PA evidence among nsMRAs)
- Satoh et al.: seated **SBP/DBP decreased by 17.7 / 9.5 mmHg**.
- Serum potassium: mild rise **≈ 3.9 → 4.0 mmol/L**.
- **47.7%** achieved target BP **<140/90 mmHg**.
- Safety: only **≈ 4.5% (2 patients)** had mild–moderate hyperkalemia.
- Trial N: **n = 44** PA patients (esaxerenone); Fujimoto et al. **n = 87** PA patients.
- Positioned as "potential first-line alternative in selected populations" (Japanese prospective + real-world data).

### Nonsteroidal MRA — Finerenone (limited PA-specific evidence)
- Exploratory trial: **BP reduction comparable to spironolactone** but **better tolerability** and **milder increases in serum potassium**.
- One real-world study: **lower rate of complete clinical and biochemical remission** during finerenone treatment.
- No PA hard-outcome (CV endpoint) data.

### Aldosterone synthase inhibitors (ASIs)
- **Baxdrostat** phase III: **SBP ≈ −15.7 mmHg vs −5.8 mmHg placebo**; SPARK study "ameliorated hypokalemia"; SPARK II **n = 15**.
- **Lorundrostat** phase II: **SBP ≈ −13.2 mmHg vs −4.1 mmHg placebo**.
- **No hard CV outcome data in PA** for ASIs; dedicated PA outcome trials described as ongoing (data in resistant hypertension).

## Relevance to nsMRA / ASI-vs-finerenone project
- Supports mechanistic rationale for **ASI + MRA combination** in PA (upstream aldosterone suppression complementing MR blockade).
- Supports **residual CV risk** framing and the non-genomic (GPER) escape argument for why finerenone/spironolactone alone may be insufficient.
- Frames finerenone as tolerability-favorable but with weaker PA remission signal vs esaxerenone.

## changes_conclusions: NO
- Narrative review; presents **no new PA hard-outcome trial**. All figures are restatements of prior primary sources (esaxerenone Satoh/Fujimoto, baxdrostat/lorundrostat RH trials, PA-vs-EH meta-analysis HR≈1.9). No RCT with CV outcomes for finerenone/nsMRA/ASI in PA. Does not contradict existing review numbers; reinforces mechanistic/residual-risk narrative. No first-in-class hard outcome to overturn prior conclusions.
