# Esaxerenone (CS-3150) in Hypertensive Patients with Primary Aldosteronism

**Design:** Multicenter, open-label, single-arm, 12-week phase 3 study. Japan, Oct 2016–Jul 2017. Trial ID JapicCTI-163349. No randomization, no placebo/comparator arm.

**Population:** Japanese adults (≥20 yr) with hypertension + primary aldosteronism (PA) diagnosed within prior 5 years. Screening: PAC >120 pg/mL and ARR >200, plus ≥1 positive confirmatory test (captopril, saline challenge, furosemide-standing, or oral salt loading per Japanese Endocrine Society guidelines). Baseline sitting SBP ≥140 and <180 mmHg, DBP ≥90 and <110 mmHg. Only permitted concomitant antihypertensives: a single CCB (excluding cilnidipine, efonidipine, azelnidipine, benidipine) or α-blocker at stable dose.

**Intervention:** Esaxerenone initiated 2.5 mg/day, escalated to 5 mg/day at week 2 or 4 if SBP/DBP ≥120/≥80 mmHg, serum K <5.1 mEq/L, and eGFR decrease from baseline <30%. 12-week treatment + 1-week follow-up.

**Exclusions relevant to class:** serum K <3.0 or ≥5.1 mEq/L, eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m². Prohibited: diuretics, renin inhibitors, other antihypertensives.

## Baseline (N=44; FAS=SAS=44)
- Mean age 49.6 ± 9.68 years; 57% female.
- Bilateral adrenal lesions + IHA (idiopathic hyperaldosteronism): 79.5%.
- Prior antihypertensive therapy: 86.4%; most common CCBs (72.7%). Concomitant CCB during study: 65.9%.
- 41/44 completed; 3 withdrew (2 adverse events, 1 withdrew consent).
- Dose escalated to 5 mg/day in 41/44 (93%); 37 (90% of escalated) titrated at week 2. Three remained at 2.5 mg/day (2 due to hypotensive effect, 1 discontinued before escalation visit).

## Efficacy (primary: change in sitting SBP/DBP baseline → end of treatment)
- Sitting SBP: baseline 154.0 ± 9.8 mmHg → end of treatment 136.4 ± 13.6 mmHg.
- Sitting DBP: baseline 100.0 ± 5.9 mmHg → end of treatment 90.5 ± 8.6 mmHg.
- **Change SBP: −17.7 mmHg (95% CI −20.6, −14.7); DBP: −9.5 mmHg (95% CI −11.7, −7.3); both p < 0.0001.**
- Significant BP reductions evident from week 2; continued to decrease through week 8; stable to week 12.

### By final dose
- 2.5 mg/day (n=3): SBP −20.7, DBP −15.3 mmHg.
- 5 mg/day (n=41): SBP −17.4, DBP −9.1 mmHg.

### BP target achievement (end of treatment)
- Target 1 (<140/90 mmHg, all patients): 47.7% (95% CI 32.5, 63.3).
- Target 2 (<130/80 if T2DM; <140/90 if no DM): 40.9% (95% CI 26.3, 56.8).

### Subgroups (SBP reduction significantly greater)
- Female vs male: p = 0.0156.
- No concomitant antihypertensive vs concomitant: p = 0.0433.
- Monotherapy subgroup (n=14): SBP −22.0, DBP −11.9 mmHg.
- With concomitant antihypertensives: SBP −15.6, DBP −8.4 mmHg.
- PRA ≥1 ng/mL/h at week 12 (n=17): SBP −20.0, DBP −10.9 mmHg.
- PRA <1 ng/mL/h at week 12 (n=24): SBP −16.5, DBP −8.7 mmHg.

## Pharmacodynamics (baseline → week 12)
- PAC increased by +83.9 pg/mL; PRA increased by +0.60 ng/mL/h. Both percent changes statistically significant (p < 0.001). (Consistent with MR blockade releasing feedback suppression of renin/aldosterone.)

## Safety
- Treatment-emergent AEs: 61.4% (27/44); most common dizziness 9.1%.
- Drug-related AEs: 25.0% (11/44).
- **Increased serum K: 4.5% (n=2). Decreased eGFR: 4.5% (n=2).**
- **No gynecomastia or breast pain.** One female reported menorrhagia (on 5 mg/day).
- One serious AE (bronchitis, moderate, unrelated to drug). Two discontinued for AEs (1 bronchitis, 1 increased serum K). No deaths.
- Serum K: baseline 4.01 ± 0.331 mEq/L → week 2 4.34 ± 0.491 mEq/L; did not continue rising after dose titration.
- One patient had serum K ≥6.0 mEq/L at 2 weeks (on 2.5 mg/day) → drug stopped, K fell to 5.2 mEq/L 5 days later. Only 1 patient reached K ≥5.5 mEq/L overall.
- Baseline hypokalemia (K <3.5 mEq/L): 3 patients (6.8%) despite K supplements; 2 of 3 normalized to ≥3.5 by week 12.
- 6 patients on concomitant K supplements at baseline — no hyperkalemia observed; similar K trajectory with/without supplements.
- eGFR decreased 8.1 mL/min/1.73m² at week 8 vs baseline, maintained to week 12 (interpreted as hemodynamic/hyperfiltration correction, not organic damage).

## Author framing / comparators (narrative, no head-to-head)
- SBP/DBP reductions (−17.7/−9.5) described as "not less than" eplerenone 100–300 mg/day and smaller than spironolactone 75–225 mg/day in a prior study (both used above approved doses; different design/population).
- Monotherapy subgroup (−22.0/−11.9) "almost comparable" to spironolactone 150 mg/day at week 8 in prior study.
- Authors state no definite conclusions can be drawn vs existing MRAs.

## Limitations
Short treatment duration, small sample, no randomization, no placebo/comparator.

## Relevance to nsMRA-in-PA class benchmark
First prospective study of a nonsteroidal MR blocker (esaxerenone) specifically in PA. Establishes nsMRA BP-lowering (−17.7/−9.5 mmHg) and safety (no gynecomastia; hyperkalemia within acceptable range; K supplements permissible) benchmark in PA — the class-comparison anchor for finerenone in PA.

_Note: no complete/partial biochemical or clinical response (PASO/PA-consensus) endpoints were reported in this study; it used BP-target achievement (47.7% / 40.9%) and biochemical PAC/PRA changes instead._
