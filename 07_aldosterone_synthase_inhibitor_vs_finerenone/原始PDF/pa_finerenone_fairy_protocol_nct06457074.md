## Background / Rationale (brief summary)
Using **spironolactone as the active control**, the trial assesses the efficacy and safety of
**finerenone** (non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist) in patients with primary
aldosteronism (PA).

## Intervention
- Two arms, randomized: **Finerenone** (experimental) vs **Spironolactone** (active comparator).
- Both drugs **started at 20 mg/day**; **dose titrated every 4 weeks** to achieve target
  **mean office BP < 140/90 mmHg**.
- **Treatment duration: 12 weeks.**

## Outcomes
**Primary outcome (12 weeks):**
- Change from baseline in **24-hour systolic BP (SBP)** measured by 24-h ambulatory BP monitoring
  (ABPM), finerenone vs spironolactone.

**Secondary outcomes (12 weeks):**
- Change from baseline in 24-hour DBP (ABPM)
- Change from baseline in daytime SBP and daytime DBP (ABPM)
- Change from baseline in nighttime SBP and nighttime DBP (ABPM)
- **Blood pressure control rate** at end of study (% with mean seated office BP < 140/90 mmHg)
- Change from baseline in **serum potassium**
- **Hypokalemia control rate** (% of baseline-hypokalemic patients reaching serum K⁺ > 3.5 mmol/L at end)
- Change from baseline in **plasma renin concentration**

**Other / exploratory outcomes (12 weeks):**
- Change in **eGFR** from baseline (mL/min/1.73 m²)
- Change from baseline in **urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)**
- Proportion with UACR change from baseline > 30 (mg/g Cr)
- Proportion of subjects with AEs; proportion with SAEs and AEs leading to study-drug discontinuation

## Key Eligibility
**Inclusion:**
1. Age 18–75, male or female.
2. Confirmed PA (positive screening + ≥1 positive confirmatory test). Screening positive =
   ARR ≥ 20 (pg/ml)/(µIU/ml) or ARR ≥ 30 (ng/dL)/(ng/ml/hr). Confirmatory: PAC post captopril
   challenge test (CCT) ≥ 110 pg/ml, or PAC post seated saline infusion test (SSIT) ≥ 80 pg/ml.
   (ARR ≥ 10 (pg/ml)/(µIU/ml) or ≥ 15 (ng/dL)/(ng/ml/hr) acceptable if hypokalemia [K⁺ < 3.5 mmol/L]
   or adrenal nodule > 1 cm.)
3. Untreated or on stable regimen (only alpha-blockers and CCBs) ≥ 4 weeks before screening.
4. Mean seated office SBP ≥ 140 or DBP ≥ 90 mmHg.
5. Able/willing to give informed consent.

**Selected exclusions:** planned PA subtyping (AVS/PET-CT) or surgery within 3 months; office
SBP ≥ 180 or DBP ≥ 110 mmHg before randomization; night-shift workers; BMI ≥ 30 kg/m²;
uncontrolled diabetes (FBG ≥ 13.3 mmol/L); other secondary hypertension (except subclinical
Cushing); NYHA III/IV heart failure or transaminases > 2× ULN; recent (6 mo) major cardiac
surgery/PCI/stroke/ACS/HF hospitalization; **hyperkalemia serum K⁺ > 5.0 mmol/L** without K
supplementation; inter-arm SBP difference > 20 mmHg; pregnancy; certain concomitant drugs.

## Notes on identification (avoid confusion with sibling trials)
- **NCT06457074 = FAIRY** (Chongqing Medical Univ, Qifu Li; open-label, N=306) — this is the seed.
- Distinct trials with similar aims: NCT06164379 (finerenone vs spironolactone, double-blind
  non-inferiority); NCT06381323 (Li P et al., single-arm finerenone, published in *Hypertension* 2026,
  DOI 10.1161/HYPERTENSIONAHA.125.26048) — **separate publications, not FAIRY.**
