# SPARK — Baxdrostat in Confirmed Primary Aldosteronism (Phase 2)

## Source note
- **Target PMID 40658651** = NEJM 2025;393(5):515-518 research **Letter** "Phase 2a Study of Baxdrostat in Primary Aldosteronism" (DOI 10.1056/NEJMc2508629). NEJM full text is paywalled; NEJM does not provide a machine-readable abstract for this letter.
- Data below are transcribed **verbatim** from the peer-reviewed conference abstract of the **same SPARK Phase 2 trial** (ENDO 2025, OR16-08; J Endocr Soc bvaf149.798; PMC12545722, open access). Sponsor: AstraZeneca.

## Design
- SPARK: Phase 2, **open-label**, multicenter trial of the aldosterone synthase inhibitor (ASI) **baxdrostat** in patients with **confirmed primary aldosteronism (PA)**.
- Eligibility: age ≥18; diagnosis of PA; willingness to discontinue MRA up to 4 weeks before baxdrostat; and SBP rise ≥20 mmHg or SBP ≥160 mmHg after MRA discontinuation, OR SBP ≥150 mmHg for newly diagnosed PA not on MRA in past 12 weeks.
- **Part 1 (12 weeks):** baxdrostat 2 mg once daily, up-titrated to 4 mg (W4) or 8 mg (W8) if no significant hypotension risk; down-titratable.
- **Part 2:** continue baxdrostat to W72 at investigators' discretion.
- **Primary endpoints:** safety, tolerability, and change from baseline in mean seated SBP at W12.

## Population (N)
- **15 patients enrolled and completed Part 1; 14 continued to Part 2.**
- Median (range) age **54 (32, 75)** years; **67% male**; **47% White**; median BMI **32.0 (24.4, 41.4)** kg/m².
- Baseline plasma renin activity (PRA) **<1 ng/mL/h in 12/15 (80%)**.
- Baseline **hypokalemia (K <3.5 mmol/L) in 8/15 (53%)**.
- Mean (range) baseline **SBP 151.5 (130, 174) mmHg**; **DBP 90.3 (74, 103) mmHg**.

## Key efficacy numbers (verbatim)

### SBP change from baseline at W12 (by dose)
- 2 mg (n=2): **–29.5 (–47, –12) mmHg**
- 4 mg (n=5): **–24.4 (–31, –17) mmHg**
- 8 mg (n=7): **–23.9 (–47, –8) mmHg**

### DBP change from baseline at W12 (by dose)
- 2 mg: **–8.0 (–14, –2) mmHg**
- 4 mg: **–10.4 (–20, 0) mmHg**
- 8 mg: **–11.6 (–25, 7) mmHg**

### BP control at W12
- **11/15 (73%)** achieved BP **<140/90 mmHg**
- **6/15 (40%)** achieved BP **<130/80 mmHg**
- Reduced BP persisted in Part 2.

### Aldosterone / renin / potassium (biochemical response — the ASI "synthesis-end" signal)
- Mean (SD) **24-h urine aldosterone excretion: 30.3 (17.5) µg/day at baseline → 3.2 (2.7) µg/day at W12** (marked suppression of aldosterone production).
- **7/15 (47%)** achieved **unsuppressed PRA (≥1.0 ng/mL/h)** at W12 (renin de-suppression = biochemical control of PA).
- Mean (SD) serum **potassium 3.9 (0.6) mEq/L at baseline → 4.7 (0.5) mEq/L at W12**.

## Safety
- **No deaths.**
- Part 1 (W4–W12): **9/15 (60%)** had a treatment-emergent adverse event (TEAE), all mild/moderate; **hyperkalemia in 1/15 (7%)**; **no serious TEAEs**.
- Part 2: **14/14 (100%)** had a TEAE (most mild/moderate); **hyperkalemia in 5/14 (36%)**; **one serious TEAE of hyperkalemia**.

## Follow-up
- Part 1 = 12 weeks (primary). Part 2 extension to **W72**.

## Conclusion (authors)
Baxdrostat, an ASI, was well tolerated and led to reduced BP and unsuppressed renin in patients with PA. Inhibiting aldosterone synthase may be an alternative to MRAs for treating PA.

## Notes for §8 (receptor blockade vs synthesis inhibition)
- This is **first-hand data in confirmed PA** (not HTN/CKD extrapolation) — the pivotal proof-of-concept that blocking aldosterone at the **synthesis end (CYP11B2)** rather than the **receptor (MRA)** produces BP lowering PLUS true biochemical PA control (urine aldosterone 30.3→3.2 µg/day; renin de-suppression 47%).
- Contrast with finerenone/spironolactone (receptor blockade): MRAs leave aldosterone elevated and only block downstream MR signaling, whereas baxdrostat lowers aldosterone production itself.
- Trade-off signal: hyperkalemia rises with longer exposure/uptitration (7% Part 1 → 36% Part 2, one serious) — the on-target consequence of shutting off aldosterone.
- Caveats for §8 weighting: **small (N=15), open-label, single-arm, no active comparator (no head-to-head vs MRA/finerenone), short primary window (12 wk)**. Hypothesis-generating pivotal Phase 2; Phase 3 (BaxPA) ongoing.
