# Outcomes after medical treatment for primary aldosteronism: an international consensus and analysis of treatment response in an international cohort (PAMO)

> NOTE: According to PubMed, full text is NOT open access (Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology, subscription; no PMC record; Sci-Hub retrieval failed). Content below is the verbatim structured abstract from PubMed / Europe PMC. Article type: Consensus Statement / Journal Article. NIH-supported (Research Support, N.I.H., Extramural).

## Background
Primary aldosteronism can be treated medically but there is no standardised method to evaluate treatment outcomes. We aimed to develop criteria for assessing the outcomes of targeted medical treatment of primary aldosteronism, analyse outcomes across an international cohort, and identify factors associated with a complete treatment response.

## Methods
An international panel of **31 primary aldosteronism experts** used the **Delphi method** to reach consensus on the definition of complete, partial, or absent biochemical and clinical outcomes of medical treatment of primary aldosteronism. Clinical data at **baseline and 6-12 months post-treatment** were collected from patients with primary aldosteronism who started targeted medical treatment **between 2016 and 2021 at 28 participating centres**.

## Findings
Consensus was reached for defining complete, partial, or absent biochemical or clinical response.

- Total **N = 1258 patients**; mean age **52 years (SD 11·5)**; **610 (48·5%) female**, **648 (51·5%) male**.
- **1057 (84·0%)** had biochemical outcome data; of these, **559 (52·9%) had a complete biochemical response**.
- Median daily dose of spironolactone significantly higher in complete biochemical responders vs non-responders: **40 mg (IQR 25-50) vs 25 mg (20-50); p=0·011**.
- Of **1248 patients with clinical outcome data**, **228 (18·3%) had a complete clinical response** whereas **227 (18·2%) had an absent response**.
- Complete clinical responders were more likely than partial/absent responders to be:
  - **women: OR 2·099, 95% CI 1·485-2·968; p<0·001**
  - require **lower doses of antihypertensive drugs at baseline: OR 0·687, 95% CI 0·603-0·782; p<0·001**
  - **less likely to have microalbuminuria or left ventricular hypertrophy: OR 0·584, 95% CI 0·391-0·873; p=0·009**

## Interpretation
The **Primary Aldosteronism Medical Treatment Outcome (PAMO) criteria** represent an internationally developed outcome standard that can guide clinical practice and research into primary aldosteronism. Efforts to optimise treatment intensity and minimise factors associated with an absent treatment response are needed to improve patient outcomes.

## Relevance to review (ASI vs finerenone / PA medical treatment)
- Large real-world benchmark for the adequacy of conventional MRA-based medical therapy in PA: only ~half achieve complete biochemical response and <20% achieve complete clinical response, motivating better agents (aldosterone synthase inhibitors, nonsteroidal MRAs such as finerenone/esaxerenone) and higher/optimised dosing.
