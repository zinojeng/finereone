# Cardiovascular events and all-cause mortality in surgically or medically treated primary aldosteronism: A Meta-analysis

Journal of the Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System, January-March 2021: 1–8. © The Author(s) 2021. CC BY-NC 4.0.

## Abstract

**Objectives:** To compare the effect of surgical or medical treatment on the risk of cardiovascular diseases (CVD) and all-cause mortality in patients with established primary aldosteronism (PA).

**Methods:** We searched PUBMED, MEDLINE and Cochrane Library for the meta-analysis. We included patients who were diagnosed with PA following guideline-supported protocols and received surgery or mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA)-based medical treatment, and age-sex matched patients with treated essential hypertension (EH). Primary endpoints were CVD incidence and all-cause mortality.

**Results:** Compared with EH, patients with treated PA had a higher risk of CVD [odds ratio (OR) 1.79; 95% confidence interval (CI) 1.39–2.31]. This elevated risk was only observed in patients with medically treated PA [OR 2.11; 95% CI 1.88–2.38] but not in those with surgically treated PA. The risk of all-cause mortality was significantly lower in patients with treated PA [OR 0.86; 95% CI 0.77–0.95] compared to EH. The reduced risk was only observed in patients with surgically treated PA [OR 0.47; 95% CI 0.34–0.66], but not in those with medically treated PA.

**Conclusions:** Patients with medically treated PA have a higher risk of CVD compared to patients with EH. Surgical treatment of PA reduces the risk of CVD and all-cause mortality in patients with PA.

**Keywords:** Primary aldosteronism, cardiovascular diseases, all-cause mortality, adrenal surgery, mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist

Date received: 4 February 2021; accepted: 26 February 2021

## Introduction

Primary aldosteronism (PA), the most common form of secondary hypertension, is a disease characterised by autonomous aldosterone secretion from adrenocortical lesions and feedback inhibition of the renin-angiotensin system. The aldosterone excess leads to a higher risk of cardiovascular damage and worse prognosis compared to patients with essential hypertension (EH). Targeted treatment with lifelong mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA) or unilateral adrenalectomy are the expert consensus recommendations for PA management. Considering that aldosterone blockade and ameliorated hypertension are often achieved after surgery or MRA treatment, it was thought that cardiovascular outcomes may also be improved by these treatments.

Whether the risk of CVD and all-cause mortality could be reduced by surgery or MRA remains controversial. It was reported that compared to patients with matched EH, the risk of CVD and all-cause mortality in surgically treated PA is significantly decreased; however, this effect was not observed in another population. On the other hand, the risk of CVD and all-cause mortality in medically treated PA was shown to be higher than patients with EH, but this difference was not observed in other studies. The authors included all longitudinal studies comparing the risk of CVD and mortality in patients with EH and surgically or medically treated PA in a meta-analysis.

## Methods

### Search strategy and selection criteria

Searched PUBMED, MEDLINE and Cochrane Library using a combination of terms: (hyperaldosteronism) OR (Primary hyperaldosteronism) OR (Primary aldosteronism) OR (Aldosteronism) OR (Conn Syndrom) and ((myocardial infarction) OR (atrial fibrillation) OR (heart failure) OR (percutaneous transluminal coronary angioplasty) OR (stroke) OR (leg pain on exertion) OR (claudication) OR (mortality)) up to July 31, 2020, with no start date restriction. Both prospective and retrospective studies included. Inclusion criteria: patients clearly diagnosed as PA receiving surgery or MRA; age-sex matched treated EH as control; all subjects followed up for more than 5 years.

Exclusion criteria: data repeatedly published (only most recent used); absence of EH patients; PA diagnosis not based on US Endocrine Society guidelines or Japan Endocrine Society guidelines; absence of follow-up data.

Primary outcomes: CVD incidence and all-cause mortality. CVD incidence defined as development of coronary artery disease (including myocardial infarction and stable or unstable angina requiring cardiac revascularisation), atrial fibrillation, heart failure, stroke (ischaemic or haemorrhagic), or death due to CVD. All-cause mortality was any cause of death. Subgroup analyses compared surgically treated PA to EH, medically treated PA to EH, and surgically treated PA to medically treated PA.

### Data extraction and quality assessment

Two unmasked independent reviewers (Y.J. and J.B.H) separately extracted basic information. Study quality assessed by the Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS); a point of less than 5 indicated relatively poor quality. Reported per PRISMA checklist.

### Data analysis and synthesis

Review Manager (RevMan, version 5.3) used to calculate summary estimates with 95% CI. Statistical heterogeneity identified using Cochran Q, with I² <25% mild, 25%–75% moderate, >75% extensive inconsistency. Random effects model with generic inverse-variance weighting used if I² >50%, otherwise Mantel-Haenszel fixed-effect model. Publication bias examined by funnel plots and Egger's tests. Stata 12.0 used for analysis; Open-meta analyst used to merge single variables with random effect model.

## Results

### Characteristics of included studies

Identified 1480 publications; of 72 full-text articles assessed, 35 excluded for lack of follow-up data, 17 for duplicate reports, 7 for lack of appropriate control group, 5 because PA diagnosis not in agreement with guidelines. Eight studies included in the final analysis. Kunzel et al. did not recruit EH subjects but compared surgically and medically treated PA. Wu et al. and Chen et al. were based on the same TAIPAI cohort but with different endpoints. Kunzel et al. and Chen et al. were only used for subgroup analyses; the other six studies were used for whole and subgroup analyses. All studies had NOS score of 5 or more; no publication bias found.

### CVD in treated PA versus EH

Six studies including 3905 patients with PA and 53,733 patients with EH. Risk of CVD in treated PA significantly higher (OR 1.79; 95% CI 1.39–2.31; p<0.001) than matched EH. In subgroup analyses, risk of CVD in medically treated PA was 2-fold higher than matched EH (OR 2.11; 95% CI 1.88–2.38; p<0.001), while no statistically significant difference between surgically treated PA and EH (OR 1.51; 95% CI 0.66–3.42; p=0.33). Patients with medically treated PA showed a 1.75-fold higher risk of CVD than surgically treated PA patients (OR 1.75; 95% CI 1.07–2.86; p=0.33).

### All-cause mortality in treated PA versus EH

Four studies including 3708 patients with PA and 54,143 patients with EH. Compared to matched EH, risk of all-cause mortality in treated PA significantly lower (OR 0.86; 95% CI 0.77–0.95; p=0.004). Lower risk of all-cause mortality only observed in surgically treated PA vs EH (OR 0.47; 95% CI 0.34–0.66; p<0.001). No significant difference between medically treated PA and EH (OR 0.95; 95% CI 0.84–1.06; p=0.34). Risk of all-cause mortality for medically treated PA was 3-fold higher than surgically treated PA (OR 2.89; 95% CI 2.06–4.05; p<0.001).

## Discussion

In this meta-analysis of eight studies evaluating CVD and all-cause mortality following targeted treatment of PA, a significantly higher risk of CVD was found in treated PA than matched EH. In subgroup analyses, CVD risk was significantly higher in medically treated PA compared to either EH or surgically treated PA. CVD risk in surgically treated PA was similar to EH. The risk of overall mortality was lower in treated PA compared with EH; the decreased mortality risk was observed in surgically treated PA when compared with EH and medically treated PA, while mortality risk in medically treated PA was similar to EH.

Monticone et al. conducted a systematic review and meta-analysis of 31 studies (about 95% cross-sectional without treatment information) comparing CVD risk between untreated PA and EH, showing increased risks of stroke (OR 2.58; 95% CI 1.93–3.45), coronary artery disease (OR 1.77; 95% CI 1.10–2.83), atrial fibrillation (OR 3.52; 95% CI 2.06–5.99) and heart failure (OR 2.05; 95% CI 1.11–3.78).

The risk of all-cause mortality decreased by 50% in surgically treated PA compared to EH, suggesting surgical treatment confers significant cardiovascular protection. Pathophysiological changes of PA (cardiac fibrosis, oxidative stress, chronic inflammation) could be alleviated by targeted treatment. Frustaci et al. found primary aldosteronism-associated cardiomyopathy (PACM) to be a reversible entity, though myocardial fibrosis remained unchanged even after adrenalectomy (some myocardial damage irreversible). The finding that CVD risk in surgically treated PA is not lower than EH may be caused by variable rate of complete clinical success after surgery (17%–62%, PASO), concurrent EH and metabolic diseases persisting after surgical cure, and irreversible target organ damage.

Regarding medically treated PA: Hundemer et al. studied 602 MRA-treated patients and 41,853 EH; after 7-year follow-up, MRA-treated patients showed significantly higher risks of incident CVD and mortality than EH. Cardiovascular protection of medical therapy may be limited by inadequate MRA dosing; Hundemer found 67% of medically treated patients had suppressed plasma renin activity (<1 g/l per h) at least 1 month after MRA initiation, indicating insufficient mineralocorticoid receptor blockade. Compliance is another factor; an Italian study indicated MRA therapy discontinued in 28% of PA patients due to adverse effects.

### Limitations

Small number of studies evaluating mortality in PA; all-cause mortality data should be interpreted with caution. Could not analyse individual CVD endpoints due to lack of access to original data. Data on dose and adequacy of MRA treatment were limited. Older patients with more comorbidities more likely given medical treatment, contributing to potential bias. Patients receiving medical treatment may have had unilateral PA not accurately diagnosed or not qualifying for surgery.

## Conclusion

Patients with surgically treated PA have a decreased risk of CVD compared to medically treated PA and a lower risk of all-cause mortality than EH. Medical treatment of PA, associated with higher risk of CVD compared to EH, requires further evaluation of adequacy of MRA dosing. The study supports early diagnosis and accurate subtyping of PA patients who are candidates for curative surgical adrenalectomy, and use of treatment targets such as renin with stringent cardiovascular risk management in medically treated PA.
