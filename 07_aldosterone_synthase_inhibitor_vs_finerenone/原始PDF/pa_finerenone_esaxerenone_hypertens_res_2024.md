# Effects of esaxerenone on BP, UACR, NT-proBNP, and QOL in primary aldosteronism

## Context / relevance
Esaxerenone is a nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (nsMRA). This
single-arm exploratory study in primary aldosteronism (PA) patients addresses the
question of BP-independent organ protection and patient-centered outcomes for the
nsMRA class: UACR and NT-proBNP fell independently of BP reduction, and QOL improved.

## Design (from abstract)
- Single-arm, before/after study in PA patients.
- N = 25 PA patients.
- Parameters measured before, and at 3 and 6 months after esaxerenone treatment.
- Endpoints: BP (SBP/DBP), eGFR, serum potassium, active renin concentration (ARC),
  urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR), NT-proBNP, and QOL (SF-36).

## Key findings (qualitative, from abstract — exact numeric deltas NOT in abstract)
- Systolic and diastolic BP decreased after treatment.
- eGFR decreased after treatment.
- Serum potassium increased after treatment.
- Active renin concentration (ARC) increased after treatment.
- UACR significantly reduced at both 3 and 6 months.
- NT-proBNP significantly decreased at 6 months (not significant at 3 months).
- Reductions in BP and UACR at 3 months were independent of estimated daily salt intake.
- The UACR-lowering and NT-proBNP-lowering effects were independent of BP reduction.
- Responders (SBP decrease > 10 mmHg at 6 months vs pretreatment) had higher
  pretreatment NT-proBNP and similar UACR before/after vs nonresponders.
- QOL (mental, physical, social) improved at 6 months vs healthy controls, increasing over time.
- Safety: no patient discontinued due to severe hyperkalemia or renal dysfunction.

## Conclusion
Esaxerenone is described as a safe and effective MRA for PA, with benefits in
hypertension, albuminuria, NT-proBNP, and QOL; BP, UACR, and NT-proBNP reductions
were independent of dietary salt intake in mild PA patients.
