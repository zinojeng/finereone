# Effects of MR antagonists on sex hormones and body composition in primary aldosteronism

## Study design
- Randomized, prospective, open-label comparison of **spironolactone (SPL, steroidal MRA)** vs **esaxerenone (ESX, nonsteroidal MRA)** in patients with primary aldosteronism (PA) without severe renal dysfunction (eGFR ≥30).
- Single center: Tokyo Women's Medical University, patients enrolled May 2019 – March 2021.
- Randomized 1:1, stratified by sex.
- SPL: started 25 mg/day, up-titrated to 50 mg/day (max 100 mg) at week 2 or 4 if SBP/DBP ≥120/80 mmHg, K <5.1 mEq/L, eGFR decrease <30%.
- ESX: started 1.25 mg/day, up-titrated to 5 mg/day similarly.
- Ca antagonists (amlodipine/nifedipine) allowed; other antihypertensives excluded.
- Assessments before and after **3-month and 6-month** treatment.
- Excluded: eGFR <30, hypopituitarism, hypogonadism, secondary HTN other than PA, essential HTN.

## Sample
- Enrolled 74; 37 assigned SPL, 37 assigned ESX.
- 1 withdrew + 4 lost to follow-up (SPL); 4 lost (ESX).
- **Analyzed: SPL n=33 (12 M / 21 F), ESX n=32 (14 M / 18 F).**
- Postmenopausal females: SPL n=13, ESX n=11.
- Baseline: age SPL 55±13, ESX 54±12; no significant baseline differences except female serum fT lower in SPL.

## Power calculation
- To detect 2.5 pg/mL between-group difference in fT change in males, power 80%, SD 2.0 pg/mL → 23 patients needed; accounting for ~10% dropout, 26 assigned males required.

## KEY RESULTS

### Blood pressure (all subjects, Table 2, mean±SEM)
- **SPL SBP**: 136.4±2.8 → 126.8±2.1 (3mo, P<0.0005) → 125.8±1.7 mmHg (6mo, P<0.0001)
- **ESX SBP**: 134.0±2.4 → 131.4±2.7 (3mo, ns) → 126.3±2.6 mmHg (6mo, P<0.005)
- **SPL DBP**: 82.3±2.2 → 76.5±1.8 (3mo, P<0.001) → 78.4±1.9 mmHg (6mo, P<0.05)
- **ESX DBP**: 83.4±1.7 → 77.8±2.8 (3mo, ns) → 78.6±2.2 mmHg (6mo, P<0.05)
- Decrease in SBP at 3 months significantly greater in SPL than ESX (Fig 1A, *P<0.05); not significant at 6 months. DBP changes not different between groups.

### Serum potassium (all subjects, mEq/L)
- **SPL**: 4.0±0.1 → 4.4±0.1 (3mo, P<0.0001) → 4.5±0.1 (6mo, P<0.0001)
- **ESX**: 4.0±0.1 → 4.1±0.1 (3mo, ns) → 4.2±0.2 (6mo, P<0.0001)
- Serum K increase significantly greater in SPL than ESX at both 3 and 6 months (Fig 1C, K significantly higher in SPL, b<0.05 vs SPL).
- **No patient in either group reached serum K ≥6.0 mEq/L.**

### Free testosterone (fT, pg/mL)
- All subjects (Table 2): **SPL** 3.1±0.6 → 4.5±0.9 (3mo, P<0.05 vs 0mo) → 4.5±1.0 (6mo, P<0.05); **ESX** 4.1±0.8 → 4.0±0.7 (3mo) → 4.2±0.8 (6mo, b<0.05 vs SPL).
- **Males (Table 3)**: SPL 7.2±0.9 → 10.5±1.2 (3mo, P<0.05) → 10.7±1.4 (6mo, P<0.05); ESX 8.1±1.1 → 7.6±1.0 (3mo, b vs SPL) → 8.2±1.2 (6mo, b vs SPL). Change in fT significantly greater in SPL than ESX (Fig 3A, *P<0.05).
- **Females (Table 4)**: SPL 0.8±0.1 → 1.0±0.1 (3mo) → 1.0±0.1 (6mo); ESX 1.0±0.1 (b vs SPL). fT significantly increased at 3 & 6 mo in SPL, no change in ESX; between-group ns in females.
- Postmenopausal females: no significant fT change in either group.
- fT significantly increased at 3 and 6 mo in SPL (both sexes) but NOT in ESX.

### Other sex hormones (all groups)
- **Estradiol, progesterone, LH, FSH**: no significant changes and no significant between-group differences.

### RAAS (all subjects)
- **PAC (pg/mL)**: SPL 240.0±15.2 → 348.7±23.9 (3mo, P<0.0001) → 351.5±25.7 (6mo, P<0.0001); ESX 282.2±21.9 → 394.1±31.4 (3mo, P<0.0005) → 382.2±30.2 (6mo, P<0.005). PAC increases similar between groups (Fig 2A, ns).
- **PRA (ng/mL/h)**: SPL 0.9±0.2 → 2.2±0.3 (3mo, P<0.0001) → 2.2±0.3 (6mo, P<0.0001); ESX 0.9±0.1 → 1.5±0.3 (3mo, P<0.05) → 1.7±0.3 (6mo, P<0.01). PRA increase significantly greater in SPL than ESX (Fig 2B, *P<0.05).

### Body composition
- **Body fat percentage** and **muscle mass percentage**: changes NOT significantly different between SPL and ESX in any subgroup. (Body fat tended to increase, muscle mass tended to decrease, comparable between groups.)
  - All subjects body fat %: SPL 31.0±1.4 → 31.7±1.5 → 32.1±1.4 (6mo, a<0.05); ESX 30.7±1.2 → 31.3±1.1 → 31.7±1.2.
  - All subjects muscle mass %: SPL 37.4±0.8 → 37.2±0.9 → 36.9±0.8 (6mo, a); ESX 37.7±0.7 → 37.4±0.7 → 37.3±0.7.
- **ECW/TBW (%)**: significantly decreased in both sexes with SPL (38.7±0.1 → 38.3±0.1 at 3mo P<0.0001 → 38.2±0.1 at 6mo P<0.001); ESX 38.6±0.1 → 38.5±0.1 (3mo P<0.05) → 38.3±0.1 (6mo P<0.0001). Decrease significantly greater in SPL at 3 months (Fig 2C, *P<0.05); ns at 6 months. First report of MRA reducing ECW/TBW.

### Doses achieved
- SPL after 3mo: 16 pts 25mg, 16 pts 50mg, 1 pt 100mg (mean 39.4±2.9 mg/day). After 6mo: 11×25, 19×50, 1×75, 2×100 mg (45±3.3 mg/day).
- ESX after 3mo: 17×1.25, 13×2.5, 2×5 mg (1.99±0.17 mg/day). After 6mo: 13×1.25, 9×2.5, 10×5 mg (2.77±0.28 mg/day).
- No patients needed additional Ca antagonist.

### Adverse effects
- No symptomatic adverse events except **1 male in SPL group developed gynecomastia**.
- No menstrual disturbances (attributed to lower SPL dose and mostly postmenopausal females).

## Conclusion
Serum fT significantly increased with SPL but not ESX (anti-androgen effect of SPL, competitive androgen receptor blockade). Serum K increases significantly higher with SPL than ESX (no hyperkalemia ≥6.0 in either). Body fat/muscle mass changes comparable between groups. ESX showed no apparent adverse effects → safe in PA without severe renal dysfunction.
