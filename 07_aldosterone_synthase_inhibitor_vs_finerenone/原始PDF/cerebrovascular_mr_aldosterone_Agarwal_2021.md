# Cardiovascular and kidney outcomes with finerenone in patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease: the FIDELITY pooled analysis

## Purpose / Design
The FIDELITY analysis was an individual patient-level, prespecified pooled efficacy and safety analysis of the two phase 3 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled finerenone trials — FIDELIO-DKD (NCT02540993) and FIGARO-DKD (NCT02545049) — across a broad spectrum of chronic kidney disease (CKD) in patients with type 2 diabetes (T2D). Patients were on maximally tolerated renin–angiotensin system inhibitor (RASi) therapy.

## Population
- **Total analyzed:** 13,026 patients (after exclusion of 145 patients with Good Clinical Practice violations).
- **Finerenone:** 6,519 patients · **Placebo:** 6,507 patients.
- **Median follow-up:** 3.0 years (IQR 2.3–3.8 years).
- **Baseline:** mean age 64.8 years; mean eGFR 57.6 mL/min/1.73 m²; 39.9% with eGFR ≥60; median UACR 515 mg/g; 99.8% on ACEi/ARB; 45.6% with prior cardiovascular disease.

## Primary Cardiovascular Outcome
Composite of cardiovascular death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure (HHF):
- Finerenone: 825/6,519 (12.7%) vs Placebo: 939/6,507 (14.4%)
- **HR 0.86 (95% CI 0.78–0.95); P = 0.0018**; NNT = 46 over 3 years.

### Individual cardiovascular components
- **Hospitalization for heart failure:** HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.66–0.92); P = 0.0030.
- Cardiovascular death, non-fatal MI, and non-fatal stroke were reported as "directionally consistent" with the composite; individual HRs/CIs for each of these components were not separately tabulated in the full-text summary retrieved.

## Primary Kidney Outcome
Composite of kidney failure, sustained ≥57% decrease in eGFR from baseline, or renal death:
- Finerenone: 360/6,519 (5.5%) vs Placebo: 465/6,507 (7.1%)
- **HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.67–0.88); P = 0.0002**; NNT = 60 over 3 years.

### Kidney secondary / component outcomes
- **Secondary kidney composite (≥40% eGFR decrease, kidney failure, or renal death):** Finerenone 854/6,519 (13.1%) vs Placebo 995/6,507 (15.3%); HR 0.85 (95% CI 0.77–0.93); P = 0.0004.
- **Sustained ≥57% eGFR decline:** HR 0.70 (95% CI 0.60–0.83); P < 0.0001.
- **Kidney failure:** HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.71–0.99); P = 0.03.
- **End-stage kidney disease (ESKD):** HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.64–0.99); P = 0.040.

## Albuminuria
Mean UACR was 32% lower with finerenone versus placebo at month 4 (ratio 0.68; 95% CI 0.66–0.70).

## Safety
- **Hyperkalaemia-related treatment-emergent adverse events:** 14.0% (finerenone) vs 6.9% (placebo).
- **Permanent discontinuation due to hyperkalaemia:** 1.7% vs 0.6%.
- **Serious hyperkalaemia:** 1.1% vs 0.2%.
- **Hyperkalaemia leading to hospitalization:** more frequent with finerenone but uncommon.
- **Hypokalaemia:** 1.1% (finerenone) vs 2.3% (placebo).
- **Serum potassium change at month 4:** +0.21 mmol/L (finerenone) vs +0.02 mmol/L (placebo).
- **Serious adverse events (any):** 31.6% (finerenone) vs 33.7% (placebo).

## Subgroups
Cardiovascular and kidney benefits were generally consistent across prespecified subgroups, including patients using SGLT-2 inhibitors or GLP-1 receptor agonists at baseline.

## Conclusion
Finerenone reduced the risk of clinically important cardiovascular and kidney outcomes versus placebo across the spectrum of CKD in patients with type 2 diabetes. Hyperkalaemia was more frequent with finerenone than placebo but was generally manageable without treatment discontinuation.

## Relevance to cerebrovascular / MR–aldosterone axis
Non-fatal stroke is a component of the primary cardiovascular composite (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, HHF), consistent with the hypothesis that mineralocorticoid receptor blockade with the nonsteroidal MRA finerenone attenuates aldosterone/MR-mediated cardiovascular (including cerebrovascular) risk in T2D-CKD.
