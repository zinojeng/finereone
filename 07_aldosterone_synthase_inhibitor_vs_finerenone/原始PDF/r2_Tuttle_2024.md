## Background
Excess aldosterone accelerates chronic kidney disease (CKD) progression. This phase 2 clinical trial assessed BI 690517 (vicadrostat), an aldosterone synthase inhibitor, for efficacy, safety, and dose selection.

## Methods
- Design: multinational, randomised, controlled, phase 2 trial.
- Population: people aged 18 years or older with an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) of 30 to less than 90 mL/min/1.73 m², a urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) of 200 to less than 5000 mg/g, and serum potassium of 4.8 mmol/L or less, taking an ACE inhibitor or angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB).
- Randomisation: participants randomly assigned (1:1) to 8 weeks of empagliflozin or placebo run-in, followed by a second randomisation (1:1:1:1) to 14 weeks of treatment with once-daily BI 690517 at 3 mg, 10 mg, or 20 mg, or placebo.
- Masking: study participants, research coordinators, investigators, and the data coordinating centre masked to treatment assignment.
- Primary endpoint: change in UACR measured in first morning void urine from baseline (second randomisation) to end of treatment.
- Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05182840 (completed).

## Results
- Enrolment: between Feb 18 and Dec 30, 2022; of 714 run-in participants, 586 were randomly assigned to receive BI 690517 or placebo.
- Baseline characteristics: 33% (n=196) women, 67% (n=390) men; 42% (n=244) had a racial identity other than White; mean age 63.8 years (SD 11.3); mean baseline eGFR 51.9 mL/min/1.73 m² (SD 17.7); median UACR 426 mg/g (IQR 205 to 889).
- Primary endpoint — percentage change in first morning void UACR from baseline to end of treatment (week 14), monotherapy (without empagliflozin):
  - Placebo: −3% (95% CI −19 to 17)
  - BI 690517 3 mg: −22% (95% CI −36 to −7)
  - BI 690517 10 mg: −39% (95% CI −50 to −26)
  - BI 690517 20 mg: −37% (95% CI −49 to −22)
- BI 690517 produced similar UACR reductions when added to empagliflozin.

### Safety
- Investigator-reported hyperkalaemia:
  - BI 690517 3 mg: 10% (14/146)
  - BI 690517 10 mg: 15% (22/144)
  - BI 690517 20 mg: 18% (26/146)
  - Placebo (with or without empagliflozin): 6% (9/147)
- Most participants with hyperkalaemia did not require intervention: 86% (72/84).
- Adrenal insufficiency (adverse event of special interest): 7/436 (2%) receiving BI 690517 vs 1/147 (1%) receiving matched placebo.
- No treatment-related deaths occurred during the study.

## Conclusion
BI 690517 dose-dependently reduced albuminuria with concurrent renin-angiotensin system inhibition and empagliflozin, suggesting additive efficacy for CKD treatment without unexpected safety signals.
