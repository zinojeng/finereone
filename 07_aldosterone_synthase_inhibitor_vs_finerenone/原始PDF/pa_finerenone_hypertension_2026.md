# Efficacy and Safety of Finerenone in Patients With Primary Aldosteronism: A Multicenter Prospective Study

**Journal:** Hypertension. 2026 Jan 22 (published); Vol 83(5): e26048.
**DOI:** 10.1161/HYPERTENSIONAHA.125.26048 · **PMID:** 41568520 · **Trial:** NCT06381323
**Article types:** Clinical Trial Phase IV; Multicenter Study
**Source:** PubMed structured abstract (full text paywalled — AHA journal; no OA / PMC / Sci-Hub copy available).

## Design
- Prospective, multicenter, single-arm, exploratory trial.
- Adults aged ≤75 years with primary aldosteronism (PA).
- Inclusion BP: office BP 140–180 / 90–120 mm Hg; eGFR ≥60 mL/min per 1.73 m².
- Intervention: finerenone 20–40 mg/day for 12 weeks.
- Primary outcome: change in daytime systolic BP (ambulatory) at 12 weeks.
- N = 57 patients ultimately treated.

## Key Results (verbatim from abstract)

### Ambulatory BP (per-protocol)
- Mean daytime systolic BP: **−6.69 ± 1.60 mm Hg** (P < 0.001)
- Mean daytime diastolic BP: **−4.55 ± 1.06 mm Hg** (P < 0.001)

### Office BP
- Office systolic BP: **−15.58 ± 1.69 mm Hg** (P < 0.001)
- Office diastolic BP: **−8.61 ± 1.02 mm Hg** (P < 0.001)

### Serum potassium
- Mean increase in serum potassium: **0.39 ± 0.05 mmol/L**
- Normal potassium concentration after 12 weeks: **94.5%** (vs baseline **61.8%**; P < 0.001)

### Renin
- Plasma renin activity increased.
- **32.7%** of patients exhibited a plasma renin activity ≥1 ng/mL per h.

### PAMO (Primary Aldosteronism Medical Treatment Outcome) criteria
- Complete biochemical response: **29.1%**
- Complete clinical response: **20.0%**

### Safety
- Treatment was well tolerated.

## Conclusion
Study demonstrated efficacy and safety of finerenone in treatment of PA, supporting its use as a potential alternative therapy. Further prospective and head-to-head randomized controlled trials are essential to establish finerenone as a viable substitute for spironolactone.

## Notes
- Finerenone = novel nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA).
- Lead institution: Nanjing Drum Tower Hospital, Nanjing University; multicenter across China (Changzhou, West China Hospital/Sichuan, Peking Union Medical College, Southeast University/Zhongda, Soochow University, Nanchang University, Fujian Medical University).
- MeSH: Hyperaldosteronism; Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists; Naphthyridines; Blood Pressure Monitoring, Ambulatory.
