# Dexfadrostat phosphate in primary aldosteronism — Phase 2 RCT (Mulatero 2024)

> Attribution: Full text retrieved from PubMed Central (PMC11015343). DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eclinm.2024.102576

## Positioning for the review (receptor-blockade vs synthesis-inhibition in PA)
- First RCT of an aldosterone synthase inhibitor (ASI) run **specifically in biochemically confirmed PA** (strict ARR/PAC diagnostic criteria, subtyped by CT/AVS), not in resistant/uncontrolled hypertension.
- ASIs (dexfadrostat, baxdrostat, lorundrostat, vicadrostat/BI 690517) reduce aldosterone *production* upstream; MRAs (spironolactone, eplerenone, finerenone) block the receptor downstream. Paper's argument: MRAs control BP but do **not correct** — and can further **elevate** — aldosterone, leaving MR-independent (e.g. GPER non-genomic) signalling and residual CV risk unaddressed. Synthesis inhibition lowers aldosterone itself (ARR corrected, renin rises).
- Dexfadrostat worked **equally in unilateral and bilateral/undetermined disease** → proposed as medical therapy usable when subtyping/AVS is not feasible or surgery not desired, or as a bridge to surgery (rapidly reversible on withdrawal).
- Osilodrostat (LCI699), the first ASI, was diverted to Cushing's disease due to cortisol (11β-hydroxylase) inhibition; dexfadrostat showed **no off-target cortisol suppression**.

## Design
- Multicentre (11 centres: Italy, Switzerland, Netherlands), randomised, phase 2, dose-finding. Nov 2019 – May 2022.
- 2-week single-blind placebo run-in → 8-week double-blind treatment → 2-week single-blind placebo withdrawal.
- Randomised 1:1:1 to oral dexfadrostat phosphate **4, 8, or 12 mg once daily** (no up-titration). Stratified by centre and sex.
- **No parallel placebo arm** (comparisons: dose groups vs placebo run-in / withdrawal periods) — key limitation.
- Diagnosis of PA required: ARR ≥40 with PAC ≥15 ng/dL and PRA <1.0 ng/mL/h (or plasma renin concentration <15 mU/L), plus confirmatory PAC >7.0 ng/dL after 4h saline load, or PAC >11.0 ng/dL 2h after 50 mg captopril.
- Inclusion: adults 18–65 y, sitting oSBP >145 mmHg, eGFR ≥45. Exclusion: spironolactone within 2 months, K+ >5.0 mmol/L, oSBP >190 or DBP >110, prolonged QT. RAAS inhibitors / β-blockers not permitted.
- Elimination half-life 9.5–11 h (phase 1).

## Primary endpoints
- Change in plasma **ARR** and mean **24 h ambulatory systolic BP (aSBP)** from baseline to end of 8-week treatment, combined dose group. Hierarchical testing (ARR first, then aSBP).

## Baseline (N=35)
- 51 screened → 36 enrolled → **35 randomised** (4 mg n=10; 8 mg n=12; 12 mg n=13). 1 discontinued pre-randomisation (COVID). All 35 completed.
- Male 26 (74.3%); White 32 (91.4%); mean age 51.9 y (SD 8.7).
- PA newly diagnosed 25 (71.4%), recently diagnosed 10 (28.6%).
- Baseline oSBP 147.7 mmHg (SD 11.8); office DBP 92.4 mmHg (SD 9.9).
- Baseline mean plasma potassium 3.5 mmol/L (SD 0.4); 32 (91.4%) on ≥1 fixed antihypertensive.

## Key results (逐字關鍵數字)
### Primary — combined dose group, baseline → end of treatment (day 56)
- **ARR**: median 15.3 (IQR 5.3–25.9) → 0.6 (IQR 0.3–3.0) = **92.1% relative reduction**; LSM change in log-normal −2.5 (95% CI −2.9, −2.2; **p<0.0001**).
- **24 h aSBP**: 142.6 mmHg (SD 14.2) → 131.9 mmHg (SD 13.4); LSM change **−10.7 mmHg** (95% CI −13.6, −7.9; **p<0.0001**).
- **24 h ambulatory DBP**: 87.7 mmHg (SD 7.7) → 82.1 mmHg (SD 7.3); LSM change −5.7 mmHg (95% CI −7.8, −3.5; p<0.0001).

### By dose
- aSBP LSM change (day 1→56): greatest at **8 mg −7.5 mmHg** vs lowest at **4 mg −5.4 mmHg** (p≤0.0005 all doses); 12 mg gave no added benefit over 8 mg.
- Ambulatory DBP decreased in all dose arms (p<0.01 all doses).
- Urinary tetrahydroaldosterone (aldosterone production marker) fell in all arms (p<0.0001): 8 mg LSM −31.0 μg/24h, 12 mg −30.7 μg/24h.
- ARR significantly reduced by day 14 in all arms (p<0.0001), maintained through day 56; rose again after withdrawal (p<0.0001 vs day 56).

### Potassium / renin
- Plasma K+ rose by day 14 in all arms (p<0.0001), stayed within normal range.
- Of 22 participants hypokalaemic at baseline (K+ <3.6 mmol/L), **20 (90.9%)** normalised (>3.6, <5.0) by day 56.
- Withdrawal: K+ LSM change 4 mg −0.5 (p<0.0001), 8 mg −0.3 (p=0.002), 12 mg −0.2 (p=0.05).
- Day 14: 24 participants (68.6%) had increased plasma renin vs day 1; plasma aldosterone fell in all participants.
- Response by sex: males 26/26 (100%) ARR decrease, 23/26 (88.5%) aSBP decrease; females 9/9 (100%) both.

### Subtype
- Change in PAC in unilateral disease ≈ bilateral/undetermined disease (drug effective regardless of subtype).
- Aldosterone precursors (deoxycorticosterone, corticosterone) rose during treatment, fell on withdrawal; **cortisol stable throughout** (no off-target inhibition).

### Safety
- **No serious TEAEs; no discontinuations for TEAE; no severe TEAEs.**
- ≥1 TEAE in 16 participants (45.7%): most common GI disorders (n=6), headache (n=4), investigations (n=3, incl. amylase/LDH/BP increases).
- **No hyperkalaemia** during 8-week treatment (contrast with baxdrostat/lorundrostat where hyperkalaemia is a notable ASI class signal).

## Limitations (per authors)
No placebo-control arm; small N=35; mostly White, few women; short 8-week treatment; referral-centre selection bias; possible residual confounding.

## Interpretation
Dexfadrostat phosphate corrected both biochemical (ARR, urinary THA, K+) and clinical (aSBP/DBP) manifestations of PA across all doses without titration, well tolerated, cortisol-sparing, effective irrespective of PA subtype. Supports synthesis inhibition as a targeted alternative/complement to MR blockade in confirmed PA. Funder: DAMIAN Pharma AG.
