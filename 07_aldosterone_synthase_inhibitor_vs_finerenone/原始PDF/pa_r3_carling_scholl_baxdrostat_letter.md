## Substantive critique captured (usable, directional)

The two criticisms of the phase 2a baxdrostat PA study that are fully legible in the free
preview:

1. **No subtype identification.** The trial did not classify participants as unilateral vs
   bilateral primary aldosteronism.
2. **Surgical alternative not accounted for.** Unilateral aldosterone-producing tumors are
   "best managed surgically" with "high cure rates"; function-preserving adrenalectomy may
   benefit even bilateral disease. Without subtyping, it is unclear whether the enrolled
   patients had the typically milder, medically manageable bilateral form.

A third point begins ("Five patients withdrew from the . . .") but is CUT OFF by the
paywall. Do not assert what the withdrawal argument concluded.

Verbatim correction text (nejm.org, https://www.nejm.org/doi/10.1056/NEJMx250017):

> . In Figure 1 of the authors' reply (page 1551), the data for two of the patients should
> have indicated withdrawal from treatment (denoted by the change of lines from solid to
> dashed) at approximately week 36 and week 60, rather than at week 12. The letter is

Note: the correction applies to Figure 1 of the **reply** (Turcu et al., page 1551), not to
