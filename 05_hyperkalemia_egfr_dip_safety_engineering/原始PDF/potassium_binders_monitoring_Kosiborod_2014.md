## Abstract

**Importance:** Hyperkalemia is a common electrolyte abnormality that may be difficult to manage because of a lack of effective therapies. Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate is a nonabsorbed cation exchanger that selectively binds potassium in the intestine.

**Objective:** To evaluate the effectiveness and safety of sodium zirconium cyclosilicate over 28 days in patients with hyperkalemia.

**Design, Setting, and Participants:** Phase 3 multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. Enrolled 258 outpatients with hyperkalemia (serum potassium ≥5.1 mEq/L) from 44 sites across the United States, Australia, and South Africa (March–August 2014).

**Interventions:** Initial 48-hour open-label phase with sodium zirconium cyclosilicate 10 g given three times daily. Among 237 patients achieving normokalemia (serum potassium 3.5–5.0 mEq/L), randomization assigned patients to receive sodium zirconium cyclosilicate 5 g (n=45), 10 g (n=51), or 15 g (n=56), or placebo (n=85) daily for 28 days.

**Main Outcomes and Measures:** Primary endpoint was mean serum potassium during days 8–29 of the randomized phase.

**Results:**
- Open-label phase: serum potassium declined from 5.6 mEq/L at baseline to 4.5 mEq/L at 48 hours. Median time to normalization was 2.2 hours; 84% of patients achieved normokalemia by 24 hours and 98% by 48 hours.
- Randomized phase: serum potassium was significantly lower across all doses vs placebo — 4.8 mEq/L (5 g), 4.5 mEq/L (10 g), and 4.4 mEq/L (15 g) vs 5.1 mEq/L (placebo) (P<.001 for all).
- Proportion of patients maintaining normal potassium: 80% (5 g), 90% (10 g), 94% (15 g) vs 46% (placebo) (P<.001).
- Edema increased with higher doses: 2% (placebo), 2% (5 g), 6% (10 g), 14% (15 g).
- Hypokalemia occurred in 10% (10 g) and 11% (15 g) of patients.

**Conclusions and Relevance:** Among outpatients with hyperkalemia, open-label sodium zirconium cyclosilicate reduced serum potassium to normal levels within 48 hours; compared with placebo, all 3 doses resulted in lower potassium levels and a higher proportion of patients with normal potassium levels for up to 28 days. Further studies are needed to evaluate longer-term efficacy and safety and effects on clinical outcomes.
