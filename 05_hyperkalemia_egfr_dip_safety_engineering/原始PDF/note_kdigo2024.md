# kdigo2024

- **Full citation**: Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes (KDIGO) CKD Work Group. KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of Chronic Kidney Disease. Kidney Int. 2024;105(4S):S117-S314. DOI: 10.1016/j.kint.2023.10.018. PMID: 38490803.

- **Source obtained**: FULL TEXT — PDF downloaded directly from the official KDIGO website (https://kdigo.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/KDIGO-2024-CKD-Guideline.pdf; 5.8 MB). Verified: title page reads "KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of Chronic Kidney Disease, VOLUME 105 | ISSUE 4S | APRIL 2024." One prior download (europepmc_PMID_40801823.pdf) was POLLUTED (contained Mahfoud et al. CME review on heart/kidney disease, German, unrelated authors) and was discarded.

- **Role in FIND-CKD**: The KDIGO 2024 guideline anchors the background treatment framework within which FIND-CKD (ns-MRA) trial patients were managed. Specifically, it positions (i) RASi (ACEi/ARB) as first-line for albuminuric CKD (graded 1B for A3 without diabetes, 2C for A2 without diabetes, 1B for A2-A3 with diabetes); (ii) SGLT2i as a first-line agent in CKD (1A for T2D+CKD+eGFR >=20; 1A for CKD+eGFR >=20+ACR >=200 mg/g or heart failure; 2B for eGFR 20-45+ACR <200 mg/g); and (iii) ns-MRA (finerenone) as a targeted add-on in T2D+CKD+persistent albuminuria >30 mg/g+eGFR >25 despite maximum-tolerated RASi (Recommendation 3.8.1: 2A). The guideline's albuminuria x GFR risk matrix (A1/A2/A3 x G1-G5, color-coded green/yellow/orange/red) defines the high-risk sub-populations targeted by FIND-CKD. Anemia management (including iron therapy) appears in Figure 18 as a "targeted therapy for complications" separate from the disease-modifying drug backbone, and Section 3.12 defers specific iron/ESA recommendations to the KDIGO Anemia in CKD guideline.

- **Key findings** (verified from full text):
  - Population: Adults and children with CKD not receiving dialysis and not kidney-transplant recipients. The document contains 28 graded recommendations and 141 practice points.
  - Risk framework: CKD prognosis is classified by a 5 x 3 GFR/albuminuria matrix (G1-G5; A1 <30 mg/g, A2 30-300 mg/g, A3 >300 mg/g). Risk cells range from green (low) to red (very high). Relative risks from CKD-PC data are tabulated; for example, for kidney failure the adjusted hazard ratios at eGFR 45-59 ml/min/1.73 m2 across A1-A3 are approximately 1.6, 2.0, and 2.4 relative to G2/A1 reference (verified from Table S4 data in converted text).
  - RASi recommendations (Section 3.6): Rec 3.6.1 (1B): start ACEi/ARB for G1-G4, A3 without diabetes. Rec 3.6.2 (2C): suggest ACEi/ARB for G1-G4, A2 without diabetes. Rec 3.6.3 (1B): recommend ACEi/ARB for G1-G4, A2 and A3 with diabetes. Rec 3.6.4 (1B): avoid dual RAAS blockade.
  - SGLT2i recommendations (Section 3.7): Rec 3.7.1 (1A): T2D+CKD+eGFR >=20 ml/min/1.73 m2. Rec 3.7.2 (1A): CKD+eGFR >=20+ACR >=200 mg/g or heart failure. Rec 3.7.3 (2B): eGFR 20-45+ACR <200 mg/g.
  - ns-MRA recommendation (Section 3.8): Rec 3.8.1 (2A): finerenone for T2D+eGFR >25+normal serum K++albuminuria >30 mg/g despite maximum-tolerated RASi. Practice Point 3.8.2: ns-MRA may be added to RASi plus SGLT2i. No effect size quoted within this 2024 CKD guideline; it cross-references KDIGO 2022 Diabetes in CKD guideline (Kidney Int. 2022;102:S1-S127) for the underlying FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD trial data.
  - Treatment algorithm (Figure 18): SGLT2i + RASi + statin comprise "first-line drug therapy for most patients"; ns-MRA and GLP-1 RA are positioned as "targeted therapies" in T2D; anemia, CKD-MBD, acidosis, and potassium abnormalities are listed as targeted complication management.

- **How it SUPPORTS FIND-CKD's conclusions**: KDIGO 2024 independently validates that the three-drug background (RASi/SGLT2i/ns-MRA) recommended for high-albuminuria CKD represents the current guideline standard, grounding FIND-CKD's design in established evidence-based practice. The albuminuria-risk matrix explains why FIND-CKD's patient selection (CKD with persistent albuminuria despite RASi) captures a population at genuinely high risk of progression. The guideline's explicit positioning of ns-MRA as an add-on to RASi+SGLT2i in T2D supports FIND-CKD's hypothesis that residual aldosterone escape after dual therapy is a treatable target.

- **How it CHALLENGES or LIMITS FIND-CKD's conclusions**: KDIGO 2024 gives finerenone only a 2A recommendation (graded "suggest" with "moderate certainty"), not a strong 1A/1B recommendation. The guideline restricts this suggestion to T2D with eGFR >25 and normal potassium at baseline — a narrower population than FIND-CKD may enroll if it includes non-diabetic CKD or patients with borderline hyperkalemia. Additionally, the SGLT2i recommendation as a near-universal first-line agent (1A) means that the incremental benefit of adding ns-MRA must now be evaluated on top of SGLT2i, which was not the context in the pivotal FIDELIO-DKD/FIGARO-DKD trials underlying Rec 3.8.1.

- **Limitations of this reference itself**:
  1. Literature search closed July 2023; SGLT2i+ns-MRA combination data from FLOW, FIDELITY-Renal, and any subsequent trials are not incorporated.
  2. The guideline defers ns-MRA recommendations entirely to the 2022 Diabetes guideline — there are no new systematic reviews of MRA trials in the 2024 CKD guideline itself for the MRA section.
  3. The anemia section (3.12) is deliberately sparse, deferring to the KDIGO Anemia guideline; the 2024 CKD guideline therefore provides minimal direct guidance on iron deficiency or oral versus intravenous iron in CKD.
  4. The 2A strength for ns-MRA reflects acknowledged gaps: absence of RCT evidence in non-diabetic CKD, limited data on combination with SGLT2i, and potassium safety uncertainties at eGFR 25-45.
  5. Pediatric recommendations are largely extrapolated from adult data.
