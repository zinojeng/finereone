In FIDELITY, a prespecified pooled analysis of the FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD studies, finerenone was found to improve cardiorenal outcomes in patients with type 2 diabetes, a urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio of 30–5000 mg/g, an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) of 25 ml/min per 1.73 m² or more and also receiving optimized renin-angiotensin system blockade treatment. This present analysis focused on the efficacy and safety of finerenone on kidney outcomes. Among 13,026 patients with a median follow-up of three years, finerenone significantly reduced the hazard of a kidney composite outcome (time to kidney failure, sustained 57% or more decrease in eGFR from baseline, or kidney death) by 23% versus placebo (hazard ratio, 0.77; 95% confidence interval, 0.67–0.88), with a three-year absolute between-group difference of 1.7% (95% confidence interval, 0.7–2.6). Hazard ratios were directionally consistent for a prespecified baseline eGFR and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio categories (Pinteraction [ 0.62 and Pinteraction [ 0.67, respectively), although there was a high degree of uncertainty in the 30–300 mg/g subgroup. Finerenone significantly reduced the hazard of end-stage kidney disease (ESKD) by 20% versus placebo.

# Procedures and outcomes

Eligible patients were randomized 1:1 to receive oral finerenone (at titrated doses of 10 or 20 mg once daily) or placebo. Both studies consisted of run-in, screening, double-blind treatment and safety follow-up periods. At either the run-in or screening visits, if the recorded UACR and eGFR levels fell outside the inclusion criteria of one trial, patients were permitted to switch to the other trial.

The efficacy outcomes in this analysis include a composite kidney outcome of time to first onset of kidney failure (defined as end-stage kidney disease or a sustained eGFR &#x3C;15 ml/min per 1.73 m²), sustained ≥57% decrease in eGFR from baseline over ≥4 weeks, or renal death. End-stage kidney disease was defined as the initiation of chronic dialysis (for ≥90 days) or kidney transplant. The sustained eGFR-based components of the composite kidney outcome (eGFR &#x3C;15 ml/min per 1.73 m² and a ≥57% decrease in eGFR from baseline) required ≥2 consecutive eGFR central laboratory measurements to meet the specified threshold over a period of ≥4 weeks. All kidney end points were prospectively adjudicated by an independent clinical event committee composed of nephrologists blinded to treatment assignment. Other outcomes included in this analysis are individual components of the kidney composite outcome and change in UACR in the overall population and according to UACR categories at baseline.

# Statistical analysis

All statistical analyses were prespecified exploratory evaluations in FIDELITY, except the following post hoc analyses: kidney outcomes using UACR as a continuous variable, the number of patients with a UACR ≥300 mg/g needed to be treated with finerenone to prevent one outcome event, and rate of hyperkalemia according to eGFR category at baseline. Where P values are provided, these are exploratory and have not been adjusted for multiplicity. Efficacy analyses were performed using the full analysis set, including all randomized patients (except those with critical Good Clinical Practice violations who were excluded from all analyses). Outcome events were reported from randomization up to the end-of-study visit. Patients without an event were censored at the date of their last contact, with complete information on all components of their respective outcomes. For time-to-first event outcomes, cumulative incidences were calculated on the basis of Aalen-Johansen estimates using all-cause death as a competing risk. In the overall population, stratified log-rank tests were used for the comparison of treatment groups. Treatment outcomes are expressed as hazard ratios (HRs) with corresponding confidence intervals (CIs) from a Cox proportional hazards regression model. Stratification factors were as follows: study, region (North America, Latin America, Europe, Asia, and other), a history of CV disease (present or absent), eGFR category at screening (25–&#x3C;45, 45–&#x3C;60, and ≥60 ml/min per 1.73 m²), and albuminuria category (UACR 30–&#x3C;300 mg/g and UACR ≥300 mg/g) at screening. For subgroup analyses, stratified Cox proportional hazards models including treatment were calculated for each subgroup category. P values for interaction are based on models including treatment, subgroup, and treatment-by-subgroup interaction. The ratio of UACR from baseline to month 4 was evaluated by an analysis of covariance model with the factors of treatment group, region, eGFR category at screening, type of albuminuria at screening, CV disease history, study, baseline log-transformed UACR value as covariate nested within UACR category, and the interaction between study and treatment. Safety analyses were performed using the safety analysis set, consisting of all randomized patients without critical Good Clinical Practice violations who took ≥1 dose of the study drug.

# RESULTS

# Subgroup analysis by baseline eGFR and UACR categories

A total of 13,026 randomized patients from the FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD studies were analyzed in FIDELITY. At the run-in visit, potassium values were available for 29,604 of 33,292 patients; 6090 (20.6%) of measured potassium values were >4.8 mmol/l, and a total of 16,525 patients completed the run-in visit. At the screening visit, 18,075 potassium values were available, of which 2527 (15.3%) were >4.8 mmol/l. The median follow-up duration was 3.0 years.¹¹ Full patient demographics and baseline characteristics for the FIDELITY analysis population have been published previously.¹¹ In total, 40% and 60% of participants had a baseline eGFR of ≥60 and &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m², respectively, and 33% and 67% had a baseline UACR of &#x3C;300 and ≥300 mg/g, respectively. The mean (SD) eGFRs were 79.9 (14.3) and 42.8 (9.7) in the ≥60 and &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m² categories, respectively, with each group having a median UACR of 562 mg/g (interquartile range, 299–1102 mg/g) and 471 mg/g (interquartile range, 148–1175 mg/g), mean serum potassium of 4.3 (0.4) and 4.4 (0.5) mmol/l, and 8.7% versus 13.9% of patients with serum potassium >4.8 mmol/l at baseline. Within the UACR &#x3C;300 and ≥300 mg/g categories, the mean eGFRs were 53.8 (18.3) and 59.5 (22.9) ml/min per 1.73 m², the median UACR values were 114 (interquartile range, 61–196) and 871 (interquartile range, 513–1564) mg/g, the mean serum potassium values were 4.4 (0.4) and 4.3 (0.4) mmol/l, and 12.4% versus 11.5% of patients had serum potassium >4.8 mmol/l, respectively. Other select characteristics of patients in baseline eGFR and UACR categories are included in Table 1.

# Effect of finerenone on the kidney composite outcome and its components

Of the 13,026 patients, 360 (5.5%) finerenone-treated patients versus 465 (7.1%) patients receiving placebo experienced a kidney composite end point event, corresponding to a 23% relative hazard reduction with finerenone versus placebo (HR, 0.77; 95% CI, 0.67–0.88; P = 0.0002). Finerenone reduced the incidence of all nonfatal components of the kidney composite outcome compared with placebo. The hazard of end-stage kidney disease (experienced by 151 [2.3%] patients in the finerenone group and 188 [2.9%] of patients in the placebo group) was reduced by 20% with finerenone versus placebo (HR, 0.80; 95% CI, 0.64–0.99; P = 0.040). Analysis of the sustained ≥57% decrease in eGFR component showed a 30% hazard reduction with finerenone compared with placebo (HR, 0.70; 95% CI, 0.60–0.83; P &#x3C; 0.0001). Few renal deaths were observed, which precluded a meaningful evaluation between treatment arms (2 and 4 patients in the finerenone and placebo groups, respectively). The number of patients who needed to be treated with finerenone to prevent one kidney outcome event was 60 (95% CI, 38–142), based on an absolute between-group risk difference of 1.7% (95% CI, 0.7%–2.6%) at 3.0 years of follow-up.¹¹

As reported previously, the change in UACR from baseline to month 4 was 32% lower with finerenone versus placebo in the overall population (ratio of least squares mean change from baseline, 0.68; 95% CI, 0.66–0.70); the ratios to baseline at month 4 of the geometric means in UACR were 0.64 and 0.94 in the finerenone and placebo groups, respectively. In the patient subgroups with a baseline UACR 30 to &#x3C;300 and ≥300 mg/g, finerenone led to a similar reduction in UACR at month 4 relative to baseline compared with placebo; ratios to baseline at month 4 of geometric means were 0.67 versus 1.03 and 0.61 versus 0.88 with finerenone versus placebo in patients with UACR 30 to &#x3C;300 and ≥300 mg/g, respectively.

In the overall population, the least squares mean change in eGFR slope from baseline to end of treatment was similar.

# Discussion

The most common treatment-emergent AE in finerenone-treated patients was hyperkalemia, which was reported more frequently in patients with eGFR &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m² at baseline. The incidence of permanent discontinuation or hospitalization due to hyperkalemia in finerenone-treated patients was low in both subgroups. The incidence of discontinuation due to hyperkalemia was 2.4% with finerenone versus 0.8% with placebo in patients with an eGFR &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m²; in patients with an eGFR ≥60 ml/min per 1.73 m², the corresponding incidences were 0.6% versus 0.3%. The incidence of hyperkalemia leading to hospitalization was 1.4% with finerenone versus 0.3% with placebo in patients with an eGFR &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m², and 0.3% versus 0%, respectively, in patients with an eGFR ≥60 ml/min per 1.73 m².

The incidence of hyponatremia was slightly higher in patients treated with finerenone than in those receiving placebo in both eGFR subgroups (1.2% vs. 0.8% in patients with eGFR &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m² and 1.1% vs. 0.5% in patients with eGFR ≥60 ml/min per 1.73 m², respectively). However, few events led to treatment discontinuation. Hypotension occurred more often in finerenone-treated patients than in placebo recipients (4.9% vs. 3.6% in patients with eGFR &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m² and 3.4% vs. 1.5% in patients with eGFR ≥60 ml/min per 1.73 m², respectively), but treatment discontinuation due to hypotension was low in all subgroups.

The frequency of acute kidney injury AEs was higher in patients with an eGFR &#x3C;60 ml/min per 1.73 m² at baseline in both treatment groups, and no difference was observed between patients treated with finerenone compared with placebo. In the Veterans Affairs Nephrology in Diabetes (VA NEPHRON-D) study (in patients with T2D), combination therapy with an angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitor and an angiotensin receptor blocker was associated with an increased risk of acute kidney injury AEs, which occurred in 18% of patients receiving losartan and lisinopril compared with 11% of patients receiving losartan alone.

Although FIDELITY, a prespecified individual patient data pooled analysis, included a large patient population with T2D across a broad spectrum of CKD, notable limitations are that patients with nonalbuminuric CKD were not included in the analysis and only a small proportion of Black patients were included. In addition, the analyses presented were exploratory in nature and were not adjusted for multiplicity. It is also important to highlight that FIDELITY data are from 2 randomized controlled trials with strict eligibility criteria and regular follow-up visits, and that insights into the use of finerenone in real-world practice will be provided by the ongoing observational study, A Non-interventional Study Providing Insights Into the Use of Finerenone in a Routine Clinical Setting (FINE-REAL; NCT05348733).

Overall, the FIDELITY analysis shows that the hazard of the kidney composite outcome and all nonfatal components of the kidney composite outcome was reduced with finerenone compared with placebo. This analysis further demonstrates that finerenone provides robust kidney efficacy and safety benefits across the spectrum of CKD in T2D and highlights the benefits of early treatment initiation to slow CKD progression in patients with T2D.
