# Kerendia (finerenone) — EMA EPAR & Product Information (SmPC)

> Verification note: Content extracted from the official EMA EPAR Product Information PDF (English, Annex I — Summary of Product Characteristics). Title, active substance (finerenone), MAH (Bayer AG), and clinical content all match the EPAR metadata. content_verified = true.

## Administrative / EPAR overview
- Medicine: Kerendia (INN: finerenone)
- Marketing Authorisation Holder: Bayer AG, Leverkusen, Germany
- EMA product number: EMEA/H/C/005200
- CHMP positive opinion: 16 December 2021; EU marketing authorisation: 16 February 2022
- Subject to additional monitoring (black triangle)
- Mechanism: non-steroidal, selective mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA)

## 1. Name / pharmaceutical form
Film-coated tablets: Kerendia 10 mg, 20 mg, and 40 mg film-coated tablets.
- 10 mg: each tablet contains 10 mg finerenone (45 mg lactose monohydrate); pink, oval-oblong, marked '10' / 'FI'.
- 20 mg: each tablet contains 20 mg finerenone (40 mg lactose monohydrate); pale yellow, marked '20' / 'FI'.
- 40 mg: each tablet contains 40 mg finerenone (25 mg lactose monohydrate); grey-orange, marked '40' / 'FI'.

## 4.1 Therapeutic indications
- Kerendia is indicated for the treatment of **chronic kidney disease (with albuminuria) associated with type 2 diabetes in adults**.
- Kerendia is indicated for the treatment of **symptomatic chronic heart failure with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≥ 40% in adults**.

## 4.2 Posology and method of administration

### Chronic kidney disease associated with type 2 diabetes (T2D)
- Recommended target dose = maximum recommended dose = **20 mg finerenone once daily**.
- Serum potassium and eGFR must be measured before initiation.

Initiation thresholds (serum potassium):
- ≤ 4.8 mmol/L → treatment can be initiated.
- > 4.8 to 5.0 mmol/L → initiation may be considered, with additional serum potassium monitoring within the first 4 weeks.
- > 5.0 mmol/L → treatment should **not** be initiated.

**Table 1 — Initiation and recommended starting dose (by eGFR)**

| eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Starting dose (once daily) |
|---|---|
| ≥ 60 | 20 mg |
| ≥ 25 to < 60 | 10 mg |
| < 25 | Not recommended |

Continuation: remeasure serum potassium and eGFR **4 weeks** after initiation/re-start/dose increase, then periodically.

**Table 2 — Continuation and dose adjustment (CKD+T2D)**

| Current serum K⁺ (mmol/L) | Current dose 10 mg | Current dose 20 mg |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 4.8 | Increase to 20 mg once daily* | Maintain 20 mg once daily |
| > 4.8 to 5.5 | Maintain 10 mg once daily | Maintain 20 mg once daily |
| > 5.5 | Withhold; consider re-starting at 10 mg once daily when serum K⁺ ≤ 5.0 mmol/L | Withhold; re-start at 10 mg once daily when serum K⁺ ≤ 5.0 mmol/L |

*Maintain 10 mg once daily if eGFR has decreased > 30% compared with the previous measurement.

### Heart failure with LVEF ≥ 40%
- Recommended target dose depends on eGFR at initiation:
  - 40 mg once daily if eGFR ≥ 60 mL/min/1.73 m²
  - 20 mg once daily if eGFR ≥ 25 to < 60 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Maximum recommended dose = 40 mg once daily.
- Initiation: if serum potassium ≤ 5.0 mmol/L, treatment can be initiated.

**Table 3 — Initiation and recommended starting dose (HF, by eGFR)**

| eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Starting dose (once daily) |
|---|---|
| ≥ 60 | 20 mg |
| ≥ 25 to < 60 | 10 mg |
| < 25 | Not recommended |

**Table 4 — Continuation and dose adjustment (HF with LVEF ≥ 40%)**

| Current serum K⁺ (mmol/L) | 10 mg | 20 mg | 40 mg |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 5.0 | Increase to 20 mg if eGFR not decreased > 30% vs previous | Increase to 40 mg if eGFR not decreased > 30% vs previous; maintain 20 mg if eGFR < 60 mL/min/1.73 m² at initiation | Maintain 40 mg; decrease to 20 mg if eGFR decreased > 30% vs previous |
| 5.0 to < 5.5 | Maintain 10 mg | Maintain 20 mg | Maintain 40 mg; decrease to 20 mg if eGFR decreased > 30% vs previous |
| 5.5 to < 6.0 | Withhold; re-start at 10 mg when serum K⁺ < 5.5 mmol/L | Decrease to 10 mg | Decrease to 20 mg |
| ≥ 6.0 | Withhold; re-start at 10 mg once daily when serum K⁺ < 5.5 mmol/L, or if repeatedly ≥ 5.5 mmol/L wait to re-start until < 5.0 mmol/L | (same) | (same) |

If eGFR decreases by ≥ 40% vs previous measurement, consider reducing the dose or withholding finerenone; once eGFR stabilises, consider increasing the dose or restarting.

### Missed dose
Take as soon as noticed, but only on the same day; do not take 2 doses to make up for a missed dose.

## 4.3 Contraindications
- Hypersensitivity to the active substance or any excipient.
- Concomitant treatment with **strong CYP3A4 inhibitors** (e.g., itraconazole, ketoconazole, ritonavir, nelfinavir, cobicistat, clarithromycin, telithromycin, nefazodone).
- **Addison's disease.**

## 4.4 Special warnings and precautions for use

### Hyperkalaemia
- Hyperkalaemia has been observed with finerenone. Higher-risk patients: **low eGFR, higher serum potassium, previous episodes of hyperkalaemia** → consider more frequent monitoring.
- If serum potassium > 5.0 mmol/L, treatment should not be initiated.
- **Monitoring:** remeasure serum potassium and eGFR 4 weeks after initiation, restart, or dose adjustment; thereafter periodically.
- CKD+T2D: if K⁺ > 4.8 to 5.0 mmol/L, initiation may be considered with extra monitoring in first 4 weeks; if K⁺ > 5.5 mmol/L, withhold — follow local hyperkalaemia guidelines; restart at 10 mg once daily once K⁺ ≤ 5.0 mmol/L.
- HF with LVEF ≥ 40%: if K⁺ > 6.0 mmol/L, withhold; restart at 10 mg once K⁺ < 5.5 mmol/L; with repeated K⁺ ≥ 5.5 mmol/L only restart when K⁺ < 5.0 mmol/L.

Concomitant medicines / hyperkalaemia risk:
- Do **not** co-administer with potassium-sparing diuretics (amiloride, triamterene) or other MRAs (eplerenone, esaxerenone, spironolactone, canrenone).
- Use with caution and monitor serum potassium with potassium supplements / potassium-enriched salt substitutes, and with trimethoprim or trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole (temporary discontinuation of finerenone may be necessary).

### Worsening of renal function (HF with LVEF ≥ 40%)
Increased incidence of worsening renal function reported. Elderly and patients with eGFR < 60 mL/min/1.73 m² are at higher risk and should be monitored more frequently.

### Renal impairment
- Risk of hyperkalaemia increases with decreasing renal function.
- Do not initiate if eGFR < 25 mL/min/1.73 m² (limited data).
- Discontinue in patients who progress to end-stage renal disease (eGFR < 15 mL/min/1.73 m²).

## 4.8 Undesirable effects

### Summary of the safety profile
- Most frequently reported adverse reaction: **hyperkalaemia (12.6%)**.
- Safety database: FIDELIO-DKD (2 818 finerenone patients, mean treatment 2.2 years), FIGARO-DKD (3 671 finerenone patients, mean 2.9 years), FINEARTS-HF (2 993 finerenone patients [10/20/40 mg], mean 2.1 years).

### Table 5 — Adverse reactions (selected)
- **Very common (≥ 1/10):** Hyperkalaemia (CKD+T2D).
- **Common (≥ 1/100 to < 1/10):** Hyperkalaemia (HF); Hyponatraemia; Hyperuricaemia; Hypotension; Diarrhoea/Constipation (HF); Pruritus (CKD+T2D); Renal impairment / Acute kidney injury (HF); Blood creatinine increased / GFR decreased.
- **Uncommon:** Haemoglobin decreased (CKD+T2D).

### Hyperkalaemia (selected adverse reaction detail)
- Mean serum potassium increased from baseline by up to **0.2 mmol/L** in the first month vs placebo, then stable.
- Pooled FIDELIO-DKD + FIGARO-DKD:
  - Hyperkalaemia events: **14.0% finerenone vs 6.9% placebo**.
  - Serious hyperkalaemia: **1.1% vs 0.2%**.
  - Serum K⁺ > 5.5 mmol/L: **16.8% (finerenone) vs 7.4% (placebo)**; > 6.0 mmol/L: **3.3% vs 1.3%**.
  - Permanent discontinuation due to hyperkalaemia: **1.7% vs 0.6%**.
  - Hospitalisation due to hyperkalaemia: **0.9% vs 0.2%**.
- FINEARTS-HF:
  - Hyperkalaemia events: **9.7% finerenone vs 4.2% placebo**.
  - Permanent discontinuation due to hyperkalaemia: **0.4% vs 0.2%**.
  - Hospitalisation due to hyperkalaemia: **0.5% vs 0.2%**.
- Majority of events mild–moderate and resolved.

### Worsening of renal function / eGFR decrease (selected detail)
- Pooled FIDELIO-DKD + FIGARO-DKD: GFR-decreased events **5.4% finerenone vs 4.2% placebo**; blood creatinine increased **2.6% vs 2.3%**; permanent discontinuation for GFR decrease equal (0.2%).
- Initial eGFR decrease (mean ~**2 mL/min/1.73 m²**) that attenuated over time and appeared reversible during continuous treatment.
- FINEARTS-HF: worsening renal function events **17.7% finerenone vs 10.9% placebo** (renal impairment 6.6% vs 3.9%; GFR decreased 5.2% vs 3.6%; acute kidney injury 3.7% vs 2.1%; renal failure 2.6% vs 1.6%; blood creatinine increased 1.2% vs 0.8%). Mean GFR difference vs placebo ~3–4 mL/min/1.73 m² (month 1–6), attenuating to ~2–3 mL/min/1.73 m² after month 6.

### Hypotension (selected detail)
- Mean systolic BP decrease 2–4 mmHg and diastolic 1–2 mmHg at month 1, stable thereafter.
- Pooled FIDELIO/FIGARO: hypotension events **4.7% finerenone vs 3.0% placebo**.

## 5.1 Clinical efficacy (selected)

### FIDELIO-DKD — Table 6 (finerenone N = 2 824 vs placebo N = 2 838)
- Primary renal composite (kidney failure, sustained eGFR decline ≥ 40%, or renal death): **498 (17.6%) vs 600 (21.1%); HR 0.82 (0.73–0.92), p = 0.0009**.
  - Kidney failure: 205 (7.3%) vs 235 (8.3%); HR 0.86 (0.72–1.05).
  - Sustained eGFR decline ≥ 40%: 473 (16.7%) vs 577 (20.3%); HR 0.81 (0.72–0.91).
- Key secondary CV composite (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or hospitalisation for HF): **366 (13.0%) vs 420 (14.8%); HR 0.86 (0.75–0.99), p = 0.0344**.
  - CV death: 128 (4.5%) vs 150 (5.3%); HR 0.86 (0.68–1.09).
  - Non-fatal MI: 70 (2.5%) vs 87 (3.1%); HR 0.80 (0.58–1.09).
  - Non-fatal stroke: 90 (3.2%) vs 87 (3.1%); HR 1.03 (0.77–1.38).
  - Hospitalisation for HF: 138 (4.9%) vs 162 (5.7%); HR 0.85 (0.68–1.07).
- All-cause mortality: 219 (7.8%) vs 244 (8.6%); HR 0.90 (0.75–1.08).

### FIGARO-DKD — Table 5 (finerenone N = 3 686 vs placebo N = 3 666)
- Primary CV composite (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or hospitalisation for HF): **458 (12.4%) vs 519 (14.2%); HR 0.87 (0.76–0.98), p = 0.0264**.
  - CV death: 194 (5.3%) vs 214 (5.8%); HR 0.90 (0.74–1.09).
  - Hospitalisation for HF: 117 (3.2%) vs 163 (4.4%); HR 0.71 (0.56–0.90).
- Secondary renal composite (kidney failure, sustained eGFR decline ≥ 40%, or renal death): 350 (9.5%) vs 395 (10.8%); HR 0.87 (0.76–1.01), p = 0.0689.

## Overdose (4.9)
Most likely manifestation of overdose is anticipated to be **hyperkalaemia**.
