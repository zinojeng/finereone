## Abstract

**Purpose:** This analysis aimed to identify all strong drug-drug interactions (DDIs) associated with drugs approved by the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) in 2021.

**Findings (as reported in the abstract):** The study examined 36 small-molecule drugs approved in 2021 and identified seven that demonstrated substantial (≥5-fold) changes in exposure as victim drugs. Six compounds — atogepant, finerenone, ibrexafungerp, infigratinib, mobocertinib, and voclosporin — showed heightened sensitivity to CYP3A inhibitors. Avacopan proved moderately sensitive to CYP3A inhibition and highly susceptible to enzyme induction. Viloxazine emerged as the sole strong perpetrator, acting through CYP1A2 inhibition.

**Conclusion:** Overall, identifying strong DDIs with newly approved drugs and understanding their mechanisms are critical to provide effective management strategies in patients who often receive multiple comedications.

## Relevance note (finerenone)

Directly relevant to finerenone safety/DDI: finerenone is identified among the drugs highly sensitive to strong CYP3A inhibitors (≥5-fold exposure increase), supporting label-based contraindications/dose adjustments with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors. This detail is from the abstract only; the quantitative fold-change tables in the full article were not accessible.
