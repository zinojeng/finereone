## 1 INDICATIONS AND USAGE

Kerendia is a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (nsMRA) indicated to reduce the risk of:

- **CKD associated with T2DM:** sustained estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) decline, end stage kidney disease, cardiovascular death, non-fatal myocardial infarction, and hospitalization for heart failure in adult patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) associated with type 2 diabetes (T2DM).
- **Heart failure (LVEF ≥ 40%):** cardiovascular death, hospitalization for heart failure, and urgent heart failure visits in adult patients with heart failure with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≥ 40%.

---

## 2 DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION

### Pre-initiation requirements
- Measure **serum potassium** and **eGFR** in all patients before initiation and dose accordingly.
- **Do not initiate Kerendia if serum potassium is > 5.0 mEq/L.**
- Measure eGFR in all patients before initiation or with dose titration and dose accordingly.
- Initiation of Kerendia in patients with heart failure and an **eGFR < 25 mL/min/1.73 m²** is not recommended.

### 2.1 Starting dosage (based on eGFR)

| eGFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) | Starting dose |
|---|---|
| ≥ 60 | 20 mg orally once daily |
| ≥ 25 to < 60 | 10 mg orally once daily |
| < 25 | Initiation not recommended |

### Target dose
- **CKD with T2DM:** target dose **20 mg** orally once daily.
- **Heart failure (LVEF ≥ 40%):**
  - eGFR ≥ 60 at initiation → target **40 mg** orally once daily
  - eGFR ≥ 25 to < 60 at initiation → target **20 mg** orally once daily

### 2.x Dose adjustment — serum potassium (CKD with T2DM)

| Current K⁺ (mEq/L) | On 10 mg | On 20 mg |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 4.8 | Increase to 20 mg | Maintain 20 mg |
| > 4.8 – 5.5 | Maintain 10 mg | Maintain 20 mg |
| > 5.5 | Withhold; restart at 10 mg when K⁺ ≤ 5.0 | Withhold; restart at 10 mg when K⁺ ≤ 5.0 |

### 2.x Dose adjustment — serum potassium (Heart Failure, LVEF ≥ 40%)

| Current K⁺ (mEq/L) | On 10 mg | On 20 mg | On 40 mg |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 5.0 | Increase to 20 mg | Increase to 40 mg (if eGFR ≥ 60) or maintain 20 mg | Maintain 40 mg |
| ≥ 5.0 to < 5.5 | Maintain | Maintain | Maintain |
| ≥ 5.5 to < 6.0 | Withhold; restart at 10 mg when K⁺ < 5.5 | Decrease to 10 mg | Decrease to 20 mg |
| ≥ 6.0 | Withhold; restart at 10 mg when K⁺ < 5.5 | Decrease to 10 mg | Decrease to 20 mg |

### eGFR / renal monitoring (heart failure)
- Measure eGFR periodically during maintenance treatment in patients with heart failure.
- Consider delaying up-titration or interrupting treatment in patients who develop clinically significant worsening of renal function.

## 3 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Kerendia is contraindicated in patients:
- Who are hypersensitive to any component of this product.
- Who are receiving concomitant treatment with **strong CYP3A4 inhibitors**.
- With **adrenal insufficiency**.

---

## 5 WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS

### 5.1 Hyperkalemia
Kerendia can cause hyperkalemia. The risk for developing hyperkalemia increases with decreasing kidney function and is greater in patients with higher baseline potassium levels or other risk factors for hyperkalemia. Measure serum potassium and eGFR in all patients before initiation of treatment with Kerendia and dose accordingly. **Do not initiate Kerendia if serum potassium is > 5.0 mEq/L.** Measure serum potassium periodically during treatment with Kerendia and adjust dose accordingly.

### 5.2 Worsening of Renal Function in Patients with Heart Failure
Measure eGFR and adjust dose as needed.

## 6 ADVERSE REACTIONS

Adverse reactions occurring in ≥ 1% of patients on Kerendia and more frequently than placebo are **hyperkalemia, hypotension, and hyponatremia**.

### 6.1 Clinical Trials Experience

**CKD with T2DM (FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD pooled):**

| Adverse reaction | Kerendia (n=6510) | Placebo (n=6489) |
|---|---|---|
| Hyperkalemia | 912 (14.0%) | 448 (6.9%) |
| Hypotension | 302 (4.6%) | 194 (3.0%) |
| Hyponatremia | 82 (1.3%) | 47 (0.7%) |

**Heart Failure (FINEARTS-HF):**
- Overall safety profile largely consistent with CKD/T2DM population.
- Adverse reactions related to worsening renal function reported more frequently with Kerendia (**18%**) vs placebo (**12%**). Most frequent: renal impairment (7% vs 4%), eGFR decreased (5% vs 4%), acute kidney injury (4% vs 2%), renal failure (3% vs 2%). Majority mild to moderate.
- These events led to dose modifications in **9%** of Kerendia vs **4%** of placebo patients.
- Hospitalization due to worsening-renal-function events: Kerendia **2.0%** vs placebo **1.3%**.
