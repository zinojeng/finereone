## Abstract Summary

The AMBER trial investigated whether patiromer enabled sustained spironolactone therapy by reducing hyperkalaemia risk in patients with resistant hypertension and advanced CKD. In the pre-specified heart failure subgroup (132 of 295 enrolled patients, 45%), researchers assessed whether this potassium binder could facilitate mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist use. The analysis found that patiromer improved medication persistence while managing potassium levels.

---

## Methods

**Study Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled parallel-group trial (NCT03071263)

**Participant Criteria:**
- Age ≥18 years
- eGFR 25–45 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Serum K+ between 4.3–5.1 mmol/L
- Unattended systolic automated office BP (AOBP) 135–160 mmHg despite ≥3 antihypertensives

**Heart Failure Subgroup:** 132 patients (69 placebo; 63 patiromer)

**Dosing:**
- Patiromer: 8.4 g once daily (maximum 25.2 g)
- Spironolactone: started at 25 mg, titrated to 50 mg at week 3 if K+ ≤5.1 mmol/L
- Study duration: 12 weeks

---

## Primary Results

**Spironolactone Persistence at Week 12 (HF subgroup):**
- Placebo group: 68.1% remained on therapy
- Patiromer group: 84.1% remained on therapy
- Between-group absolute difference: 16.0% (95% CI 1.8–30.2; P = 0.0504)

**Comparison to non-HF subgroup:**
- Placebo: 64.6% persistence
- Patiromer: 86.9% persistence (P = 0.0006)
- No significant interaction between HF and non-HF groups (P = 0.8085)

**Hyperkalaemia Outcomes (HF patients, serum K+ ≥5.5 mmol/L):**
- 42 (61%) placebo recipients
- 28 (44%) patiromer recipients

**Study drug discontinuation due to hyperkalaemia:**
- 16 (23%) placebo patients
- 7 (11%) patiromer patients

**Spironolactone Dosing (HF group):**
- 50 mg daily achievement: 59% placebo vs. 76% patiromer
- Cumulative dose difference: 387.5 mg higher in patiromer group (LS mean)

---

## Blood Pressure Outcomes

**Systolic AOBP Reduction (baseline to week 12):**

HF subgroup:
- Placebo: 9.0 mmHg reduction (P < 0.0001 vs. baseline)
- Patiromer: 7.8 mmHg reduction (P < 0.0001)
- Between-group difference: not significant (P = 0.60)

Non-HF subgroup:
- Placebo: 12.1 mmHg reduction
- Patiromer: 14.4 mmHg reduction
- Between-group difference: not significant (P = 0.36)

---

## Safety Findings

**Adverse Events (HF subgroup):**
- Placebo: 48% experienced AEs (33/69 patients)
- Patiromer: 46% experienced AEs (29/63 patients)

**Most Common AE Class:** Gastrointestinal disorders
- Placebo: 17% (12 patients); Patiromer: 11% (7 patients)
- Diarrhoea: 6% placebo vs. 3% patiromer

**Serious Adverse Events:**
- 3 (2%) in HF subgroup overall
- 1 death (aortic rupture) in placebo group

**Renal Function Changes:**
- eGFR decline >30%: 16% placebo vs. 24% patiromer
- No patients with baseline eGFR <30 experienced >50% decline
- No dialysis initiations during study

**Electrolyte Monitoring:**
- Serum magnesium <0.58 mmol/L: 3 HF patients (1 placebo, 2 patiromer); none <0.49 mmol/L threshold
- Serum calcium >2.62 mmol/L: 4 HF patients (2 per treatment group)

## Discussion / Conclusions

The analysis demonstrated that patiromer increased the proportion of patients who remained on spironolactone over 12 weeks of treatment. In this mixed heart failure population (both reduced and preserved ejection fraction), the potassium binder enabled safer mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist use—a critical issue since hyperkalaemia fear commonly prevents optimal HF therapy implementation.

**Clinical Context:** Hyperkalaemia represents a major barrier to RAAS inhibitor use in HFrEF, where mortality benefits are well established. The study population's median NT-proBNP concentrations (placebo 730 ng/L, patiromer 1062 ng/L) confirmed significant cardiac dysfunction.

**Limitations:**
- Predominantly white enrollment (99% HF group)
- Short 12-week follow-up insufficient for long-term cardiovascular/renal outcome assessment
- Not powered to detect NT-proBNP changes
- Unable to compare HFrEF versus HFpEF subpopulations separately

**Conclusions:** Findings were consistent with the overall AMBER trial results and support patiromer's role in enabling spironolactone therapy in high-risk patients with resistant hypertension, advanced CKD, and heart failure.
