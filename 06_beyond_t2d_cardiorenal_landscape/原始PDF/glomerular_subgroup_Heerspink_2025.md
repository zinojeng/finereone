# Design and baseline characteristics of the FIND-CKD randomized trial

**Trial registration:** ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05047263; EU CTR 2023-506897-11-00.
Funded by Bayer AG, Berlin, Germany.

## Abstract

**Background.** Finerenone, a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA), improved kidney and cardiovascular outcomes in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes (T2D) in phase 3 outcome trials. FIND-CKD investigates the effect of finerenone, in addition to standard of care, on the progression of kidney disease in adults with CKD without diabetes.

**Methods.** FIND-CKD (NCT05047263 and EU CTR 2023-506897-11-00) is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trial in patients with CKD of non-diabetic aetiology. Adults with a urinary albumin:creatinine ratio (UACR) ≥200–≤3500 mg/g and an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) ≥25–<90 mL/min/1.73 m² receiving a maximum tolerated dose of a renin–angiotensin system (RAS) inhibitor were randomized 1:1 to once-daily placebo or finerenone 10 or 20 mg (depending on eGFR: 10 mg if eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73 m²; 20 mg if eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²). The primary efficacy outcome is total eGFR slope, defined as the mean annual rate of change in eGFR from baseline to month 32. Secondary efficacy outcomes include a combined cardiorenal composite (time to kidney failure, sustained ≥57% decrease in eGFR, hospitalization for heart failure or cardiovascular death), as well as separate kidney and cardiovascular composite outcomes. Adverse events are recorded to assess tolerability and safety.

**Results.** Across 24 countries, 3231 patients were screened and 1584 were randomized to study treatment. The most common causes of CKD were chronic glomerulonephritis (57.0%) and hypertensive/ischaemic nephropathy (29.0%). Immunoglobulin A (IgA) nephropathy was the most common glomerulonephritis (26.3% of the total population). At baseline, mean eGFR was 46.7 mL/min/1.73 m² and median UACR was 818.9 mg/g. Diuretics were used by 282 participants (17.8%), statins by 851 (53.7%) and calcium channel blockers by 794 (50.1%). SGLT2 inhibitors were used in 16.9% of patients; these individuals had a similar mean eGFR (45.6 vs 46.8 mL/min/1.73 m²) and a slightly higher median UACR (871.9 vs 808.3 mg/g) compared with those not using SGLT2 inhibitors at baseline.

**Conclusions.** FIND-CKD is the first phase 3 trial of finerenone in patients with CKD of non-diabetic aetiology.

**Keywords:** clinical trial, eGFR slope, finerenone, non-diabetic chronic kidney disease, immunoglobulin A nephropathy

## Key Learning Points

**What was known:**
- Finerenone reduced the risk of kidney failure and improved cardiovascular outcomes in patients with CKD associated with T2D in the phase 3 FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD outcome trials.
- Finerenone acts by targeting pathways of pathophysiological sodium retention, inflammation and fibrosis mediated by mineralocorticoid receptor overactivation in the vasculature, heart and kidney.
- Finerenone may also be beneficial in improving kidney and cardiovascular outcomes in patients with CKD without diabetes; this hypothesis is being tested in FIND-CKD.

**This study adds:**
- FIND-CKD is the first phase 3 trial studying the efficacy of finerenone in patients with CKD of non-diabetic aetiology, including hypertension and chronic glomerulonephritis (e.g. IgA nephropathy and focal segmental glomerulosclerosis).
- The primary endpoint in FIND-CKD is the estimated GFR slope at 32 months.
- The trial uses a hybrid decentralized clinical trial (DCT) model integrating telemedicine and remote data collection.

**Potential impact:**
- FIND-CKD will determine the efficacy of finerenone for slowing kidney disease progression in patients with CKD without diabetes, providing a potential expanded role for finerenone beyond T2D.

## Materials and Methods

### Study design
FIND-CKD is a multicentre, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group phase 3 study in patients with non-diabetic CKD of different disease aetiologies at risk of progressive eGFR decline. The primary objective is to demonstrate whether finerenone added to standard of care (optimized RAS blockade) is superior to placebo in slowing kidney disease progression in patients with CKD without diabetes. The study is registered (NCT05047263; EU CTR 2023-506897-11-00) and estimated to be completed in February 2026.

### Study patients
Patients ≥18 years of age with a clinical diagnosis of CKD were eligible if they had either:
- eGFR ≥25–<60 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR ≥200–<500 mg/g, **OR**
- eGFR ≥25–<90 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR ≥500–≤3500 mg/g.

Additional inclusion: stable and maximum tolerated dose of ACEI or ARB for ≥4 weeks and serum potassium ≤4.8 mmol/L.

**Key exclusion criteria:** established diagnosis of type 1 diabetes, T2D or glycated haemoglobin (HbA1c) ≥6.5%; systolic BP ≥160 or diastolic BP ≥100 mmHg; symptomatic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction with class 1A indication for steroidal MRAs; autosomal dominant or autosomal recessive polycystic kidney disease; lupus nephritis or ANCA-associated vasculitis or any other kidney disease requiring immunosuppressive therapy within 6 months prior to enrolment.

To ensure a pre-specified ratio for a population at risk of progressive renal function decline, the number of participants with eGFR ≥25–<60 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR ≥200–<500 mg/g was capped at ≈10% of the total population.

### Randomization and study treatment
Patients were randomized 1:1 to finerenone (10 or 20 mg) or placebo (sham 10 or 20 mg) once daily in a blinded fashion on top of stable, maximum tolerated ACEI or ARB therapy. Randomization was stratified by screening UACR (≤1000 mg/g versus >1000 mg/g) and baseline SGLT2 inhibitor use (yes/no). Starting dose depended on eGFR: lower dose of 10 mg if eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73 m²; higher (target) dose of 20 mg if eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m². Finerenone is up- or down-titrated based on potassium and eGFR.

### Study follow-up
Visits scheduled at screening, baseline (day 1), month 1 and month 3, then every 3 months up to month 12; thereafter 4-month visits until the end of study. Minimum planned treatment duration is 32 months; maximum ≈49 months. An additional visit is scheduled 4 weeks after study drug discontinuation to assess off-drug effects. The first patient was enrolled in September 2021; enrolment completed in May 2023.

### Efficacy and safety assessments
eGFR calculated using the 2009 CKD-EPI equation (creatinine, age, sex, race); pre-specified sensitivity analyses using 2021 equations; cystatin C collected. UACR measured at screening, day 1, months 1, 3, 6, 12 and 24, end of treatment and follow-up. Health status assessed via EQ-5D-5L. Hyperkalaemia is an adverse event of special interest; serum potassium levels >5.5 mmol/L and >6.0 mmol/L are monitored.

### Statistical analysis
A total sample size of 1500 patients (750 per arm) was calculated to have >90% power to demonstrate superiority of finerenone to placebo for total eGFR slope from baseline (day 1) to month 32 at two-sided significance level of 5%. Power calculation assumes a 0.7 mL/min/1.73 m²/year total eGFR slope difference at month 32. Screening ≈3160 patients was planned to achieve 1580 randomized (allowing for ≈5% discontinuation).

**Primary outcome:** total eGFR slope (mean annual rate of change in eGFR from baseline to month 32), modelled with a two-slope linear spline mixed effects model with fixed change point at month 3 to differentiate acute and chronic eGFR slope phases.

**Secondary outcomes:** combined cardiorenal endpoint (end-stage kidney disease, sustained decrease in eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73 m², sustained ≥57% decrease in eGFR from baseline, hospitalization for heart failure, or death from cardiovascular-related causes); kidney composite (kidney failure or sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease); cardiovascular composite (CV death or hospitalization for heart failure). Tested via stratified log-rank and stratified Cox proportional hazards. Hierarchical confirmatory testing order: annual eGFR slope → composite cardiorenal → composite kidney → composite cardiovascular.

## Discussion (key points)

- FIND-CKD addresses the knowledge gap left by FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD (which excluded patients without diabetes), evaluating efficacy and safety of finerenone in CKD without diabetes.
- Mineralocorticoid receptor activation contributes to sodium/water retention, inflammation and fibrosis. Steroidal MRAs (spironolactone, eplerenone) reduced albuminuria and stabilized eGFR decline in small studies but increase potassium/hyperkalaemia risk, limiting use; neither is approved for CKD treatment. Finerenone has distinct pharmacological properties (short half-life 2–3 h, distinct receptor binding) associated with lower hyperkalaemia risk than steroidal MRAs.
- The primary endpoint is change in eGFR (eGFR slope) over 32 months — a valid surrogate; a meta-analysis of 66 RCTs showed strong association between treatment effects on eGFR slope and effects on the 57% GFR decline/kidney failure endpoint (median HR 0.74, 95% Bayesian prediction interval 0.60, 0.89 for a 0.75 mL/min/1.73 m²/year treatment effect). The 32-month minimum treatment duration was chosen based on FIDELIO-DKD post hoc analysis of total slope difference at 32 months.
- A composite endpoint of ≥57% GFR decline or kidney failure is a key secondary outcome, though the trial is not powered to detect statistically significant treatment effects on it. FIND-CKD uses a ≥57% GFR decline threshold (vs ≥40% in FIDELIO-DKD) so that acute haemodynamically-mediated GFR reductions do not confound detection of treatment effects.
- FIND-CKD recruited a broad population with different underlying CKD aetiologies. Compared with DAPA-CKD and EMPA-KIDNEY non-diabetic subgroups, FIND-CKD had higher mean eGFR (46.7 vs 41.7 vs 38.7), higher proportion of glomerulonephritis, and highest IgAN proportion (26.3%) — likely reflecting the larger Asian population (glomerulonephritis more common in Asia). There were 417 participants with IgAN.
- SGLT2 inhibitors were used in 17% of the cohort (>4 weeks pre-enrolment). Guidelines were updated during recruitment to recommend SGLT2 inhibitors added to ACEI/ARB. The ongoing CONFIDENCE trial (NCT05254002) prospectively assesses monotherapy vs combination of empagliflozin and finerenone in 807 adults with T2D and CKD.
- Diuretics used in only 18% (much lower than other recent CKD progression trials), reflecting the high proportion of Asian patients and exclusion of symptomatic HFrEF patients.
- A novel aspect is the offer to participate in the decentralized clinical trial (DCT) model with remote study procedures via telemedicine and wearable devices.

## Conclusion
FIND-CKD is designed to evaluate the efficacy, safety and tolerability of finerenone compared with placebo in patients with CKD without diabetes. Should a beneficial effect on slowing GFR decline be established, it will expand the role of finerenone for kidney protection beyond T2D.
