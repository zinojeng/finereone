## Abstract / Scope

Scoping review of SGLT2 inhibitor efficacy in IgA nephropathy (IgAN) across five
outcome domains: acute eGFR changes, chronic eGFR decline, proteinuria reduction,
kidney outcomes, and mortality.

## Included studies (n = 5)

- 2 randomized controlled trials (IgAN subgroups): DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY
- 3 observational / real-world cohorts (total ~548 IgAN patients)

## Randomized controlled trial data

### DAPA-CKD (IgAN subgroup)
- Dapagliflozin 137 vs. placebo 133
- Baseline eGFR: 43.8 mL/min/1.73 m^2
- Median UACR: 900 mg/g
- Follow-up: 2.1 years
- Composite outcome: >=50% eGFR decline, ESKD, or death from renal/CV cause

### EMPA-KIDNEY (IgAN subgroup)
- Empagliflozin 413 vs. placebo 404
- Baseline eGFR: 43.3 mL/min/1.73 m^2
- Median UACR: 662 mg/g
- Follow-up: 2.0 years
- Primary outcome: kidney disease progression or cardiovascular death

## Observational / real-world data

- 3 cohorts, ~548 IgAN patients total
- Proteinuria reductions ~20-50% over 3-12 months
- Biopsy-proven IgAN cohorts: proteinuria reduced ~23% at 3 months, ~27% at 6 months
- Sustained proteinuria reductions of ~35-48% over 12 months

## Discussion (key points)

Evidence is described as convergent: initiation of SGLT2 inhibitors is associated
with a predictable early eGFR dip that stabilizes, consistent reductions in
proteinuria over months, and slower longer-term eGFR decline.

## Conclusion (as reported)

"Evidence supporting SGLT2 inhibitors in IgA nephropathy is broad and convergent
across randomized subgroup analyses and observational/real-world studies. The
collective findings are mechanistically coherent, with an expected early eGFR dip
followed by stabilization, consistent proteinuria reductions, and signals of
slower long-term eGFR decline and kidney disease progression. While IgAN-dedicated
randomized trials remain limited, the mapped evidence supports the role of SGLT2
inhibitors as kidney-protective therapy in IgAN and highlights priorities for
future IgAN-specific research."
