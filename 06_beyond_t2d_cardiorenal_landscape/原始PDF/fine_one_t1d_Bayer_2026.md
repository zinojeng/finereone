# FDA Grants Priority Review for Finerenone (KERENDIA) in Type 1 Diabetes–Associated CKD

## Source 1 — Bayer Official Press Release (bayer.com/en/us)

**WHIPPANY, N.J., May 21, 2026 – FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Headline:** Bayer's KERENDIA (finerenone) Granted Priority Review of Supplemental New Drug Application by U.S. FDA for Treatment of Adults with Type 1 Diabetes and Chronic Kidney Disease.

**Subhead:** If approved for this investigational new use, KERENDIA (finerenone), a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid antagonist (MRA), would be the first MRA indicated for adults with type 1 diabetes and chronic kidney disease.

### Summary
Bayer announced today that the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) accepted its supplemental New Drug Application (sNDA) and granted Priority Review designation for KERENDIA (finerenone), which is being investigated for the treatment of adults with type 1 diabetes (T1D) and chronic kidney disease (CKD).

### Key Facts
- The sNDA was supported by the Phase III FINE-ONE trial, which showed KERENDIA significantly reduced urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) over six months compared with placebo when either are used alongside standard of care for adults with T1D and CKD.
  - The sNDA was also supported by pooled Phase III data from the FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD trials in adult patients with CKD associated with type 2 diabetes (T2D).
- UACR is an important marker of cardiovascular risk and kidney disease progression.
- Approximately 20–30% of people in the U.S. with T1D also have CKD, which puts them at risk of cardiovascular events and kidney failure.
- The FDA grants Priority Review designation for the evaluation of medicines that, if approved, would provide a significant improvement in the safety or effectiveness of the treatment, prevention, or diagnosis of a serious condition.
- KERENDIA is currently approved by the FDA for use in adults with CKD associated with T2D, as well as adults with heart failure with left ventricular ejection fraction (HF LVEF) ≥40%.

### Why This sNDA Acceptance Matters
If approved, KERENDIA, a non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA), would be the first MRA indicated for adults with T1D and CKD.

> "The FDA's acceptance of this application underscores the clinical importance of our ongoing program for KERENDIA, and growing evidence base, across broad patient populations in cardiovascular and kidney diseases. With five Phase III trials now having achieved their primary endpoints — including FINE‑ONE, which forms the basis of this submission — we're proud that this milestone brings us one step closer to potentially addressing unmet needs among people living with type 1 diabetes and chronic kidney disease."
> — **Carolina Aldworth, M.D., MSc, Executive Medical Director at Bayer**

FINE-ONE is the first Phase III study since the 1990s to show positive results for patients with T1D and CKD.

### KERENDIA's Currently Approved Indications
- Since 2021: approved to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death, hospitalization for heart failure (HF), non-fatal myocardial infarction, sustained eGFR decline, and end-stage kidney disease in adult patients with CKD associated with T2D.
- July 2025: FDA approval to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death, hospitalization for heart failure, and urgent heart failure visits in adults with HF LVEF ≥40%.

### FINE-ONE Clinical Trial Results
FINE-ONE (NCT05901831) is a pivotal, global, randomized, prospective, double-blind, multicenter, Phase III study in people with T1D and CKD. It enrolled **242 adult participants**, with the primary objective to demonstrate whether the addition of finerenone, 10 or 20 mg once daily, to standard of care is superior to placebo in reducing UACR over six months.

Results:
- KERENDIA significantly reduced UACR compared to placebo over the six-month study period (**LS mean change = 0.75; 95% CI [0.65 to 0.87]; P<0.001**) — i.e., approximately a 25% relative reduction in UACR vs placebo.
- Safety/tolerability were largely consistent with existing evidence in T2D and CKD.
  - Treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs): **47.1%** (finerenone) vs **49.2%** (placebo).
  - Treatment-emergent serious adverse events: **11.8%** (finerenone) vs **11.5%** (placebo).
  - Hyperkalemia (adverse event of special interest): **10.1%** (finerenone) vs **3.3%** (placebo). Treatment discontinuation due to hyperkalemia: **1.7%** vs **0%**.

Detailed FINE-ONE results were presented at the American Society of Nephrology (ASN) Kidney Week 2025 and published in the *New England Journal of Medicine* (NEJMoa2512854).

### Clinical Trial Program (FINEOVATE)
KERENDIA's program comprises 10 Phase III studies with dedicated programs in HF (MOONRAKER) and CKD (THUNDERBALL).
- MOONRAKER (HF): FINEARTS-HF; ongoing investigator-sponsored REDEFINE-HF, CONFIRMATION-HF, FINALITY-HF.
- THUNDERBALL (CKD): completed FIDELIO-DKD (NCT02540993), FIGARO-DKD (NCT02545049), FINE-ONE (NCT05901831), FIND-CKD (NCT05047263); ongoing FIONA (NCT05196035), FIONA-OLE (NCT05457283).

## Source 2 — Pharmacy Times Editorial Coverage

**Title:** FDA Grants Priority Review to Finerenone for Chronic Kidney Disease in Adults With Type 1 Diabetes
**Author:** Luke Halpern, Associate Editor
**Published:** May 25, 2026

### Summary
The FDA has granted priority review to finerenone (Kerendia; Bayer) for treating adults with type 1 diabetes and chronic kidney disease. If approved, it would be the first nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist indicated for this population.

### Key Facts and Figures (as reported by Pharmacy Times)
Disease burden:
- Approximately 20–30% of US adults with type 1 diabetes also have CKD.
- This population faces elevated cardiovascular and kidney-failure risk.

FINE-ONE trial data:
- Sample size: 242 participants.
- Primary endpoint: UACR reduction over 6 months.
- Efficacy: approximately 25% UACR reduction vs placebo (95% CI, 0.75 [0.65–0.87]; P = .0001).
- Adverse events: 47.1% (finerenone) vs 49.2% (placebo).
- Serious adverse events: 11.8% (finerenone) vs 11.5% (placebo).
- Hyperkalemia: 10.1% (finerenone) vs 3.3% (placebo).
- Treatment discontinuation due to hyperkalemia: 1.7% vs 0%.

Current approved indications:
1. Since 2021: CKD associated with type 2 diabetes.
2. July 2025: Heart failure with LVEF ≥40%.

Dispensing considerations noted:
- Contraindicated with strong CYP3A4 inhibitors; avoid grapefruit/grapefruit juice.
- Caution with moderate/weak CYP3A4 inhibitors (potassium monitoring); avoid strong/moderate CYP3A4 inducers.
- Contraindicated in adrenal insufficiency or hypersensitivity.
- Serum potassium should not exceed 5 mEq/L at initiation.
