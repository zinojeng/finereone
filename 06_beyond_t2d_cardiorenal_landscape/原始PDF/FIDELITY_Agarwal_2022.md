## Introduction

Patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes have high residual cardiorenal morbidity and mortality, despite current therapies, and the risks of progression towards kidney failure and cardiovascular events increase with severity and stage of CKD.

The FInerenone in chronic kiDney diseasE and type 2 diabetes: Combined FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD Trial programme analYsis (FIDELITY) pools these complementary studies with similar designs, assessments, and conduct.

## Study Design

Prespecified pooled efficacy and safety analysis of two phase III, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicentre clinical trials:

| Feature | FIDELIO-DKD | FIGARO-DKD |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| N | 5,734 | 7,437 |
| Primary outcome | Kidney composite | CV composite |
| Median follow-up | 2.6 years | 3.4 years |
| CKD definition | UACR 30-<300 mg/g + eGFR 25-<60 + diabetic retinopathy; or UACR 300-5000 + eGFR 25-<75 | UACR 30-<300 + eGFR 25-90; or UACR 300-5000 + eGFR ≥60 |

**Critical GCP violations**: 145 patients prospectively excluded, leaving **N = 13,026** for analysis.

## Patient Characteristics (Baseline)

- Age: 64.8 ± 9.5 years; 69.8% male
- Mean eGFR: 57.6 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Median UACR: 515 mg/g (IQR 198-1,147)
- eGFR ≥60: 39.9%; 45-<60: 26.4%; 25-<45: 32.5%
- History of CV disease: 45.6%
- Mean HbA1c: 7.7%; mean SBP: 136.7 mmHg
- On RAS inhibition: 99.8%
- SGLT-2 inhibitors at baseline: 6.7%
- GLP-1RAs at baseline: 7.2%
- Median follow-up: 3.0 years (IQR 2.3-3.8 years)

## Primary Outcomes

### Composite CV Outcome (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, or HHF)
- Finerenone: 825/6,519 (12.7%) vs Placebo: 939/6,507 (14.4%)
- **HR 0.86 (95% CI 0.78-0.95), P = 0.0018**
- 3-year risk difference: 2.2%; NNT = 46 (95% CI 29-109)

### Composite Kidney Outcome (kidney failure, sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease, or renal death)
- Finerenone: 360/6,519 (5.5%) vs Placebo: 465/6,507 (7.1%)
- **HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.67-0.88), P = 0.0002**
- 3-year risk difference: 1.7%; NNT = 60 (95% CI 38-142)

## Secondary / Exploratory Outcomes

- HHF: HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.66-0.92), P = 0.0030
- ESKD: HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.64-0.99), P = 0.040
- Sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease: HR 0.70 (95% CI 0.60-0.83), P < 0.0001
- Kidney composite (≥40% eGFR threshold): HR 0.85 (95% CI 0.77-0.93), P = 0.0004
- All-cause mortality: not significantly different, P = 0.051
- All-cause hospitalization: P = 0.087
- UACR at 4 months: 32% lower with finerenone (ratio of LS-mean 0.68; 95% CI 0.66-0.70)

## Safety

| Event | Finerenone (n=6,510) | Placebo (n=6,489) |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Any AE | 86.1% | 86.4% |
| Hyperkalaemia (investigator-reported) | 14.0% | 6.9% |
| Permanent discontinuation due to hyperkalaemia | 1.7% | 0.6% |
| Serious hyperkalaemia | 1.1% | 0.2% |
| Fatal hyperkalaemia | 0% | 0% |
| Hypokalaemia | 1.1% | 2.3% |
| Acute kidney injury | 3.4% | 3.6% |
| Serious AEs | 31.6% | 33.7% |

Mean serum K+ change at 4 months: +0.21 mmol/L (finerenone) vs +0.02 mmol/L (placebo); stable thereafter.
SBP change at 4 months: -3.2 mmHg (finerenone) vs +0.5 mmHg (placebo).

## Conclusions

Finerenone reduced the risk of clinically important cardiovascular and kidney outcomes vs. placebo across the spectrum of CKD in patients with type 2 diabetes. HHF was the main driver of cardiovascular benefit. ESKD reduced by 20%. Hyperkalaemia risk is manageable with low rates of fatal or clinically impactful events.

## Key Limitations

1. All patients had T2D — no non-diabetic CKD data
2. Exploratory pooled analysis — statistical tests not hypothesis-confirming, no multiplicity adjustment
3. Small proportion of Black patients
4. Low SGLT-2i use at baseline (6.7%) due to trial era (2015-2018 enrollment)
5. Patients with HFrEF excluded
6. Industry-sponsored (Bayer)
