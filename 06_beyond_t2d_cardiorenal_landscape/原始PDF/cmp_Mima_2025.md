# Effect of dapagliflozin on eGFR slope in IgA nephropathy based on renal pathological assessment using image analysis software

**Authors:** Akira Mima, Takahiro Nakamoto, Keishi Matsumoto, Yuta Saito, Takaaki Morikawa, Shinji Lee
**Journal:** Metabolism Open (2025), Article 100392
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.metop.2025.100392 | **PMID:** 40994844 | **PMCID:** PMC12454904

## Abstract

**Introduction.** Studies have examined the effect of dapagliflozin, a sodium-glucose co-transporter-2 (SGLT2) inhibitor, on chronic kidney disease (CKD), including immunoglobulin A nephropathy (IgAN). Dapagliflozin decreases albuminuria and slows the decline in estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR). However, its renoprotective effects may not be observed in all patients with IgAN in real-world clinical practice. In this study, the authors aimed to investigate the potential relationship between renal histopathology analyzed using imaging software and the renoprotective effects of dapagliflozin.

**Methods.** The mesangial matrix fraction (Mes Fx) in patients with IgAN was analyzed using Image-J. The relationships between eGFR slope decline, changes in urinary protein, and the degree of Mes Fx before and after dapagliflozin treatment were investigated.

**Results.** A significant correlation was evident between the degree of Mes Fx and eGFR slope decline in patients with IgAN. Furthermore, it was suggested that when the Mes Fx exceeded 10 % of the total glomerular surface area, the effect of dapagliflozin in slowing the eGFR decline was unclear.

**Conclusions.** This study has limitations, including the absence of a control group, the small data size, and its pilot study nature. Therefore, it cannot be stated unequivocally that dapagliflozin's efficacy is definitively demonstrated by Mes Fx predictive role.

**Keywords:** Immunoglobulin A nephropathy; Dapagliflozin; Mesangial matrix fraction; Image-J; Estimated glomerular filtration rate slope decline; Proteinuria

## Study Design & Methods

- **Setting/population:** Patients aged >=20 years diagnosed with IgAN after kidney biopsy at Osaka Medical and Pharmaceutical University Hospital between November 2006 and December 2021, who received dapagliflozin (10 mg/day) in addition to standard treatments (steroids, RAAS inhibitors, antiplatelet agents, tonsillectomy). Retrospective analysis of electronic medical records.
- **eGFR equation:** IDMS-traceable MDRD with 3-variable Japanese equation: 194 × serum creatinine^-1.094 × age^-0.287 × 0.739 (if female).
- **eGFR slope** = (eGFR_final – eGFR_baseline) / observation period.
- **Mesangial matrix fraction (Mes Fx):** percentage of mesangial PASM-positive area per total glomerular surface area, quantified via ImageJ (version 1.53v). All glomeruli from each patient analyzed.
- **Oxford (MEST-C) classification** scored 0–7; each specimen evaluated by >=3 blinded pathologists.
- **Statistics:** Pearson's correlation coefficient and t-tests (two-tailed); StatView v5 and Excel; significance P < 0.05.
- Laboratory/pathology change window: values 1 year before / at start of dapagliflozin vs 6 months to 1 year after.

## Key Results (numbers verbatim from source)

- **Mes Fx vs MEST-C score:** No association (r = 0.332, P = 0.179; Fig. 2).
- **Mes Fx vs change in eGFR** (before/after dapagliflozin): significant negative correlation, **r = −0.823, P < 0.001** (Fig. 3A).
- **Mes Fx vs eGFR slope (ΔeGFR):** negative correlation, **r = −0.638, P = 0.004** (Fig. 3B).
- **Threshold finding:** Effect of dapagliflozin in slowing eGFR decline (positive ΔeGFR) was significant when Mes Fx was **below 10 %**; when Mes Fx > 10 % of total glomerular surface area, eGFR slopes before/after dapagliflozin were negative (renoprotective effect not achieved).
- **Mes Fx vs change in proteinuria:** no relationship (r = −0.022, P = 0.931; Fig. 3C).
- **Change in urinary erythrocytes:** not correlated with dapagliflozin treatment (r = −0.377, P = 0.136; Fig. 3D).
- **Tubulointerstitial markers vs Mes Fx** (before/after dapagliflozin):
  - Urinary NAG: r = 0.104, P = 0.747 (Fig. 4A).
  - Urinary β2 microglobulin: r = 0.266, P = 0.488 (Fig. 4B).

## Discussion — key points & contextual comparators cited

- First evidence that image analysis of Mes Fx could predict the renoprotective effect of dapagliflozin in IgAN. When Mes Fx > 10 %, eGFR slopes before/after dapagliflozin were negative (renoprotection not achieved).
- Limitation stressed: no control group of IgAN patients not receiving dapagliflozin; the negative Mes Fx–eGFR correlation may partly reflect the natural disease course independent of dapagliflozin.
- Glomerular lesions appeared independent of tubulointerstitial lesions (no correlation between glomerular sclerosis and urinary NAG / β2 MG).
- **Cited external trials (comparators, not this study's data):**
  - **DAPA-CKD:** In 1398 non-diabetic CKD patients, dapagliflozin on hard renal endpoints (sustained eGFR decline >50 %, ESRD, or renal/CV death) — hazard ratio 0.5 (95 % CI 0.35–0.72). A sub-analysis included 270 IgAN patients.
  - **DIAMOND** (crossover RCT): 53 non-diabetic CKD patients (24-h urinary protein >500 and ≤3500 mg, mean eGFR 58 ± 23 mL/min/1.73 m²). Transient eGFR decrease (−6.6 mL/min/1.73 m²), recovered on discontinuation; no effect on proteinuria (dapagliflozin 0.9 % change vs placebo 0.9 %).
  - **EMPA-KIDNEY:** 6609 CKD patients (eGFR 20–45, or 45–90 with UACR >200), including 817 IgAN patients; empagliflozin reduced risk of renal disease progression or CV death vs placebo.
- Proposed mechanism: SGLT2 inhibition regulates intraglomerular pressure via tubuloglomerular feedback (TGF), reducing glomerular hyperfiltration; mechanical-stress signaling (TGF-β, angiotensin II) drives extracellular matrix synthesis and podocyte hypertrophy.
- Initial eGFR "dip" after dapagliflozin recovers by ~2 months; the 7.9 ± 4.0 month observation window minimizes dip effect.

## Conclusion

Quantification of the mesangial matrix fraction using ImageJ may serve as a predictor of dapagliflozin renoprotective response in IgAN, with a possible threshold around 10 % of total glomerular surface area. Findings are hypothesis-generating only given the pilot design, small sample (n = 18), retrospective single-center nature, and absence of a control group.
