## Abstract / Objective

The study examined whether drug-induced short-term albuminuria reduction predicts long-term renal protection. A meta-analysis of clinical trials was conducted to correlate placebo-corrected drug effects on albuminuria with effects on ESRD (end-stage renal disease) outcomes, minimizing the selection bias inherent in observational studies.

## Methods

- **Databases:** MEDLINE and EMBASE (1950–April 2014).
- **Inclusion criteria for RCTs:**
  - Mean follow-up ≥1000 patient-years
  - Reported ESRD outcomes
  - Measured albuminuria at baseline and follow-up
- **Data extraction:** Two independent reviewers, standardized protocol; quality via Jadad scores (range 4–5).
- **Statistics:** Univariate meta-regression of placebo-adjusted treatment effects on albuminuria vs. ESRD; subgroup analyses across drug classes and patient characteristics.

## Results — Study Characteristics

- **21 randomized controlled trials** included
- **78,342 patients**
- **4,183 ESRD events**
- Median time to first albuminuria measurement: **6 months**
- Publication range: 1994–2013
- Median albuminuria reduction: **19.2%** (range of treatment effects −1.3% to −32.1%)
- Overall ESRD risk reduction: **17% (95% CI, 8% to 25%)**

## Results — Primary Finding

> "For each 30% reduction in albuminuria, the risk of ESRD decreased by **23.7% (95% CI, 11.4% to 34.2%; P=0.001)**"

## Subgroup Analyses

Association remained consistent regardless of:
- Drug class (RAAS inhibitors vs. non-RAAS inhibitors; **P=0.73**)
- Baseline albuminuria levels
- Baseline eGFR
- Systolic blood pressure
- Diabetic vs. nondiabetic populations

## Sensitivity Analysis

Excluding the SHARP trial (contributed 2,141 events):
> "per 30% reduction in albuminuria, the reduction in risk of ESRD was **27.4% (95% CI, 0.7% to 46.9%; P=0.05)**"

## Study Population Details

**Fourteen trials** tested RAAS inhibitors:
- 5 studies: ACE inhibitor
- 4 studies: ARB
- 3 studies: dual RAAS blockade
- 2 studies: additional RAAS combinations

**Seven trials** tested non-RAAS interventions:
- 2 lipid-lowering
- 2 dietary protein restriction
- 2 intensive blood pressure control
- 1 glycosaminoglycan

**Patient characteristics across trials:**
- Age range: 12–68 years
- Male proportion: 28%–93%
- Baseline eGFR: 19–92 ml/min per 1.73 m²
- Baseline albuminuria/proteinuria: 7.2 to 3,500 mg/24h
- Diabetes status: 0%–100%

## Notable Findings on Failed Trials

- ALTITUDE: 11% albuminuria reduction → predicted only ~8% ESRD reduction.
- ONTARGET: 7% reduction → "unlikely to translate into clinically relevant reductions in risk of ESRD."

## Conclusions

- "Short-term albuminuria reduction is associated with long-term renal protection across different interventions and populations."
- Albuminuria can serve as a valid surrogate endpoint for ESRD in clinical trials, with the caveat: "albuminuria reduction can only be a substitute for renal protection when the intervention is otherwise safe."
- A ~30% reduction in albuminuria appears necessary to confer clinically meaningful renoprotection; smaller reductions (7%–11%) are unlikely to translate into detectable ESRD risk reduction.
- Supports using albuminuria as a surrogate for initial drug approval, provided long-term trials confirm renoprotective efficacy on hard clinical endpoints.
