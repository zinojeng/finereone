# Efficacy and Safety of Finerenone Across the Ejection Fraction Spectrum in Heart Failure With Mildly Reduced or Preserved Ejection Fraction: A Prespecified Analysis of the FINEARTS-HF Trial

Docherty KF, Henderson AD, Jhund PS, Claggett BL, Desai AS, Mueller K, Viswanathan P, Scalise A, Lam CSP, Senni M, Shah SJ, Voors AA, Zannad F, Pitt B, Vaduganathan M, Solomon SD, McMurray JJV.
*Circulation.* 2025;151:45–58. DOI: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.124.072011. Published January 7, 2025.
Correspondence: John J.V. McMurray, University of Glasgow.

## Abstract

**BACKGROUND:** The effects of treatments for heart failure (HF) may vary among patients according to left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF). In FINEARTS-HF (Finerenone Trial to Investigate Efficacy and Safety Superior to Placebo in Patients With Heart Failure), the nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist finerenone reduced the risk of cardiovascular death and total worsening HF events in patients with HF with mildly reduced or preserved ejection fraction. We examined the effect of finerenone according to LVEF in FINEARTS-HF.

**METHODS:** FINEARTS-HF was a randomized, placebo-controlled trial examining the efficacy and safety of finerenone in patients with HF and LVEF ≥40%. The treatment effect of finerenone was examined in prespecified analyses according to LVEF categories (<50%, ≥50% to <60%, and ≥60%) and with LVEF as a continuous variable. The primary outcome was a composite of total (first and recurrent) worsening HF events and cardiovascular death.

**RESULTS:** Baseline LVEF data were available for 5993 of the 6001 participants in FINEARTS-HF. Mean and median LVEF were 53±8% and 53% (IQR, 46%–58%), respectively. LVEF was <50% in 2172 (36%), between 50% and <60% in 2674 (45%), and ≥60% in 1147 (19%). Patients with higher LVEF were older, were more commonly female, were less likely to have a history of coronary artery disease, and more frequently had a history of hypertension and chronic kidney disease compared with those with a lower LVEF. Finerenone reduced the risk of cardiovascular death and total HF events consistently across LVEF categories (LVEF <50% rate ratio, 0.84 [95% CI, 0.68–1.03]; LVEF ≥50% to <60% rate ratio, 0.80 [0.66–0.97]; and LVEF ≥60% rate ratio, 0.94 [0.70–1.25]; Pinteraction=0.70). There was no modification of the benefit of finerenone across the range of LVEF when analyzed as a continuous variable (Pinteraction=0.28). There was a similar consistent effect of finerenone on reducing the total number of worsening HF events (continuous Pinteraction=0.26).

**CONCLUSIONS:** In patients with HF with mildly reduced or preserved ejection fraction, finerenone reduced the risk of cardiovascular death and worsening HF events, irrespective of LVEF.

**REGISTRATION:** ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04435626; EudraCT 2020-000306-29.

**Key Words:** aldosterone; heart failure; mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists; therapeutics

## Clinical Perspective

**What Is New?**
- Previous post hoc analyses of randomized clinical trials in patients with heart failure with mildly reduced or preserved ejection fraction have suggested that the benefits of neurohumoral modulating therapies are attenuated or absent at higher LVEF.
- In this prespecified analysis of FINEARTS-HF, the nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist finerenone reduced the risk of cardiovascular death and worsening HF events consistently across the entire range of LVEF in patients with HFmrEF or HFpEF.

**What Are the Clinical Implications?**
- These data support that finerenone should be considered as a foundational treatment for HFmrEF or HFpEF, regardless of LVEF.

## Methods

FINEARTS-HF was a prospective, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, event-driven trial of finerenone vs placebo in patients with HFmrEF or HFpEF. Ethics committees of the 653 participating institutions in 37 countries approved the protocol.

**Key inclusion criteria:** age ≥40 years; symptomatic HF (NYHA class II–IV); diuretic treatment for ≥30 days before randomization; LVEF ≥40% with evidence of structural heart disease (left atrial enlargement or LV hypertrophy) within 12 months of screening; elevated natriuretic peptides (NT-proBNP ≥300 pg/mL [or BNP ≥100 pg/mL] in sinus rhythm; NT-proBNP ≥900 pg/mL [or BNP ≥300 pg/mL] in atrial fibrillation). Both ambulatory and hospitalized patients eligible. Patients with previous LVEF <40% improved to ≥40% were eligible.

**Key exclusion criteria:** eGFR <25 mL·min·1.73 m²; serum/plasma potassium >5.0 mmol/L at screening or randomization; symptomatic hypotension with mean systolic BP <90 mm Hg.

**Treatment/dosing:** 1:1 randomization to finerenone or placebo. Starting dose 10 mg once daily if eGFR ≤60 mL·min·1.73 m² (max maintenance 20 mg); starting dose 20 mg once daily if eGFR >60 mL·min·1.73 m² (max maintenance 40 mg).

**Ejection fraction:** LVEF available in 5993 of 6001 patients (>99%). Analyzed in groups (<50%, ≥50%–<60%, ≥60%) and as a continuous variable; sensitivity analysis added ≥60%–<70% and ≥70% categories.

**Outcomes:** Primary outcome = composite of total (first and recurrent) HF events (HF hospitalization or urgent HF visit) and cardiovascular death. Prespecified secondary outcomes: total number of HF events; improvement in NYHA class baseline→12 months; change in KCCQ Total Symptom Score at 6, 9, 12 months; composite kidney end point (sustained eGFR decline ≥50% over ≥4 weeks, or sustained eGFR decline <15 mL·min·1.73 m², or dialysis/renal transplantation); all-cause death.

**Statistics:** Total (first and recurrent) events analyzed with semiparametric proportional-rates models (Lin et al); time-to-first-event with Cox models; stratified by geographic region. Continuous LVEF modeled as spline with 3 knots (10th, 50th, 90th quantiles). Adjustment for age, sex, eGFR, NYHA class, heart rate, systolic BP, BMI, (log)NT-proBNP, and history of type 2 diabetes, previous HF hospitalization, atrial fibrillation, and myocardial infarction. Analyses in Stata v18; P<0.05 nominally significant.

## Discussion (key points)

- FINEARTS-HF is the first trial to show a drug targeting a neurohumoral pathway can reduce morbidity and mortality and improve symptoms in HFmrEF/HFpEF. The beneficial effect of finerenone on cardiovascular death and total worsening HF events was consistent across the LVEF range (≥40%).
- Contrasts with prior neurohumoral therapies (candesartan/CHARM, sacubitril-valsartan/PARAGON-HF, spironolactone/TOPCAT) where benefit appeared attenuated or absent at higher (normal) LVEF; SGLT2 inhibitors were consistent across LVEF.
- Although benefit appeared attenuated at LVEF >70% on visual spline assessment, heterogeneity tests were nonsignificant; only 108 patients had LVEF >70% (41 primary events; 1.8% of patients), limiting certainty.
- Apparent kidney-outcome interaction and hyperkalemia interaction at high LVEF attributed to small event numbers / play of chance.
- Proposed mechanisms for consistent benefit: finerenone's greater MR selectivity/binding affinity and balanced cardiac-vs-kidney tissue distribution vs spironolactone/eplerenone; higher NT-proBNP in high-LVEF FINEARTS-HF patients (531 pg/mL sinus rhythm; 1697 pg/mL AF) vs pooled HFmrEF/HFpEF trials (~375 and ~1470 pg/mL) suggesting greater neurohumoral activation; antifibrotic mechanism (hypertension-related myocardial fibrosis); relative exclusion of undiagnosed amyloid cardiomyopathy.
- Ongoing trials: CONFIRMATION-HF (NCT06024746), REDEFINE-HF (NCT06008197).

**Limitations:** LVEF investigator-reported, not core-lab verified; LVEF could be up to 12 months old at randomization; requirement for elevated natriuretic peptides limits inference in low/normal NT-proBNP patients.

## Conclusions

In the randomized, placebo-controlled FINEARTS-HF trial, the nonsteroidal MRA finerenone reduced the risk of cardiovascular death and worsening HF events consistently across the range of LVEF in patients with HFmrEF or HFpEF.
