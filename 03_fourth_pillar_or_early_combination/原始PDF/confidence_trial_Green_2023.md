# CONFIDENCE Trial Design (Green et al., 2023, Nephrology Dialysis Transplantation)

> NOTE: Fulltext is paywalled at OUP. Text below was extracted from the publisher HTML page via WebFetch and the abstract was cross-verified via Crossref metadata. Numbers should be re-checked against the original PDF where load-bearing.

## Abstract (verbatim from Crossref)

**Background:** Despite available interventions, people with type 2 diabetes (T2D) remain at risk of chronic kidney disease (CKD). Finerenone, a potent and selective nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist, and sodium–glucose cotransporter 2 inhibitors (SGLT2is) can reduce both kidney and cardiovascular risks in people with CKD and T2D. Here we outline the design of a study to investigate whether dual therapy with finerenone and an SGLT2i is superior to either agent alone.

**Methods:** CONFIDENCE (NCT05254002) is a randomized, controlled, double-blind, double-dummy, international, multicenter, three-armed, parallel-group, 7.5- to 8.5-month, Phase 2 study in 807 adults with T2D, stage 2–3 CKD and a urine albumin:creatinine ratio (UACR) ≥300–<5000 mg/g. The primary objective is to demonstrate that 6 months of dual therapy comprising finerenone and the SGLT2i empagliflozin is superior for reducing albuminuria versus either agent alone. Interventions will be once-daily finerenone 10 mg or 20 mg (target dose) plus empagliflozin 10 mg, or empagliflozin 10 mg alone, or finerenone 10 mg or 20 mg (target dose) alone.

**Results:** The primary outcome is a relative change from baseline in UACR among the three groups. Secondary outcomes will further characterize efficacy and safety, including changes in estimated glomerular filtration rate and incident hyperkalemia.

**Conclusions:** CONFIDENCE is evaluating the safety, tolerability and efficacy of dual use of finerenone and an SGLT2i in adults with CKD and T2D. Should an additive effect be shown, early and efficient intervention with dual finerenone and SGLT2i therapy could slow disease progression and provide long-term benefits for people with CKD and T2D.

## Study Overview

CONFIDENCE (NCT05254002) is described as the first parallel-group, randomized controlled trial to evaluate the safety, tolerability and additive efficacy of dual finerenone and empagliflozin therapy in people with CKD and T2D.

## Introduction / Rationale

Despite existing interventions, individuals with T2D and CKD face elevated risks of kidney failure and cardiovascular events. Both finerenone (a selective nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist) and SGLT2 inhibitors independently reduce adverse kidney and cardiovascular outcomes. Whether combined therapy provides additive benefits remained unexplored; preclinical models and post hoc analyses suggested potential synergistic renoprotective effects warranting investigation.

## Study Design

- Randomized, controlled, double-blind, double-dummy Phase 2 study
- ~125 sites across 13 countries
- Duration: 7.5–8.5 months per participant; final randomization targeted for May 2023
- Sample size: ~807 randomized participants (from ~1614 screened)

## Study Population

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Age ≥18 years
- Type 2 diabetes
- CKD stages 2–3 (eGFR 30–90 mL/min/1.73 m²)
- UACR 300–<5000 mg/g
- On maximum tolerated ACE inhibitor or ARB dose

**Two consecutive parts (progressive inclusion):**
- Part A: eGFR 40–90 mL/min/1.73 m² with 24-hour ambulatory BP monitoring
- Part B: eGFR 30–90 mL/min/1.73 m² without mandatory BP monitoring (initiated after safety review of first 50 Part A participants)

## Randomization & Treatment Arms

1:1:1 randomization to:
1. Dual therapy: finerenone (10 or 20 mg once daily) + empagliflozin (10 mg once daily)
2. Finerenone monotherapy: finerenone (10 or 20 mg once daily) + empagliflozin placebo
3. SGLT2i monotherapy: empagliflozin (10 mg once daily) + finerenone placebo

**Stratification factors:**
- eGFR level (<60 vs ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²)
- UACR category (≤850 vs >850 mg/g)

**Dosing strategy:** Finerenone dosing depends on baseline eGFR (10 mg if <60; 20 mg target if ≥60). After 30 days, uptitration to 20 mg permitted if potassium ≤4.8 mmol/L and eGFR decline <30%. Downtitration allowed for safety.

## Study Visits & Assessments

- Visit schedule: Days 14, 30, 90, 180, and follow-up at Day 210
- UACR (first morning void samples collected at home, measured at all visits)
- eGFR (CKD-EPI creatinine equation; sensitivity analysis with race-free equation)
- Serum/plasma potassium (all visits; local and central monitoring)
- Blood pressure (all visits; 24-hour ambulatory monitoring in Part A)
- 12-lead ECG (screening, baseline, end-of-treatment)
- Safety and adverse event monitoring

## Primary Outcomes

Primary objective: demonstrate that dual finerenone + empagliflozin therapy is superior in reducing UACR compared with either empagliflozin or finerenone alone.

Two primary comparisons (relative UACR change from baseline at Day 180):
1. Dual vs finerenone alone
2. Dual vs empagliflozin alone

## Secondary Outcomes

- UACR changes at various timepoints
- Achievement of 30%, 40%, and 50% UACR reduction thresholds
- eGFR changes (initial and longer-term)
- Acute kidney injury incidence
- Safety: hyperkalemia incidence at defined thresholds, genital mycotic infections, symptomatic hypotension

## Sample Size Justification

Assumptions:
- Empagliflozin: ~30% UACR reduction at Day 180
- Finerenone: ~35% UACR reduction at Day 180
- Dual therapy: ~20% additional reduction versus monotherapy

Calculations: base sample sizes of 226–228 per arm achieved 80% power to detect superiority. Accounting for ~15% dropout and ~50% screening failure rates, final target of 269 participants per arm.

## Statistical Analysis Plan

- Analysis population: Full Analysis Set (all randomized participants without GCP violations) for primary outcomes; Safety Analysis Set (all randomized participants receiving ≥1 dose) for safety
- Primary analysis: repeated-measures mixed model with factors for treatment group, visit, treatment × visit interaction, stratification levels (UACR and eGFR categories), and log-transformed baseline UACR as covariate
- Pairwise treatment comparisons with 95% confidence intervals
- Two-sided t-tests at α = 0.025 per comparison (Bonferroni-Holm adjustment for multiple testing)
- UACR analyzed assuming log-normal distribution; multiple imputation for missing values
- Overall two-sided α = 0.05 (0.025 for initial hypothesis testing)

## Rationale for UACR as Primary Endpoint

UACR serves as a validated surrogate for CKD progression; a 30% decrease in albuminuria is associated with a 27% reduction in hazard of a composite kidney outcome. This enables shorter study duration and smaller sample size compared with event-based outcomes.
