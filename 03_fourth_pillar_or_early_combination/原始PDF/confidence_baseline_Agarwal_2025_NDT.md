## ABSTRACT

**Background.** Finerenone, a selective nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist, and sodium–glucose cotransporter 2 inhibitors (SGLT2is) both reduce chronic kidney disease (CKD) progression and improve kidney/cardiovascular (CV) outcomes. The CONFIDENCE (COmbinatioN effect of FInerenone anD EmpaglifloziN in participants with chronic kidney disease and type 2 diabetes using a UACR Endpoint) study (NCT05254002; EudraCT 2021-003037-11) hypothesis is that early combination of finerenone and empagliflozin, an SGLT2i, is superior to either drug alone in reducing urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) over 6 months.

**Methods.** CONFIDENCE is an ongoing, fully enrolled, randomized, controlled, double-blind, multicentre phase 2 clinical trial in adults (≥18 years of age) with CKD and type 2 diabetes (T2D), estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) of 30–90 mL/min/1.73 m² and UACR of ≥100 to <5000 mg/g. Participants taking the clinically maximum tolerated dose of a renin–angiotensin system inhibitor for >1 month at screening were eligible. Participants were randomized 1:1:1 to once-daily finerenone plus empagliflozin, finerenone plus placebo, or empagliflozin plus placebo; doses were 10 mg once daily for finerenone and 10 or 20 mg once daily for empagliflozin, depending on eGFR at baseline. Randomization was stratified by eGFR (<60 or ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²) and UACR (≤850 or >850 mg/g). The primary efficacy outcome is the relative change in UACR from baseline to Day 180.

**Results.** There were 818 participants randomized across 143 sites in 14 countries between July 2022 and August 2024. Mean (standard deviation) eGFR was 54.2 (17.1) mL/min/1.73 m². Median (interquartile range) UACR was 583 (292, 1140) mg/g. Mean (standard deviation) HbA1c was 7.3 (1.2)%. Mean systolic/diastolic blood pressure was 135.2/77.3 mmHg. Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists and insulin were used by 182 (23%) and 313 (39%) participants, respectively. Atherosclerotic CV disease, diabetic retinopathy and a history of heart failure were present in 223 (28%), 126 (16%) and 30 (4%) participants, respectively.

**Conclusions.** The CONFIDENCE trial enrolled a diverse population with CKD and T2D, and will determine the impact of simultaneous initiation of combination finerenone and an SGLT2i versus individual therapy on potentially mitigating the progression of CKD in people with T2D.

**Trial registration number:** ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05254002; EudraCT 2021-003037-11.

**Keywords:** chronic kidney disease, clinical trial, finerenone, sodium–glucose cotransporter 2 inhibitor, type 2 diabetes

## KEY LEARNING POINTS
**What was known:**
- CKD in T2D increases the risk of kidney failure and, compared with T2D alone, may more than double the rate of CV morbidity and all-cause mortality risk.
- Finerenone and SGLT2is reduce adverse kidney and CV outcomes in people with CKD and T2D.

**This study adds:**
- CONFIDENCE enrolled a contemporary cohort of participants with CKD and T2D at high risk of kidney disease progression and CV events.
- CONFIDENCE is the first randomized controlled trial to examine the effect of initial simultaneous treatment with a nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist and an SGLT2i, versus either agent alone, on UACR and safety outcomes.

**Potential impact:**
- The CONFIDENCE trial will determine the safety of simultaneous initiation, as well as the benefit of dual therapy, of finerenone and SGLT2is in people with CKD and T2D.

## INTRODUCTION (key points)
- CKD affects approximately 9% of the population worldwide, with diabetes being a leading cause of CKD. Accounts for 1.2 million deaths globally in 2017.
- The 2024 KDIGO guideline and the American Diabetes Association recommend renin–angiotensin system inhibitors, SGLT2is, the selective nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist finerenone, and a GLP-1 RA with demonstrated benefit in a population with CKD and T2D.
- Secondary analyses of FIDELITY (pooled analysis of FIGARO-DKD [NCT02545049] and FIDELIO-DKD [NCT02540993]) suggested a potential additive effect of finerenone and SGLT2is on kidney and CV outcomes in a subset of participants with CKD and T2D.
- CONFIDENCE is the first randomized controlled trial examining the safety (including on initial combined haemodynamic effects) and efficacy of combination therapy comprising finerenone and the SGLT2i empagliflozin, superior to either drug alone in reducing UACR.

## MATERIALS AND METHODS

### Study design
- CONFIDENCE is an ongoing, fully enrolled, randomized, controlled, double-blind, double-dummy, global, multicentre phase 2 clinical trial in people (≥18 years of age) with CKD and T2D.
- One key amendment (dated 24 October 2023) increased generalizability/enrolment: UACR inclusion lowered to ≥100 mg/g instead of previously ≥300 mg/g.
- CONFIDENCE comprised two consecutive parts. Part A: participants with eGFR 40–90 mL/min/1.73 m² enrolled and underwent ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) for 24 h after administration of first study drug dose on Day 1. Part B: extension to participants with eGFR as low as 30 mL/min/1.73 m² (no ABPM required).
- Participants had T2D with HbA1c <11% at screening and were treated with the maximum tolerated dose of an ACEi or ARB for >1 month at screening (Visit 1). UACR ≥100 to <5000 mg/g and eGFR of 30–90 mL/min/1.73 m².
- eGFR calculated by the 2009 CKD-EPI (CKD-EPI creatinine) equation, with a modification (year 2010) for Japanese participants.
- Exclusion criteria included bilateral clinically relevant kidney artery stenosis (>75%) or other nondiabetic kidney disease, heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (NYHA class II–IV), or an event within 90 days prior to screening of stroke, transient ischaemic cerebral attack, acute coronary syndrome or hospitalization for worsening heart failure.
- Randomization visit (Visit 2) occurred up to 2 weeks after screening and defined baseline. Stratified by eGFR (<60 or ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m²) and UACR (≤850 mg/g, >850 mg/g).
- Randomized 1:1:1 to 10 or 20 mg finerenone plus 10 mg empagliflozin, once-daily; 10 or 20 mg finerenone plus placebo; or once-daily 10 mg empagliflozin plus placebo.
- Starting dose of finerenone based on eGFR at screening: 10 mg once daily if eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73 m² and 20 mg once daily (target dose) if eGFR ≥60 mL/min/1.73 m². Lower dose can be uptitrated to 20 mg after 30 days if serum/plasma potassium ≤4.8 mmol/L and eGFR decrease is <30%.
- Primary efficacy outcome: relative change in log-transformed UACR from baseline at Day 180.
- Secondary outcomes: proportion achieving a reduction in UACR >30% from baseline to Day 180; incidence of hyperkalaemia (moderate ≥5.5 to ≤6.0 mmol/L; severe >6.0 mmol/L) and acute kidney injury; and assessment of initial and longer-term changes in eGFR.

### Study design figure (Figure 1)
- Visit 1 Screening (Day −14); Visit 2 Start of treatment (Day 0); Visit 3 (Day 14 ±2); Visit 4 (Day 30 ±4); Visit 5 (Day 90 ±5); End of treatment (Day 180 ±5); End of study/follow-up (Day 210 ±5).
- Three arms: Finerenone (10 or 20 mg once daily) + Empagliflozin (10 mg once daily); Finerenone (10 or 20 mg once daily) + Placebo; Empagliflozin (10 mg once daily) + Placebo.

## RESULTS

### Participant disposition
- In total, 1664 participants were screened. 805 did not pass screening; of those, 314 (39%) did not meet inclusion criteria of a clinical diagnosis of CKD (by eGFR, UACR and other specified criteria) and 289 (36%) met exclusion criteria of serum potassium levels >4.8 mmol/L at screening.
- 818 participants randomized across 143 sites in 14 countries. First participant randomized July 2022; randomization completed August 2024.
- 17 participants excluded from the full analysis set (N = 801) due to Good Clinical Practice violations (n = 14) or randomization errors (n = 3).
- Lower (≤850 mg/g) UACR category: n = 520 (63.6% of randomized); Higher (>850 mg/g) UACR category: n = 281 (34.4% of randomized). (Figure 2 shows 529 and 289 randomized in the respective categories.)

### Baseline demographics and clinical characteristics (full analysis set N = 801)
- Mean (SD) age 66.5 (10.3) years; 499 (62.3%) participants ≥65 years of age.
- 603 (75.3%) male, 198 (24.7%) female.
- Enrolled globally: 360 (44.9%), 225 (28.1%), 216 (27.0%) in Asia, North America and Europe, respectively.
- Race: 371 (46.3%) Asian, 352 (43.9%) White, 67 (8.4%) Black/African American; 83 (10.4%) Hispanic or Latino ethnicity.
- Smoking: 117 (14.6%) current and 257 (32.1%) former smokers; 427 (53.3%) never smoked.
- Baseline mean (SD) eGFR 54.2 (17.1) mL/min/1.73 m². eGFR categories (mL/min/1.73 m²): <30 = 27 (3.4%); ≥30 to <45 = 264 (33.0%); ≥45 to <60 = 230 (28.7%); ≥60 to 75 = 173 (21.6%); >75 = 107 (13.4%).
- Median (IQR) UACR 583.44 (291.72 to 1140.00) mg/g. Albuminuria: A1 = 2 (0.2%); A2 = 207 (25.8%); A3 300 to 1000 mg/g = 365 (45.6%); A3 >1000 mg/g = 225 (28.1%).
- Mean (SD) BMI 29.3 (6.1) kg/m². Mean (SD) SBP/DBP 135 (13) / 77 (10) mmHg. Mean (SD) HbA1c 7.3 (1.2)%. Mean (SD) serum potassium 4.48 (0.42) mmol/L.

### Medication usage (full analysis set)
- 788 (98.4%) using an ACEi or ARB. Statins in 313 (39.1%); antiplatelet agents in 319 (39.8%); centrally acting antihypertensive drugs in 37 (4.6%). Diuretics in 291 (36.3%); beta-blockers in 268 (33.5%).
- Diabetes medications: insulin 313 (39.1%); oral hypoglycaemic agents — sulfonylureas n = 189 (23.6%), DPP-4 inhibitors n = 249 (31.1%), metformin n = 481 (60.0%). GLP-1 RAs used by 182 (22.7%).

### Medical conditions
- Atherosclerotic CV disease present in 223 (27.8%) participants.
- History of heart failure prior to screening in 30 (3.7%).
- Diabetic retinopathy in 126 (15.7%).
- Atrial fibrillation in 51 (6.4%).
- Hypertension in 703 (87.8%).

### ABPM substudy
- Data monitoring committee reviewed (unblinded) ABPM data for Part A participants; safety assessment confirmed no elevated hypotension risk in the first 24 h following combined initiation of study medication. Following feedback, enrolment extended (Part A to Part B) to participants with eGFR as low as 30 mL/min/1.73 m².

## DISCUSSION (key points)
- CONFIDENCE is the first randomized clinical trial focused on efficacy in UACR-lowering, and the safety of initiating combination therapy with finerenone and empagliflozin compared with either agent alone, in participants with CKD and T2D.
- Racially and ethnically diverse: 46.3% Asian, 8.4% Black/African American, 10.4% Hispanic or Latino ethnicity.
- At baseline almost all participants (98.4%) were on an ACEi or ARB, and over one-third on statins or antiplatelet agents. Over 20% were on a GLP-1 RA at baseline (dose stable for 4 weeks; uptitrations of GLP-1 RAs not allowed post-randomization).
- Key baseline characteristics: median UACR of 583 mg/g, mean eGFR of 54 mL/min/1.73 m² and mean HbA1c of 7.3%.
- Comparison across trials (Table 2): mean eGFR similar to CREDENCE (56.2); higher than FIDELIO-DKD (44.3), EMPA-KIDNEY (36.0), DAPA-CKD (43.8), FLOW (47.0) and MOSAIC (35.0); but lower than FIGARO-DKD (67.8). Median UACR (583) similar to FLOW (568); higher than FIGARO-DKD (308) and EMPA-KIDNEY (348); lower than FIDELIO-DKD (852), CREDENCE (927), DAPA-CKD (1017) and MOSAIC (986).
- Mediation analyses cited: finerenone-induced UACR reductions (as continuous variable) mediated 84% of the treatment effect on kidney outcomes in pooled FIDELIO-DKD/FIGARO-DKD; change in UACR with finerenone mediated 37% of treatment effect for CV outcomes. UACR mediated empagliflozin treatment effect on kidney outcomes by 31% in EMPA-REG OUTCOME; canagliflozin on kidney outcomes by 24% in CANVAS. In CANVAS, UACR mediated 42% of the canagliflozin effect on the kidney endpoint in participants with baseline UACR ≥30 mg/g, and 7% in those with baseline UACR <30 mg/g.

## Conclusions
CONFIDENCE is the first trial to examine combination therapy comprising finerenone and an SGLT2i in people with CKD and T2D, and will provide the evidence to determine the potential role of simultaneous initiation of finerenone and SGLT2is to better inform the care of people in this population.
