# Empagliflozin in Patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (EMPA-KIDNEY)

**The EMPA-KIDNEY Collaborative Group** — N Engl J Med. 2023 Jan 12;388(2):117-127. doi:10.1056/NEJMoa2204233. PMID:36331190.

> 全文來源:Europe PMC Funders Author Manuscript 版本(作者接受稿,CC BY-ND 4.0),PMC 可得。以下摘要與關鍵數字已對照 PDF 原文肉眼驗證。

## Abstract

**Background** — This study, the EMPA-KIDNEY trial, was designed to assess the effects of empagliflozin in a broad range of patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) at risk of progression.

**Methods** — The investigators randomly assigned 6609 participants to empagliflozin (10 mg once daily) versus matching placebo. Eligibility required an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) of ≥20 to <45 ml/minute/1.73 m² (irrespective of albuminuria); or ≥45 to <90 ml/minute/1.73 m² with a urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (ACR) of ≥200 mg/g. The primary outcome was a composite of kidney disease progression (end-stage kidney disease, a sustained eGFR <10 ml/minute/1.73 m², a sustained decline in eGFR of ≥40%, or a renal death) or death from cardiovascular causes.

**Results** — During a median of 2.0 years of follow-up, a primary outcome event occurred in **432 of 3304 patients (13.1%)** in the empagliflozin group and in **558 of 3305 patients (16.9%)** in the placebo group (**hazard ratio 0.72; 95% CI 0.64 to 0.82; P<0.001**), with consistent results in those with or without diabetes and across the range of eGFR studied. There were fewer hospitalizations from any cause in the empagliflozin group (**HR 0.86; 95% CI 0.78 to 0.95; P=0.003**), but no statistically significant effect on the composite of hospitalization for heart failure or cardiovascular death (**4.0% vs 4.6%**), or on death from any cause (**4.5% vs 5.1%**). The rates of serious adverse events were broadly similar in the two groups.

**Conclusions** — Empagliflozin reduced the risk of the composite outcome of kidney disease progression or cardiovascular death in a wide range of patients at risk of CKD progression. (Funded by Boehringer Ingelheim, Eli Lilly and others; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT03594110; EudraCT 2017-002971-24.)

## Background / Rationale (verbatim key points)

- CKD is often progressive; decreased GFR and albuminuria are key risk factors for developing kidney failure. Slowing progression and avoiding dialysis/transplant is highly desirable.
- In diabetic kidney disease with increased albuminuria, large placebo-controlled trials showed that RAS inhibitors, SGLT2 inhibitors, and the non-steroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist finerenone all reduced the risk of progression to kidney failure.
- Globally, the majority of people with CKD have low levels of albuminuria (ACR <300 mg/g) and do not have diabetes; therefore studying a wide range of CKD patients has particular public health importance.
- A prespecified subgroup analysis of dapagliflozin (in CKD with ACR ≥200 mg/g) found kidney benefits extended to patients without diabetes, but limited information existed for eGFR <30 ml/min/1.73 m² or for the wider range of CKD patients.
- EMPA-KIDNEY (multicenter, international, randomized, parallel-group, double-blind, placebo-controlled) aimed to include large numbers of patients without diabetes, patients with eGFR <30 ml/min/1.73 m², and patients with low levels of proteinuria.

## Methods (key design details)

- **Design/oversight:** Steering Committee-led; sponsor Boehringer Ingelheim; independent Data Monitoring Committee reviewed unblinded data with a protocol-defined formal interim analysis for efficacy.
- **Sites:** Conducted at **241 centers in eight countries**.
- **Participants:** Adults with race-adjusted CKD-EPI eGFR ≥20 but <45 ml/min/1.73 m² (irrespective of albuminuria); or eGFR ≥45 but <90 ml/min/1.73 m² with urinary ACR ≥200 mg/g at screening. Required a clinically appropriate dose of single-agent RAS-inhibitor (unless not tolerated/indicated per investigator judgment). Patients with or without diabetes eligible. Polycystic kidney disease and kidney transplant excluded.
- **Procedures:** Pre-randomization run-in phase with 15-week supply of once-daily placebo tablets; after ≥6 weeks run-in, willing participants had central blood/urine samples collected before randomization.
- **Intervention:** Empagliflozin 10 mg once daily vs matching placebo.
- **Primary outcome:** Composite of kidney disease progression (ESKD, sustained eGFR <10 ml/min/1.73 m², sustained decline in eGFR ≥40%, or renal death) or death from cardiovascular causes.

## Relevance to review (fourth pillar / early combination)

EMPA-KIDNEY is one of the pivotal SGLT2 inhibitor CKD outcome trials, extending SGLT2-inhibitor kidney benefit to a broad CKD population — including patients without diabetes and those with lower eGFR (down to ≥20 ml/min/1.73 m²) and low-level albuminuria — supporting SGLT2 inhibition as a core (pillar) therapy in CKD alongside RAS inhibition (background RAS-inhibitor use was an eligibility expectation).
