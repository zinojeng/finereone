- **Role in FIND-CKD**: FIDELITY is cited as the definitive benchmark for finerenone efficacy and hyperkalemia risk in type 2 diabetic CKD. FIND-CKD (non-diabetic CKD) draws on FIDELITY for: (1) anticipated effect size for the cardiorenal composite outcomes in a similar MRA-naive, maximum-RASi background population; (2) the hyperkalemia discontinuation rate (1.7% over ~3 years) as the comparator safety signal; (3) the albuminuria trajectory (32% UACR reduction at 4 months) as a pharmacodynamic surrogate. The central question FIND-CKD must address is whether the magnitude of benefit seen in diabetic CKD (HR ~0.77-0.86) translates to, is attenuated in, or exceeds that in non-diabetic CKD — where the underlying fibroinflammatory pathophysiology driven by MR overactivation may differ.

- **Key findings** (all figures verified from full text):
  - Population: 13,026 patients with T2D + CKD (UACR 30-5,000 mg/g, eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m²), all on maximum-tolerated RAS inhibition; mean eGFR 57.6 mL/min/1.73 m², median UACR 515 mg/g; 45.6% with prior CV disease; median follow-up 3.0 years (IQR 2.3-3.8).
  - Primary CV composite (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke, HHF): HR 0.86 (95% CI 0.78-0.95), P = 0.0018; NNT 46 over 3 years.
  - Primary kidney composite (kidney failure, sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease, renal death): HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.67-0.88), P = 0.0002; NNT 60 over 3 years.
  - HHF (dominant driver of CV benefit): HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.66-0.92), P = 0.0030.
  - ESKD: HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.64-0.99), P = 0.040.
  - Sustained ≥57% eGFR decrease: HR 0.70 (95% CI 0.60-0.83), P < 0.0001.
  - UACR at 4 months: 32% lower with finerenone (ratio LS-mean 0.68; 95% CI 0.66-0.70).
  - Hyperkalemia leading to permanent discontinuation: finerenone 1.7% vs placebo 0.6%; no fatal events.
  - All-cause mortality: P = 0.051 (non-significant).

- **How it SUPPORTS or CHALLENGES FIND-CKD's conclusions**:
  - SUPPORTS: If FIND-CKD shows a similar or larger cardiorenal benefit in non-diabetic CKD (HR in the 0.77-0.86 range or lower), it directly validates that finerenone's MR-antagonism benefit extends beyond the T2D etiology, strengthening the case for broad CKD application. The UACR reduction at 4 months (32%) sets the pharmacodynamic benchmark — concordance in FIND-CKD would confirm on-target MR blockade regardless of diabetic status.
  - CHALLENGES (potential): If FIND-CKD's effect size is materially smaller than FIDELITY, it would suggest that the diabetic/metabolic milieu amplifies MR-overactivation-driven damage, limiting generalizability. Non-diabetic CKD etiologies (e.g., IgA nephropathy, FSGS, hypertensive nephrosclerosis) may have different MR-dependency, making the FIDELITY benchmark less applicable. Additionally, FIDELITY's low SGLT-2i background (6.7%) means that in a contemporary non-diabetic CKD trial where SGLT-2i use is absent, the incremental effect may be assessed in a purer population but cannot be directly contextualized against triple therapy (RASi + SGLT-2i + finerenone) scenarios.

- **Limitations of this reference itself**:
  1. Diabetic CKD only — the entire population has T2D; mechanistic extrapolation to non-diabetic CKD (the FIND-CKD population) requires assumption that MR overactivation is similarly operative.
  2. Prespecified but exploratory pooling — p-values are exploratory; no multiplicity correction was applied; this is not a formally powered single trial.
  3. Industry sponsorship (Bayer) conducted all statistical analyses; all authors had data access but the sponsor led analysis.
  4. Low representation of Black patients and patients with non-albuminuric CKD, limiting generalizability.
  5. Low SGLT-2i background (6.7%) reflects 2015-2018 enrollment — does not reflect current standard of care where SGLT-2i are guideline-recommended for CKD; additive benefit of finerenone on top of SGLT-2i in non-diabetic CKD is extrapolated, not tested here.
  6. HFrEF excluded — limits applicability to the HF-CKD overlap population.
  7. All-cause mortality was non-significant (P = 0.051) — the trial was not powered for mortality as a primary endpoint.
