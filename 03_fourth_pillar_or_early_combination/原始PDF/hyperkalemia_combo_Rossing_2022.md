# Finerenone in Patients With CKD and Type 2 Diabetes by SGLT2 Inhibitor Treatment: The FIDELITY Analysis

## Objective
Finerenone reduced the risk of kidney and cardiovascular events in people with chronic kidney disease (CKD) and type 2 diabetes in the FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD phase 3 studies. Effects of finerenone on outcomes in patients taking sodium–glucose cotransporter 2 inhibitors (SGLT2is) were evaluated in a prespecified pooled analysis (FIDELITY) of these studies.

## Research Design and Methods
Patients with type 2 diabetes and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR) ≥30 to ≤5,000 mg/g and estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) ≥25 mL/min/1.73 m² were randomly assigned to finerenone or placebo; SGLT2is were permitted at any time. Outcomes included a cardiovascular composite (cardiovascular death, nonfatal myocardial infarction, nonfatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure) and a kidney composite (kidney failure, sustained ≥57% eGFR decline, or renal death) end point, changes in UACR and eGFR, and safety outcomes.

## Study Population
- Total analyzed: 13,026 patients.
- 877 (6.7%) received an SGLT2i at baseline.
- 1,113 (8.5%) initiated an SGLT2i during the trial (958 finerenone-treated and 1,032 placebo-treated patients initiating SGLT2i during the trial per PMC full text).

## Cardiovascular Composite Outcomes
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: HR 0.87 (95% CI 0.79–0.96)
- With SGLT2i at baseline: HR 0.67 (95% CI 0.42–1.07)
- P-interaction = 0.46 (no significant heterogeneity)

All-cause mortality:
- Without SGLT2i: HR 0.90 (95% CI 0.80–1.02)
- With SGLT2i: HR 0.58 (95% CI 0.30–1.10)
- P-interaction = 0.24

Hospitalization for heart failure:
- Without SGLT2i: HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.68–0.95)
- With SGLT2i: HR 0.44 (95% CI 0.19–0.99)

## Kidney Composite Outcomes
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.69–0.92)
- With SGLT2i at baseline: HR 0.42 (95% CI 0.16–1.08)
- P-interaction = 0.29 (no significant heterogeneity)

## Albuminuria (UACR) Change, Baseline to Month 4
- With SGLT2i at baseline: 37% reduction (ratio 0.63; 95% CI 0.57–0.70)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: 31% reduction (ratio 0.69; 95% CI 0.67–0.71)
- P-interaction = 0.17

## eGFR Changes
Baseline to month 4 between-group differences (finerenone vs. placebo):
- With SGLT2i: −3.69 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Without SGLT2i: −2.23 mL/min/1.73 m²
- Difference: −1.46 mL/min/1.73 m² (95% CI −1.89 to −1.04)

Chronic eGFR slope (month 4 to end of study):
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone −1.92 (95% CI −2.61 to −1.23); placebo −3.45 (95% CI −4.15 to −2.76) mL/min/1.73 m²
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone −2.54 (95% CI −2.81 to −2.27); placebo −3.72 (95% CI −3.99 to −3.45) mL/min/1.73 m²

## Systolic Blood Pressure
Maximum between-group differences (finerenone vs. placebo) at month 4:
- With SGLT2i: −3.6 mmHg
- Without SGLT2i: −3.7 mmHg

## Safety: Hyperkalemia
Any hyperkalemia-related adverse events:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 10.3% vs. placebo 2.7%
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 14.3% vs. placebo 7.2%

Serum potassium >6.0 mmol/L:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 4 (0.9%) vs. placebo 3 (0.7%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 207 (3.4%) vs. placebo 77 (1.3%)

Hyperkalemia events causing discontinuation:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 1.1% vs. placebo 0.7%
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 1.7% vs. placebo 0.6%

Central lab—serum potassium >5.5 mmol/L:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 34 (7.9%) vs. placebo 13 (3.0%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 1,041 (17.4%) vs. placebo 457 (7.7%)

## Other Adverse Events
Any adverse event:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 398 (90.9%) vs. placebo 384 (87.5%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 5,204 (85.7%) vs. placebo 5,223 (86.3%)

Serious adverse events:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 146 (33.3%) vs. placebo 141 (32.1%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 1,914 (31.5%) vs. placebo 2,045 (33.8%)

Acute kidney injury:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 5 (1.1%) vs. placebo 15 (3.4%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 215 (3.5%) vs. placebo 219 (3.6%)

Events leading to death:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 2 (0.5%) vs. placebo 9 (2.1%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 108 (1.8%) vs. placebo 142 (2.3%)

Adverse events leading to discontinuation:
- With SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 18 (4.1%) vs. placebo 23 (5.2%)
- Without SGLT2i at baseline: finerenone 396 (6.5%) vs. placebo 328 (5.4%)

## Conclusions
Benefits of finerenone compared with placebo on cardiorenal outcomes in patients with CKD and type 2 diabetes were observed irrespective of SGLT2i use. Baseline SGLT2i use did not affect cardiovascular or kidney composite risk reduction with finerenone (P-interaction = 0.46 and 0.29, respectively), nor did SGLT2i use concomitant with study treatment. No new safety concerns emerged with combined therapy.
