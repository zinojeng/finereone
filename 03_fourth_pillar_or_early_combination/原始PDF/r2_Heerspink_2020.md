# Dapagliflozin in Patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (DAPA-CKD)

## Abstract

### Background
Patients with chronic kidney disease have a high risk of adverse kidney and cardiovascular outcomes. The effect of dapagliflozin in patients with chronic kidney disease, with or without type 2 diabetes, is not known.

### Methods
The investigators randomly assigned 4304 participants with an estimated glomerular filtration rate (GFR) of 25 to 75 ml/min/1.73 m² and a urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (albumin in milligrams, creatinine in grams) of 200 to 5000 to receive dapagliflozin (10 mg once daily) or placebo. The primary outcome was a composite of a sustained decline in the estimated GFR of at least 50%, end-stage kidney disease, or death from renal or cardiovascular causes.

### Results
The independent data monitoring committee recommended stopping the trial because of efficacy. Over a median of 2.4 years, a primary outcome event occurred in 197 of 2152 participants (9.2%) in the dapagliflozin group and 312 of 2152 participants (14.5%) in the placebo group (hazard ratio, 0.61; 95% CI, 0.51 to 0.72; P<0.001; number needed to treat to prevent one primary outcome event, 19 [95% CI, 15 to 27]). The hazard ratio for the composite of a sustained decline in the estimated GFR of at least 50%, end-stage kidney disease, or death from renal causes was 0.56 (95% CI, 0.45 to 0.68; P<0.001), and the hazard ratio for the composite of death from cardiovascular causes or hospitalization for heart failure was 0.71 (95% CI, 0.55 to 0.92; P=0.009). Death occurred in 101 participants (4.7%) in the dapagliflozin group and 146 participants (6.8%) in the placebo group (hazard ratio, 0.69; 95% CI, 0.53 to 0.88; P=0.004). The effects of dapagliflozin were similar in participants with type 2 diabetes and in those without type 2 diabetes. The known safety profile of dapagliflozin was confirmed.

### Conclusions
Among patients with chronic kidney disease, regardless of the presence or absence of diabetes, the risk of a composite of a sustained decline in the estimated GFR of at least 50%, end-stage kidney disease, or death from renal or cardiovascular causes was significantly lower with dapagliflozin than with placebo. ## Introduction

Worldwide estimates indicate that nearly 700 million persons have chronic kidney disease. Chronic kidney disease is an important contributor to illness and is associated with a diminished quality of life and a reduced life expectancy. Until recently, the only classes of medication shown to slow a decline in kidney function were ACE inhibitors and ARBs; most of that evidence was generated in patients with type 2 diabetes.

SGLT2 inhibitors decrease glycated hemoglobin levels and have shown favorable effects on kidney and cardiovascular outcomes in large clinical trials involving patients with type 2 diabetes. The CREDENCE trial showed that long-term administration of canagliflozin conferred renal and cardiovascular protection in patients with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease. The benefits of SGLT2 inhibitors appear to be independent of their blood glucose–lowering effects and may be mediated by natriuresis and glucose-induced osmotic diuresis, leading to a reduction in intraglomerular pressure. The DAPA-CKD trial was designed to assess the long-term efficacy and safety of dapagliflozin in patients with chronic kidney disease, with or without type 2 diabetes.

## Methods

### Trial Design and Oversight
Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter clinical trial. Sponsored by AstraZeneca and conducted at 386 sites in 21 countries from February 2, 2017, through June 12, 2020. Analyses conducted by the sponsor were replicated by an independent academic group at the University Medical Center Groningen.

### Participants
Adults with or without type 2 diabetes who had an estimated GFR of 25 to 75 ml/min/1.73 m² and a urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio of 200 to 5000 were eligible. All participants were required to be receiving a stable dose of an ACE inhibitor or ARB for at least 4 weeks before screening (unless documented to be unable to take them). Key exclusion criteria: documented type 1 diabetes, polycystic kidney disease, lupus nephritis, or ANCA-associated vasculitis; immunotherapy for primary or secondary kidney disease within 6 months before enrollment.

### Trial Procedures
Participants randomly assigned to dapagliflozin (10 mg once daily) or matching placebo in an approximate 1:1 ratio. Randomization stratified according to diagnosis of type 2 diabetes (yes/no) and urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (≤1000 or >1000). Recruitment of participants with an estimated GFR of 60 to 75 ml/min/1.73 m² was halted on November 27, 2017, to ensure that no more than 10% of participants had stage 2 chronic kidney disease.

### Outcomes
- **Primary composite outcome** (time-to-event): first occurrence of a decline of at least 50% in estimated GFR (confirmed), onset of end-stage kidney disease (maintenance dialysis ≥28 days, kidney transplantation, or estimated GFR <15 ml/min/1.73 m² confirmed), or death from renal or cardiovascular causes.
- **Secondary outcomes** (hierarchical): (1) composite kidney outcome of sustained decline in estimated GFR ≥50%, end-stage kidney disease, or death from renal causes; (2) composite cardiovascular outcome of hospitalization for heart failure or death from cardiovascular causes; (3) death from any cause.

### Statistical Analysis
Estimated that 681 primary outcome events would be needed to detect a 22% lower relative risk (hazard ratio of 0.78) with 90% power at two-sided alpha of 0.05, assuming an annual placebo event rate of 7.5%. A closed testing procedure with prespecified hierarchical ordering controlled type I error. Primary efficacy analysis based on the intention-to-treat population. Cox proportional-hazards model stratified by randomization factors and adjusted for baseline estimated GFR. Analyses performed with SAS software version 9.4.

## Results

### Participants and Follow-up
From February 2017 through October 2018, 7517 participants were screened, of whom 4094 underwent randomization; 210 additional participants from China underwent randomization December 2019 through March 2020 (total 4304). Baseline characteristics balanced between groups. Mean age 61.8±12.1 years; 1425 participants (33.1%) female. Mean estimated GFR 43.1±12.4 ml/min/1.73 m²; median urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio 949; 2906 participants (67.5%) had type 2 diabetes.

After a review meeting on March 26, 2020, the independent data monitoring committee recommended discontinuing the trial because of clear efficacy, based on 408 primary outcome events. April 3, 2020 chosen as cutoff for efficacy analyses. Median follow-up 2.4 years (IQR 2.0 to 2.7). Dapagliflozin discontinued (non-death reasons) in 274 participants and placebo in 309 (12.7% and 14.4%). 4289 participants (99.7%) completed the trial.

### eGFR Slope (Fig. 3)
Least-squares mean estimated GFR slopes from baseline to 30 months: dapagliflozin −2.86±0.11 vs. placebo −3.79±0.11 ml/min/1.73 m²/year; between-group difference 0.93 ml/min/1.73 m²/year (95% CI, 0.61 to 1.25). During the first 2 weeks there was a greater reduction in eGFR with dapagliflozin (−3.97±0.15 vs. −0.82±0.15 ml/min/1.73 m²). Thereafter (chronic phase), the annual change in mean eGFR was smaller with dapagliflozin than placebo (−1.67±0.11 vs. −3.59±0.11 ml/min/1.73 m²), for a between-group difference of 1.92 ml/min/1.73 m²/year (95% CI, 1.61 to 2.24).

## Discussion

Participants with chronic kidney disease, with or without type 2 diabetes, randomly assigned to dapagliflozin had a lower risk of the primary composite outcome than those assigned to placebo. Each component of the composite outcome occurred less frequently with dapagliflozin, with similar results for death from cardiovascular causes or hospitalization for heart failure and for death from any cause. The trial adds to the literature by examining an SGLT2 inhibitor, added to background therapy including an ACE inhibitor or ARB, in participants with chronic kidney disease with or without type 2 diabetes.

In contrast to the CREDENCE trial (lower eGFR cutoff 30 ml/min/1.73 m²), DAPA-CKD examined effects in patients of whom 32.5% did not have type 2 diabetes and 14.5% had an estimated GFR below 30 ml/min/1.73 m². The trial confirms that the kidney-protective effects of SGLT2 inhibitors extend to the broader population with chronic kidney disease without type 2 diabetes, for whom ACE inhibitors had been the only pharmacologic treatments shown to prevent kidney failure. The markedly lower mortality in the dapagliflozin group supports the use of dapagliflozin as an addition to the therapeutic management of chronic kidney disease. The lower risk of hospitalization for heart failure or death from cardiovascular causes is consistent with the DECLARE–TIMI 58 and DAPA-HF trials.

Dapagliflozin had an acceptable safety profile in this population, which included participants with an estimated GFR as low as 25 ml/min/1.73 m². There were no cases of diabetic ketoacidosis with dapagliflozin, and hypoglycemic episodes did not occur in participants without diabetes.

**Limitations:** The trial was stopped early on the recommendation of the independent data monitoring committee, which may have reduced the power of some secondary outcomes. As in other SGLT2 inhibitor trials, there was an initial dip in estimated GFR followed by stabilization of kidney-function decline, reflecting favorable hemodynamic changes in the glomerulus. Estimated GFR values were not collected after trial completion, so reversibility of the initial dip could not be ascertained.

## Conclusion

Among patients with chronic kidney disease, regardless of the presence or absence of diabetes, the risk of the primary composite kidney/cardiovascular outcome was significantly lower with dapagliflozin than with placebo, with a favorable effect on mortality and a confirmed safety profile.
