## Abstract (source: PubMed record 26325557)

**Background / Objective:** Evaluation of the safety and efficacy of the nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist finerenone, added on to renin-angiotensin system (RAS) blockade, in patients with diabetic kidney disease and elevated urinary albumin excretion.

**Design, Setting, Participants:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial conducted across 148 sites in 23 countries. 821 participants were randomized (screened from 1,501 patients). Baseline mean age was 64.2 years; 78% were male.

**Interventions:** Seven finerenone dose groups (1.25 to 25 mg once daily) or placebo, for 90 days.

**Main Outcomes and Measures:** Primary endpoint was the ratio of the urinary albumin-creatinine ratio (UACR) at day 90 relative to baseline. Safety outcomes tracked serum potassium and estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) changes.

**Results:** Finerenone produced dose-dependent reductions in UACR. Placebo-corrected UACR ratios (day 90 vs baseline) were:
- 7.5 mg: 0.79 (90% CI, 0.68–0.91; P = .004)
- 10 mg: 0.76 (90% CI, 0.65–0.88; P = .001)
- 15 mg: 0.67 (90% CI, 0.58–0.77; P < .001)
- 25 mg: 0.62 (90% CI, 0.54–0.72; P < .001)

Discontinuation due to hyperkalemia ranged from approximately 1.7% to 3.2% across the higher finerenone dose groups. No excess decline in kidney function was observed relative to placebo.

**Conclusions:** Addition of finerenone to RAS blockade improved the urinary albumin-creatinine ratio in a dose-dependent manner compared with placebo in patients with diabetic nephropathy. Larger trials with active comparators and longer follow-up were needed to further assess efficacy and safety.
