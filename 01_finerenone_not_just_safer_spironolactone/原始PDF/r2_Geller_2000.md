# Activating Mineralocorticoid Receptor Mutation in Hypertension Exacerbated by Pregnancy

**Authors:** David S. Geller, Anita Farhi, Nikki Pinkerton, Michael Fradley, Michael Moritz, Adrian Spitzer, Gretchen Meinke, Francis T. F. Tsai, Paul B. Sigler, Richard P. Lifton

**Affiliations:** Howard Hughes Medical Institute; Departments of Genetics, Internal Medicine (Nephrology), and Molecular Biophysics and Biochemistry, Yale University School of Medicine, Boyer Center for Molecular Medicine, New Haven, CT; Department of Pediatrics, Albert Einstein College of Medicine, New York, NY.

**Citation:** Science. 2000 Jul 7;289(5476):119-121. doi:10.1126/science.289.5476.119. PMID: 10884226.

## Abstract

Hypertension and pregnancy-related hypertension are major public health problems of largely unknown causes. We describe a mutation in the mineralocorticoid receptor (MR), S810L, that causes early-onset hypertension that is markedly exacerbated in pregnancy. This mutation results in constitutive MR activity and alters receptor specificity, with progesterone and other steroids lacking 21-hydroxyl groups, normally MR antagonists, becoming potent agonists. Structural and biochemical studies indicate that the mutation results in the gain of a van der Waals interaction between helix 5 and helix 3 that substitutes for interaction of the steroid 21-hydroxyl group with helix 3 in the wild-type receptor. This helix 5–helix 3 interaction is highly conserved among diverse nuclear hormone receptors, suggesting its general role in receptor activation.

## Key Findings (full text)

### Background and rationale
- Blood pressure is normally reduced throughout gestation, but ~6% of pregnancies are complicated by the development of hypertension, raising the risk of pre-eclampsia (a hypertensive disorder of pregnancy that increases maternal and perinatal mortality). The prompt resolution of many cases with delivery suggests pregnancy-specific factors.
- Loss-of-function mutations in MR (locus symbol *NR3C2*) cause pseudohypoaldosteronism type 1 (PHA1), a salt-wasting/hypotension disease. Normally, activation of MR by aldosterone raises renal salt reabsorption by increasing activity of the epithelial sodium channel of the distal nephron.
- To determine if gain-of-function mutations in *MR* could cause increased renal salt reabsorption and hypertension, the authors screened *MR* in 75 patients with early onset of severe hypertension.

### Identification of the mutation
- A 15-year-old boy with severe hypertension, suppressed plasma renin activity, low serum aldosterone, and no other underlying cause was heterozygous for a missense mutation resulting in substitution of leucine for serine at codon 810 (**S810L**).
- The S810L mutation lies in the MR hormone-binding domain (HBD), altering an amino acid conserved in all MRs from *Xenopus* to human but not found in other nuclear receptors. S810L was not detected in 160 control chromosomes.

### Cosegregation with hypertension (kindred 503)
- 23 relatives of the proband were evaluated. **11 had been diagnosed with severe hypertension before age 20** (a rare trait; ~1% in the general population), whereas the remainder had unremarkable blood pressures — findings suggesting Mendelian segregation of hypertension.
- MR_L810 precisely cosegregated with early-onset hypertension in the family, providing strong evidence of linkage. **Maximum lod score for linkage = 5.24 at recombination fraction of zero (odds of 174,000:1 in favor of linkage).**
- Carriers showed marked increase of blood pressure even when taking antihypertensive medication, as well as suppression of aldosterone secretion. Nonsignificant trend toward lower serum potassium; no significant effects of gender or age on phenotypic expression. Deceased pedigree members with early-onset hypertension all died of heart failure before age 50.

### Functional / biochemical studies
- Wild-type MR (MR_WT) or MR_L810 expressed in Cos-7 and HeLa cells with luciferase under control of the MR-sensitive MMTV promoter. With aldosterone, MR_WT and MR_L810 activities were indistinguishable. **In the absence of added steroid, MR_L810 showed 27% of maximal activity (P < 0.001 versus MR_WT)** — constitutive activity that may contribute to hypertension.
- Steroids bearing 21-hydroxyl groups (e.g., aldosterone, deoxycorticosterone [DOC], 11-deoxycortisol, corticosterone) bound and activated both MR_WT and MR_L810 similarly.
- **Steroids with neither modification (no 21-hydroxyl and no 11-keto group), such as progesterone, estradiol, and testosterone — normally MR antagonists — became potent agonists of MR_L810 but not MR_WT.** The dose-response curve for activation of MR_L810 by progesterone was indistinguishable from that of MR_WT with aldosterone.
- **Spironolactone** (an MR antagonist) is also a potent **agonist** of MR_L810 — the authors infer this medication is contraindicated in MR_L810 carriers.
- Progesterone-mediated MR activation in the presence of varying MR_WT:MR_L810 ratios (MR activates transcription as a dimer): activation was intermediate between fully active and completely inactive heterodimers, consistent with partial activation of heterodimers.

### Pregnancy exacerbation
- Progesterone levels normally increase ~100-fold in pregnancy, reaching ~500 nM; this suggests females with MR_L810 might develop severe hypertension in pregnancy.
- Two female MR_L810 carriers had undergone five pregnancies; all complicated by marked exacerbation of hypertension. Example: blood pressure decreased early in the first pregnancy of one carrier, then rose dramatically to 170/130 mmHg at 28 weeks despite antihypertensive therapy, accompanied by low serum potassium with marked renal potassium wasting. Aldosterone levels (normally increase ~10-fold in pregnancy) were undetectable. No proteinuria, edema, or neurologic changes (excluding pre-eclampsia). Because of worsening BP (210/120 mmHg), a Caesarean section was performed at 34 weeks; healthy son delivered. Subsequent pregnancies followed similar courses; two other carrier pregnancies also complicated, precipitating delivery in the sixth and seventh months. Findings support progesterone's in vivo agonism of MR_L810.

### Structural mechanism
- MR and progesterone receptor (PR) HBDs are highly similar, differing by only 3 of 18 residues lining the hormone-binding cavity. Modeling based on the known PR structure: in the MR_L810 model, the L810 side chain in helix 5 projects into the ligand-binding cavity, forming van der Waals interactions with both A773 of helix 3 and the C19 methyl group of the steroid (Fig. 3A). In MR_WT, S810 interacts only with the C19 methyl group.
- The steroid 21-hydroxyl group required for MR_WT activation interacts with N770 in helix 3.
- 19-norprogesterone (19-NP; lacks C19 methyl) fully activated MR_L810, indicating the L810–C19 methyl interaction is not required for activation. Substitution of methionine (longer side chain) at 810 retained activation by 19-NP; shorter side chains (valine, alanine) gave progressive loss.
- Second-site complementation: MR with A773 substitution (V810-G773 pair) showed no activation by 19-NP, but activation restored in the V810-A773 pair — supporting the importance of the helix 3–helix 5 interaction.

### Binding affinities
- Dissociation constants for aldosterone: MR_WT Kd = 1.10 ± 0.09 nM vs MR_L810 Kd = 1.06 ± 0.12 nM (indistinguishable).
- Progesterone, 19-NP, and spironolactone all showed increased competition with aldosterone for binding to MR_L810 (substantially higher affinity for the mutant). Example: concentration of spironolactone required to compete 50% of aldosterone binding was **10-fold lower** for MR_L810 than MR_WT.

### Broader significance
- The residues corresponding to MR positions 810 and 773 are methionine-glycine (M759, G722) in PR and leucine-alanine in the estrogen receptor (L387, A350) and retinoid X receptor (RXR; L314, A277); all show van der Waals interactions in their crystal structures. Only MR_WT violates this rule, and the S810L mutation restores this interaction — suggesting general significance of the helix 5–helix 3 interaction in nuclear receptor activation.
- In men in this kindred, free progesterone levels are normally low; free 17-hydroxyprogesterone (17-HP) levels rival those of aldosterone, and 17-HP is an agonist of MR_L810, suggesting gain of 17-HP agonist activity may contribute to hypertension in males.

### Conclusion
These findings define a new Mendelian form of hypertension featuring marked exacerbation in pregnancy. The mutation demonstrates the ability of a single amino acid substitution to dramatically alter nuclear receptor ligand selectivity and activation.

## Notes / relevance to review (MR antagonists: spironolactone vs finerenone)
- Directly relevant to the review's theme: **spironolactone acts as an AGONIST at the constitutively active MR_L810 mutant receptor** (not an antagonist), and is therefore contraindicated in Geller-syndrome carriers. This underscores that steroidal MR ligand behavior can be structurally context-dependent.
- Establishes the structural biology of the MR hormone-binding domain (helix 3 / helix 5 / N770 / A773) that informs how different MR antagonists (steroidal spironolactone vs nonsteroidal finerenone) interact with the receptor.
