# FIND-CKD — Finerenone in Persons with CKD without Diabetes

Heerspink HJL, Neuen BL, Agarwal R, et al. N Engl J Med. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2604625. Published online June 4, 2026. ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05047263. Funded by Bayer.

---

## 1. Study Design

- **Phase / type:** Phase 3, international, prospective, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial.
- **Randomization:** 1:1 to oral finerenone vs matching placebo, via interactive response technology.
- **Stratification factors (2):** (1) baseline SGLT2 inhibitor use (yes vs no); (2) screening urinary albumin-to-creatinine ratio (uACR ≤1000 vs >1000 mg/g).
- **Blinding:** All investigators, treating physicians, participants, and trial personnel unaware of group assignment throughout.
- **Follow-up / treatment duration:** Participants treated for at least 32 months; primary endpoint = total eGFR slope baseline→month 32. Median treatment period 36.6 months. Data shown to month 44 plus end-of-treatment and a follow-up visit ~4 weeks after last dose.
- **Sites/countries:** 24 countries across Asia, Europe, Latin America, and North America (regions analyzed: North America, Asia, Europe, Latin America).
- **Sponsor (Bayer) role:** Trial designed by steering committee and Bayer. Data collected by investigators, **analyzed by the sponsor**, and **independently confirmed by University Medical Center Groningen staff using original data**. First author wrote initial draft and had full data access; first + last authors vouch for accuracy/completeness; sponsor + investigators vouch for protocol fidelity. Independent Data and Safety Monitoring Committee oversaw the trial.
- **Enrollment window:** September 21, 2021 – May 12, 2023.
- **Visit schedule:** Screening, baseline, then months 1, 3, 6, 9, 12; thereafter every 4 months until end of trial. Extra visit 4 weeks after trial-regimen discontinuation.
- **K+ / eGFR monitoring:** At every visit, blood drawn for eGFR and serum potassium at the **central laboratory** (used for all eGFR outcomes and K+ cutoffs >5.5 and >6.0 mmol/L). Potassium and eGFR also measured at **local laboratories for dosing decisions** at each visit. uACR and uPCR measured exclusively centrally.

## 2. Eligibility

**Inclusion:**
- Age ≥18 years.
- CKD defined as: eGFR ≥25 to <60 ml/min/1.73 m² with uACR ≥200 to <500 mg/g, OR eGFR ≥25 to <90 ml/min/1.73 m² with uACR ≥500 to ≤3500 mg/g (albumin in mg, creatinine in g). (The 200–<500 lower-albuminuria stratum was capped at ~10% of the total population.)
- Stable, maximum-tolerated labeled dose of ACE inhibitor or ARB for ≥4 weeks before screening.
- Serum potassium ≤4.8 mmol/L.
- Negative pregnancy test (women of childbearing potential).
- Able to provide informed consent.

**Exclusion (full list):**
- Type 1 or type 2 diabetes, or HbA1c ≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol).
- SBP ≥160 or DBP ≥100 mmHg.
- Symptomatic heart failure with reduced EF with class 1A indication for MRAs.
- uACR >3500 mg/g at screening.
- Autosomal dominant or autosomal recessive polycystic kidney disease.
- Lupus nephritis or ANCA-associated vasculitis or any other kidney disease requiring immunosuppressive therapy within 6 months before screening.
- Acute kidney injury requiring dialysis within 6 months before screening.
- History of organ transplantation.
- CV event (HF decompensation, MI, stroke, TIA, pulmonary embolism, or elective CABG) within 3 months before screening, or elective PCI within 1 month before screening.
- Addison's disease.
- Hepatic insufficiency Child-Pugh C.
- Concomitant other MRA, renin inhibitor, sacubitril/valsartan, or K-sparing diuretic that cannot be stopped ≥4 weeks before screening.
- Concomitant ACEi + ARB if one not discontinued ≥4 weeks before screening.
- Concomitant potent CYP3A4 inhibitors or moderate/potent inducers (stopped ≥7 days before randomization).

## 4. Intervention / Dosing

- **Starting dose:** finerenone 20 mg/day if screening eGFR ≥60; 10 mg/day if screening eGFR 25 to <60.
- **Up-titration:** 10→20 mg/day (target) permitted from month 1 onward if serum K+ ≤4.8 mmol/L AND eGFR not >30% below last-visit value.
- **Dose decrease:** allowed any time for safety.
- **Hyperkalemia hold/restart:** if serum K+ >5.5 mmol/L → withhold finerenone/placebo for 72 hours; restart at 10 mg/day once K+ ≤5.0 mmol/L.
- Given on top of individually tolerated maximum labeled dose of an ACEi or ARB.

## 5. Outcomes & Statistical Methods

- **Primary:** Total eGFR slope = mean annual rate of eGFR change from baseline to month 32, via a **two-slope linear spline mixed-effects model** (random-coefficient). Change point (knot) fixed at month 3 (t*=0.2464 yr). Fixed effects: treatment-specific intercept, time since randomization, time since month 3, stratification factors and their time interactions. Random effects: intercept, acute slope (baseline→mo 3), and directional change in slope at change point. Unstructured covariance by treatment.
  - **Acute slope** = baseline→month 3 (reported per 3 months).
  - **Chronic slope** = month 3→discontinuation of trial regimen (annual).
- **Secondary (hierarchical testing order, confirmatory):**
  1. Composite kidney–CV outcome (sustained ≥57% eGFR decline [≥4 wk], kidney failure [eGFR <15 confirmed ≥4 wk / long-term dialysis ≥30 d / transplant], HF hospitalization, or CV death).
  2. Composite kidney outcome (sustained ≥57% eGFR decline or kidney failure).
  3. Composite CV outcome (HF hospitalization or CV death).
  - Analyzed via stratified Cox proportional-hazards models + stratified log-rank tests; Aalen–Johansen cumulative incidence accounting for competing risk of death.
- **Exploratory:** chronic eGFR slope; eGFR change baseline→week 4 post-treatment; subgroup analyses (stratification factors, baseline eGFR, etc.); uACR change baseline→month 6; ≥30% uACR reduction; composite of sustained ≥40% eGFR decline or kidney failure.
- **Estimands / intercurrent-event handling:**
  - **Primary estimand:** Death and chronic dialysis/transplant handled by **composite strategy** (failure/"unfavorable" eGFR values imputed via multiple imputation for scheduled visits after the ICE). Premature discontinuation/extended interruption handled by **treatment-policy strategy** (participants continued visits; collected eGFR included).
  - **Alternative estimand:** shared-parameter model (Vonesh 2019) with **hypothetical strategy** for death/dialysis/transplant (effect if no participant experienced these), treatment-policy for discontinuation.
- **Sensitivity analyses:** shared-parameter model; eGFR slopes using cystatin C–based equations; multiple imputation with on-treatment discontinuation indicators; tipping-point analysis.
- **Sample size:** 1500 participants → >90% power at two-sided α=0.05 to detect 0.7 ml/min/1.73 m² slower annual eGFR decline with finerenone; assumed 5% intercurrent events and 5% trial discontinuation.
- **Populations:** efficacy = full analysis set (all randomized); safety = all who received ≥1 dose. SAS v9.4+.

## 6. RESULTS — Efficacy

**Primary (total eGFR slope, baseline→month 32):**
- Finerenone −3.3 (95% CI −3.6 to −3.1) ml/min/1.73 m²/yr.
- Placebo −4.0 (95% CI −4.3 to −3.8).
- **Difference 0.7 (95% CI 0.3 to 1.1); P<0.001.** (Figure 2 detail: −3.34 vs −4.02; diff 0.68, 95% CI 0.32 to 1.05.)

**Slope components (Figure 1B):**
- Acute slope (baseline→mo 3): finerenone −2.0 vs placebo −0.8 per 3 mo; difference **−1.2 (95% CI −1.7 to −0.6)** per 3 mo (finerenone causes initial eGFR dip). Baseline→mo3 mean eGFR change 1.2 ml/min/1.73 m² greater (less decline) — note text says rate at mo 3 was 1.2 higher with finerenone.
- Chronic slope (mo 3→end of treatment): finerenone −2.9 vs placebo −4.1; difference **1.2 (95% CI 0.9 to 1.6)** /yr.

- eGFR at month 32: 39.4±18.2 (fin) vs 38.1±18.4 (plb).
- Off-treatment (baseline→week 4 post-treatment) eGFR change: −8.7 (95% CI −9.7 to −7.8) fin vs −11.1 (95% CI −12.0 to −10.1) plb; difference 2.4 (95% CI 1.2 to 3.5).
