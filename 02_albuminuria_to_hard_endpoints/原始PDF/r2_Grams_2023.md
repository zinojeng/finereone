## Abstract (verified — PubMed)

**Importance / Background.** Chronic kidney disease (low estimated glomerular filtration rate [eGFR] or albuminuria) affects approximately 14% of adults in the US.

**Objective.** To evaluate associations of lower eGFR based on creatinine alone, lower eGFR based on creatinine combined with cystatin C, and more severe albuminuria with adverse kidney outcomes, cardiovascular outcomes, and other health outcomes.

**Design / Setting / Participants.** Individual-participant data meta-analysis of:
- **27,503,140** individuals from **114** global cohorts (eGFR based on creatinine alone)
- **720,736** individuals from **20** cohorts (eGFR based on creatinine and cystatin C)
- **9,067,753** individuals from **114** cohorts (albuminuria)
- Data span **1980 to 2021**.

**Exposures.** The CKD-EPI 2021 equations for eGFR based on creatinine alone and eGFR based on creatinine and cystatin C; and albuminuria estimated as urine albumin to creatinine ratio (UACR).

**Main Outcomes (10 adverse outcomes).** Kidney failure requiring replacement therapy, all-cause mortality, cardiovascular mortality, acute kidney injury, any hospitalization, coronary heart disease, stroke, heart failure, atrial fibrillation, and peripheral artery disease. Analyses performed within each cohort and summarized with random-effects meta-analyses.

**Population characteristics.**
- eGFR (creatinine alone) population: mean age 54 years (SD 17); 51% women; mean follow-up 4.8 years (SD 3.3); mean eGFR 90 mL/min/1.73 m² (SD 22); median UACR 11 mg/g (IQR 8-16).
- eGFR (creatinine + cystatin C) population: mean age 59 years (SD 12); 53% women; mean follow-up 10.8 years (SD 4.1); mean eGFR 88 mL/min/1.73 m² (SD 22); median UACR 9 mg/g (IQR 6-18).

**Results.** Lower eGFR (whether based on creatinine alone or based on creatinine and cystatin C) and higher UACR were each significantly associated with higher risk for each of the 10 adverse outcomes, including those in the mildest categories of chronic kidney disease.

> Example (verified, from abstract): Among people with a **UACR < 10 mg/g**, an **eGFR of 45 to 59 mL/min/1.73 m²** based on creatinine alone was associated with significantly higher hospitalization rates compared with an eGFR of 90 to 104 mL/min/1.73 m² — **adjusted HR 1.3 (95% CI, 1.2-1.3)**; **161 vs 79 events per 1000 person-years**; **excess absolute risk 22 events per 1000 person-years (95% CI, 19-25)**.

**Conclusions.** In this retrospective analysis of 114 cohorts, lower eGFR based on creatinine alone, lower eGFR based on creatinine and cystatin C, and more severe UACR were each associated with increased rates of 10 adverse outcomes, including adverse kidney outcomes, cardiovascular diseases, and hospitalizations.
