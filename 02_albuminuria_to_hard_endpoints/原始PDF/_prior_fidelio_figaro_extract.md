# FIDELIO-DKD and FIGARO-DKD — Finerenone Trial Data Extraction

Source files:
- FIDELIO-DKD: NEJMoa2025845.md (Bakris et al., N Engl J Med 2020;383:2219-29; NCT02540993)
- FIGARO-DKD: NEJMoa2110956.md (Pitt et al., N Engl J Med 2021;385:2252-63; NCT02545049)

Both trials: phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 1:1, funded by Bayer. Finerenone (nonsteroidal selective mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist) vs placebo on background of maximally tolerated RAS blockade (ACEi or ARB). Initial dose 10 mg/day if eGFR 25 to <60, 20 mg/day if eGFR >=60; target 20 mg/day. Drug withheld if K+ >5.5 mmol/L, restarted when <=5.0.

---

## FIDELIO-DKD

### Population & key eligibility
- Adults (>=18 yr), type 2 diabetes, CKD, on max-tolerated ACEi/ARB.
- Two entry routes:
  - uACR 30 to <300 (mg/g) + eGFR 25 to <60 mL/min/1.73 m2 + diabetic retinopathy, OR
  - uACR 300 to 5000 + eGFR 25 to <75 mL/min/1.73 m2.
- Screening serum K+ <=4.8 mmol/L required.
- Predominantly advanced (stage 3-4) CKD with severely elevated albuminuria.
- Baseline (total N=5674): mean eGFR 44.3 mL/min/1.73 m2 (~55% had eGFR <45); median uACR 852 (IQR 446-1634); 87.5% uACR >=300; mean SBP 138.0 mmHg; HbA1c 7.7%; diabetes duration 16.6 yr; SGLT2i use only 4.6%, GLP-1 RA 6.9%.

### N
- Randomized 5734; analyzed 5674 (finerenone 2833, placebo 2841). Median follow-up 2.6 years.

### Primary outcome
- Definition: composite of kidney failure, sustained eGFR decrease >=40% from baseline (>=4 wk), or death from renal causes. (Kidney failure = ESKD [chronic dialysis >=90 d or transplant] or eGFR <15.)
- Result: 504/2833 (17.8%) vs 600/2841 (21.1%); HR 0.82 (95% CI 0.73-0.93); P=0.001. NNT 29 over 3 yr.

### Key secondary outcome
- Definition: composite of CV death, nonfatal MI, nonfatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure.
- Result: 367 (13.0%) vs 420 (14.8%); HR 0.86 (95% CI 0.75-0.99); P=0.03. NNT 42 over 3 yr.
- Components: CV death HR 0.86 (0.68-1.08); nonfatal MI 0.80 (0.58-1.09); nonfatal stroke 1.03 (0.76-1.38); HF hospitalization 0.86 (0.68-1.08).

### Other notable outcomes
- Secondary composite kidney outcome (kidney failure, eGFR decrease >=57%, or renal death): 252 (8.9%) vs 326 (11.5%); HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.65-0.90).
- All-cause death: HR 0.90 (0.75-1.07), NS.
- uACR reduction baseline to month 4: 31% greater with finerenone (ratio of LS-mean change 0.69; 95% CI 0.66-0.71).

### eGFR-slope data
- Not reported numerically in main text; effects on LS-mean change in eGFR slope shown only in Supplementary Figure S6 (no value given in main article).

### Hyperkalemia & K-related discontinuation
- Investigator-reported hyperkalemia (any): 18.3% finerenone vs 9.0% placebo (~2x).
- Hyperkalemia-related discontinuation of trial regimen: 2.3% vs 0.9%.
- No fatal hyperkalemia events.
- Serum K+ >5.5 mmol/L: 21.7% vs 9.8%; K+ >6.0: 4.5% vs 1.4%.
- Max mean K+ between-group difference 0.23 mmol/L at month 4.
- Hypokalemia less common with finerenone (1.0% vs 2.2%).

### SBP effect
- Modest. Change in mean SBP: -3.0 mmHg (finerenone) vs -0.1 mmHg (placebo) at month 1; -2.1 vs +0.9 mmHg at month 12.

## FIGARO-DKD

### Population & key eligibility
- Adults (>=18 yr), type 2 diabetes, CKD, on max-tolerated ACEi/ARB.
- Two entry routes (wider, earlier CKD than FIDELIO):
  - uACR 30 to <300 + eGFR 25 to 90 mL/min/1.73 m2 (stage 2-4 CKD), OR
  - uACR 300 to 5000 + eGFR >=60 mL/min/1.73 m2 (stage 1-2 CKD).
- Screening serum K+ <=4.8 mmol/L required.
- Patients heavily represented in FIDELIO (uACR 300-5000 + eGFR 25 to <60) were excluded. HFrEF excluded.
- Baseline (total N=7352): mean eGFR 67.8 mL/min/1.73 m2 (61.7% >=60); median uACR 308 (IQR 108-740); 50.7% uACR >=300, 46.4% uACR 30-<300; mean SBP 135.8 mmHg; HbA1c 7.7%; prior CV disease 45.3%; SGLT2i use 8.4%, GLP-1 RA 7.5% at baseline.

### N
- Randomized 7437; analyzed 7352 (finerenone 3686, placebo 3666). Median follow-up 3.4 years.

### Primary outcome
- Definition: composite of CV death, nonfatal MI, nonfatal stroke, or hospitalization for heart failure.
- Result: 458/3686 (12.4%) vs 519/3666 (14.2%); HR 0.87 (95% CI 0.76-0.98); P=0.03. NNT 47 over 3.5 yr.
- Benefit driven primarily by HF hospitalization: 117 (3.2%) vs 163 (4.4%); HR 0.71 (95% CI 0.56-0.90).
- Other components: CV death HR 0.90 (0.74-1.09); nonfatal MI 0.99 (0.76-1.31); nonfatal stroke 0.97 (0.74-1.26).

### Key (first) secondary outcome
- Definition: composite of kidney failure, sustained eGFR decrease >=40% from baseline, or death from renal causes.
- Result: 350 (9.5%) vs 395 (10.8%); HR 0.87 (95% CI 0.76-1.01) — NOT statistically significant (so subsequent outcomes exploratory).

### Other notable outcomes
- ESKD: 32 (0.9%) vs 49 (1.3%); HR 0.64 (95% CI 0.41-0.995).
- Kidney composite with >=57% eGFR decrease: 108 (2.9%) vs 139 (3.8%); HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.60-0.99).
- All-cause death: 333 (9.0%) vs 370 (10.1%); HR 0.89 (0.77-1.04), NS.
- uACR reduction baseline to month 4: 32% greater with finerenone (ratio of LS-mean change 0.68; 95% CI 0.65-0.70).

### eGFR-slope data
- Not reported in the main text (no chronic eGFR slope value given).

### Hyperkalemia & K-related discontinuation
- Hyperkalemia (investigator-reported): 10.8% finerenone vs 5.3% placebo.
- Hyperkalemia-related permanent discontinuation: 1.2% vs 0.4%.
- Hyperkalemia hospitalization: 0.6% vs 0.1%. No hyperkalemia deaths.
- Lower hyperkalemia rate than FIDELIO, attributed to higher mean eGFR (68 vs 44).
- Hypokalemia: 1.1% vs 2.4%. Gynecomastia rare (0.1% vs 0.1%).
- Serum K+ between-group difference 0.16 mmol/L from month 1, stable thereafter.

### SBP effect
- Modest. Finerenone-minus-placebo difference in SBP change from baseline: -3.5 mmHg at month 4; -2.6 mmHg at month 24.

## FIDELITY pooled analysis (Agarwal 2022, Eur Heart J)
- NOT PRESENT in either source text. The string "FIDELITY" does not appear; the only "fidelity" matches refer to "fidelity of the trial to the protocol." Both papers (2020 and 2021) predate the FIDELITY pooled analysis, so its combined population, design, and headline cardiorenal HRs are NOT reported in these documents. (FIDELIO-DKD published Oct 2020; FIGARO-DKD published Aug 2021; the FIGARO discussion notes the two trials are "complementary" and together span stage 1-4 CKD, but does not cite or summarize a pooled FIDELITY analysis.)
